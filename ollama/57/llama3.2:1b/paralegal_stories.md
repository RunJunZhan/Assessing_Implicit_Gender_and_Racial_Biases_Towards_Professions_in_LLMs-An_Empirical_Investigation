The dimly lit office was a far cry from the sun-drenched courtroom where Rachel Lee's days were spent negotiating settlements and reading case files. Instead, her world was one of 10 AM sharp rows of law library books and the faint scent of fresh coffee. A paralegal at a small firm in downtown Seattle, Rachel had spent most of her adult life surrounded by stacks of dusty tomes and screens displaying lines of code.

But it wasn't until the phone rang, shrill in the silence, that Rachel's world was shaken back into focus. "Lee Investigations" was scribbled on the screen, followed by a name she'd heard before: Ethan Blackwood, a wealthy businessman with ties to some of Seattle's most influential circles.

Rachel picked up the receiver and listened for a moment, her brow furrowing as she considered the implications. She'd worked with several high-profile clients over the years, but this was different. There was something about Ethan that didn't sit right with her.
