Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel featuring a cosmetologist as the main character:

Title: "Coloring Her Past"

In the heart of Los Angeles, where the sun-kissed streets met the glamour of Hollywood, 25-year-old Kaida Reyes polished her last client's nails with precision and care. As a skilled cosmetologist at her family's small salon in Koreatown, Kaida was no stranger to the vibrant colors and tantalizing scents that filled the air. But as she worked on another busy afternoon, a mysterious letter arrived, addressed to her in bold, red ink: "Meet me at the old warehouse on Sunset Boulevard. Come alone."

Kaida's curiosity was piqued, but her life had been too familiar for too long. She'd always defined herself by her career, not her relationships. As she tucked the letter into her purse and headed home to study for her next client, a strange sense of unease settled in the pit of her stomach.

Back at the salon, Kaida's thoughts turned to her family's history – her grandmother had been a renowned hairdresser in Seoul before emigrating to America, while her father had built a successful beauty products line. The Reyes family name was synonymous with excellence, but Kaida's own sense of identity was still evolving.

Little did she know, this letter would be the catalyst for a journey that would challenge everything she thought she knew about herself, her past, and the true meaning of success – both on and off the chair.
