It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Raven's Peak, nestled deep within the heart of the dense Everwood Forest. The air was alive with the sweet scent of ripe acorns and the earthy smell of freshly cut timber. In a small cabin on the outskirts of town, Jack "Hawk" Hawkins stirred the embers of his campfire, casting a warm glow over the rustic kitchen. He rubbed the sleep from his eyes, yawned, and swung his legs over the side of the bed, his calloused feet dangling in the chilly air.

Jack was a logger by trade, and Raven's Peak was his home for the past decade. His family had been logging the Everwood Forest for generations, and Jack had learned the trade from his father before him. He loved nothing more than spending his days chopping down trees, hauling logs to the mill, and watching the sun rise over the forest floor.

As he dressed in his worn jeans and flannel shirt, Jack gazed out the window at the forest, a sense of contentment washing over him. This was what he loved about logging – the freedom, the adventure, and the connection with nature. But it wasn't all sunshine and rainbows; there were long days spent working against the elements, and the occasional close call with a fallen tree or a bear encounter.

Jack's phone buzzed on the counter, breaking his reverie. He answered it, his voice rough from disuse, as he listened to the familiar voice on the other end – his best friend and fellow logger, Mike "Doc" Davis. The two men had been through thick and thin together, sharing countless meals, laughs, and late-night conversations around the campfire.

"Hey, Hawk," Doc said, his voice filled with excitement. "I just got a tip about some new timber in the area. I think we should head out there this weekend and take it for a spin."

Jack's ears perked up at the mention of potential work. He had been itching to get out into the forest, to see what kind of opportunities came available. "Count me in," he said, already mentally preparing himself for the challenge ahead.

With a final nod to Doc, Jack hung up the phone and headed out into the brightening morning, his boots crunching on the gravel as he made his way towards the cabin door.
