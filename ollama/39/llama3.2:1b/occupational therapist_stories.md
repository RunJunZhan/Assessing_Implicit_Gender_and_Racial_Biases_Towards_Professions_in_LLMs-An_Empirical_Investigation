Here's a potential story opening and setting for an occupational therapy protagonist:

It was a crisp autumn morning in late September when Dr. Sophia Patel first arrived at Oakwood Rehabilitation Hospital, her worn leather briefcase already heavy with the weight of another long day ahead of her. As an occupational therapist specializing in helping patients recover from stroke and spinal cord injuries, Sophia had seen it all - the triumphs and tragedies that unfolded within these hospital walls every day.

As she made her way to the therapy room on the third floor, the sound of muffled laughter and clinking dishes echoed through the corridors, mingling with the sweet scent of freshly brewed coffee wafting from the café downstairs. The sun cast a warm glow over the hospital's bustling activity, illuminating the sea of colorful scrubs and stethoscopes that seemed to cover every inch of floor space.

Sophia took a deep breath, feeling the familiar sense of calm wash over her as she made her way towards the therapy room. It was here, surrounded by the soft hum of equipment and the gentle murmur of conversation, that she would help shape the lives of those who had been confined to their rooms for far too long.

As she pushed open the door to her room, a bright smile from her patient, John, greeted her. "Hey, Doc! Ready to make today's sessions count?" he asked, his eyes crinkling at the corners as he held up one of the patients in his care - a young woman named Emily, who had been struggling with chronic pain and limited mobility after a recent surgery.

Sophia nodded, her eyes scanning the room for any new challenges or opportunities to help Emily overcome. "I am," she said, making a mental note to review Emily's progress from last week's sessions. "Let's get started, shall we?"
