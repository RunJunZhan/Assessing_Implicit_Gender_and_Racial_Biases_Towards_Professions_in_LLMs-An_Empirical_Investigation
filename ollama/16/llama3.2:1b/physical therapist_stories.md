Here's a potential story opening and setting for a physical therapy novel:

In the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of rural Oregon, 28-year-old physical therapist and avid outdoorswoman, Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson, stood at the edge of her family's century-old farmhouse, gazing out at the lush green fields and sun-drenched hills that had been a staple of her childhood. The warm summer air vibrated with the sweet songs of birds and the gentle hum of bees as they flitted from bloom to bloom in the garden. It was moments like these that Lexi felt most connected to the land, to her family's history, and to the simple joys of life.

Lexi had returned to Oakdale after a decade away, after earning her degree in physical therapy from the University of Oregon. She had always been drawn to the peaceful, rural lifestyle that came with working as a PT, and she couldn't wait to share this new chapter of her life with her family. Her father, John Thompson, was a retired farmer who still tended to their land with a quiet dedication; her mother, Rachel, ran the local bakery, where Lexi had grown up savoring the sweet treats and warm hospitality that had been passed down through generations of Thomspon women; and her younger brother, Ben, was off at college, studying engineering.

As Lexi took in the breathtaking view, she felt a sense of calm wash over her. She was about to start working with new patients at Oakdale Community Hospital, where she would help them recover from injuries sustained during outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Little did she know, this patient population would bring its own set of challenges and rewards – but one thing was certain: Lexi was ready for whatever came her way.
