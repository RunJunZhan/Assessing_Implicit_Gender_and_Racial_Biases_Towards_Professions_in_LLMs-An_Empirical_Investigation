It was a brisk autumn morning in the bustling city of New Haven, where the golden hues of the sunrise cast a warm glow over the towering skyscrapers. The air was alive with the hum of traffic, the wail of sirens, and the chatter of pedestrians rushing to start their day. Amidst the chaos, 35-year-old Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stood at the edge of her office building, gazing out at the city she had called home for nearly two decades.

As a high-powered executive in the finance industry, Lexi was accustomed to the fast-paced and demanding nature of her job. But on this particular morning, she felt a sense of unease wash over her. It started with small things – the coffee machine had been acting up overnight, and she'd received an unexpected meeting invite from a rival company that morning. The prospect of rubbing shoulders with their top executives made her stomach twist into knots.

Lexi took a deep breath and stepped back into the safety of her office, closing the door behind her. She slipped on her worn leather jacket and pulled up her sleeves, feeling the familiar comfort of the material against her skin. As she settled into her high-backed chair, her assistant, Emma, knocked softly on the doorframe.

"Morning, Lexi," Emma said with a bright smile. "All set for today?"

Lexi nodded curtly, not bothering to hide the tension emanating from her. She was used to being in control, and it wasn't just about the job – it was about who she wanted to spend time with, too.
