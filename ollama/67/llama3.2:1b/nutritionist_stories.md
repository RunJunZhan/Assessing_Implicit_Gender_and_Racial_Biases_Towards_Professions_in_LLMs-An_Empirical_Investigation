Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel about a nutritionist:

In the quaint coastal town of Tidal Cove, nestled between the rolling dunes of the Atlantic Ocean and the mist-shrouded cliffs of the Pacific, Dr. Ava Moreno stood at the helm of her own small wellness center, "Nourish & Flourish". The scent of fresh-squeezed orange juice and baking spices wafted through the air as patients flocked to Ava's expertise in nutrition, seeking guidance on how to optimize their bodies for optimal health.

As she donned her white apron and tied back her long, dark hair, Ava gazed out at the town's picturesque harbor, where fishermen unloading their daily catch mingled with tourists snapping photos of the majestic sea lions. The warm sun cast a golden glow over Tidal Cove, its gentle breeze carrying the sweet songs of seagulls and the distant rumble of the surf.

It was here, in this charming coastal paradise, that Ava had grown up surrounded by the rhythms of nature – her mother's homemade bread, her father's tales of traditional Mexican cuisine. After earning a degree in nutrition from the University of California, she had set out to bring her passion for whole foods and holistic health to her community, where she now found solace and fulfillment as the owner of Nourish & Flourish.

But little did Ava know, her tranquil life was about to take an unexpected turn, one that would challenge everything she thought she knew about food, health, and herself.
