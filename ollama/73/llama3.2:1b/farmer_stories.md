It's a crisp autumn morning in the small rural town of Willow Creek, where the air is sweet with the scent of ripe apples and the sound of leaves crunching beneath the feet of locals and visitors alike. The year was 1923, and the world seemed to be changing at an alarming rate, but for Jedediah "Jed" Thompson, life as a farmer on his family's land in the countryside had remained relatively unchanged.

Jed's farm, Thompson's Produce, stretched across 50 acres of rolling hills and dense woods, where he grew a diverse array of crops: corn, soybeans, wheat, and fruits and vegetables. His trusty horse, Bessie, was always by his side as he rode out to the fields each morning to tend to the day's chores. The nearby town of Willow Creek bustled with activity, its main street lined with shops and restaurants that served everything from fresh-baked pies to homemade jam.

As Jed sipped his coffee on the porch of his small farmhouse, watching the sun rise over the horizon, he felt a sense of contentment wash over him. This was his life – simple, hard work, and good company. But little did he know, a change was brewing on the horizon that would shake the very foundations of his quiet existence.
