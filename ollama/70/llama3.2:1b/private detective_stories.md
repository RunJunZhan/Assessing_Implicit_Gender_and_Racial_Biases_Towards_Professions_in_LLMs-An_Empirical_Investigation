It was a drizzly autumn evening in the city of Ashwood, where the mist-shrouded skyscrapers seemed to lean in on each other like sentinels. The rain dripped from the eaves of the apartment building that housed private detective Ethan Blackwood, a man with a reputation for being as elusive as he was brilliant. His office, a cramped but well-appointed space above the local diner, was already filling with the warm glow of evening sunlight as Blackwood settled in for another long night of work.

Ethan's office, nestled between a vintage bookstore and a used car dealership, was the kind of place that seemed to exist outside of time. The walls were adorned with faded photographs and dusty trophies from cases past, while the air was thick with the scent of stale coffee and worn leather. A small desk in the center of the room held the stack of files and notes that had accumulated over the years, a testament to Blackwood's ability to dig up secrets and uncover lies.

As he sipped his lukewarm coffee, Blackwood's gaze drifted out the window to the rain-soaked streets below. It was a night like any other in Ashwood, where the city's residents were too caught up in their own lives to notice the small, hidden corners that Ethan knew every inch of. But tonight felt different. Tonight, there was a sense of anticipation in the air, as if something was about to change, and Blackwood was ready.
