It was a crisp autumn morning on the vast, open range of the Pine Flat Ranch, where cattle grazed lazily under the golden light of dawn. The air was alive with the sweet scent of wet earth and the gentle lowing of livestock as they stirred from their slumber. Among them was Kameron "Kae" Thompson, a rugged and weathered rancher in his late 30s, who had spent his entire life working the land. His worn cowboy hat and dusty Stetson hat tipped slightly to one side, giving him a perpetually tousled look, as if he'd just rolled out of bed and stumbled onto the range.

Kae stood at the edge of the ranch's small corral, his calloused hands gripping the wooden fence posts as he gazed out at the seemingly endless expanse of rolling hills and scrubby bushes. He took a deep breath, feeling the salty air fill his lungs, and began to walk towards the ranch house, a sturdy clapboard building that had been in his family for generations. As he approached, the sound of clinking steel and the murmur of hushed conversations drifted out into the morning air, mingling with the distant lowing of cattle.

Kae pushed open the creaky door and stepped inside, his eyes adjusting to the warm light within. The ranch house was a cozy haven, filled with the familiar scent of old leather and worn wool, and the soft glow of candlelight cast a comforting warmth over the space. He dropped his hat on the nearest stool and made his way towards the kitchen, where his wife, Rachel, stood busily stirring a pot of stew on the stovetop, her curly brown hair tied back in a loose ponytail as she worked.

"Morning, hon," Kae said, tipping his hat back on his head as he took a seat at the table. "What's cookin'?"
