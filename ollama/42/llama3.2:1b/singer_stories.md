It was a crisp autumn evening in the small coastal town of Ravenswood, where the misty veil of dawn slowly lifted to reveal the sleepy streets lined with centuries-old oak trees. The air was alive with the sweet scent of ripe seafood and the distant sound of seagulls crying out over the waves.

Aurélia "Rory" Thompson stepped off the worn wooden dock and onto the weathered stones that led up to the small beachside café where she'd agreed to meet her record label executive, Julian Styles. The late sun cast a warm glow on Rory's features as she adjusted her guitar strap over her shoulder, the instrument slung low against her hip.

Rory was a singer-songwriter with a voice that could melt the coldest of hearts and a personality that could light up even the darkest of rooms. Her debut album, "Whispers in the Wind," had been met with critical acclaim and commercial success, but Rory's heart still belonged to the sea and her music.

As she pushed open the door to the café, Julian looked up from behind the bar, his piercing blue eyes locking onto hers with a warmth that made her feel at ease. "Rory! Fancy running into you here."
