It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Ravenswood, nestled deep within the heart of the Pacific Northwest forest. The air is alive with the sweet scent of damp earth and the distant chirping of birds as the sun rises over the towering trees. In a small cabin on the outskirts of town, Jack "Hawk" Hawkins wakes up to the sound of his alarm clock, a rusty old telephone that's been passed down through generations of his family.

Jack is a logger by trade, with calloused hands and a rugged exterior that hides a sharp mind and a quick wit. He's spent countless hours in these very woods, learning the ins and outs of logging from his father and grandfather before him. As he rolls out of bed and begins to get ready for another long day of work, the sounds of the forest grow louder - the rustle of leaves, the snap of twigs, and the distant rumble of a train that will soon carry Jack out into the woods.

With his backpack slung over his shoulder and a worn denim jacket draped over his shoulder, Hawk heads towards the local diner, where he'll meet up with his buddy, Rusty "The Bull" Thompson. Over a hearty breakfast of pancakes and eggs, they discuss the latest news from the mill - some good, some bad. But for now, as Jack finishes getting dressed and prepares to head out into the woods, the only thing that matters is getting to work before the day gets too hot.
