Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel featuring a dietitian protagonist:

---

The sun was setting over the bustling streets of New Haven, casting a warm orange glow over the historic city. In a small apartment above her family's bakery, Emma Taylor stirred a steaming bowl of oatmeal, the familiar comfort of the creamy porridge a balm for her frazzled nerves.

As the owner and head chef of Taylor's Treats, a popular bakery specializing in healthy baked goods, Emma had always been driven to create delicious meals that nourished both body and soul. But as she worked to meet the demanding expectations of her family's business, Emma found herself struggling to balance her passion for nutrition with the pressure to produce profit.

Emma's own health had suffered as a result – she had grown up eating an unhealthy diet of processed snacks and sugary treats, which had led to a range of chronic issues. Now, at 30 years old, she was determined to make a change. She spent every spare moment researching the latest research on nutrition and wellness, devouring books and articles on everything from intermittent fasting to probiotics.

As the doorbell above her apartment door jangled, signaling the arrival of a new customer – a young professional named Michael Chen, who had stumbled upon Taylor's Treats while searching for healthy alternatives to his company's junk food options – Emma felt a surge of excitement. This was the opportunity she had been waiting for: the chance to prove herself as a true expert in her field and build a career that would bring her joy and fulfillment.

But as Michael ordered a selection of nutrient-dense desserts, Emma couldn't shake the feeling that she was playing with fire. Her clients, it seemed, were more interested in quick fixes than long-term solutions – and she had a nagging sense that she might be contributing to their problems rather than helping them solve them.
