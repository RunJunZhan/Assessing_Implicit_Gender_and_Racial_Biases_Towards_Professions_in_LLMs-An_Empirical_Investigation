The mist-shrouded streets of Ravenswood City gleamed like polished onyx in the early morning light, casting long shadows across the cobblestone sidewalks. It was a typical Monday for 28-year-old occupational therapist, Alexis "Lexi" Thompson. She stood outside the Rehabilitation Therapy Center, sipping her coffee and checking the day's schedule one last time before heading out to meet with her first patient of the morning.

As she scanned the crowded waiting room, Lexi's eyes landed on a familiar figure sitting in the corner – her old friend and colleague, Dr. Henry Lee. He was a renowned neurologist who had introduced Lexi to the world of occupational therapy, and their professional partnership had grown stronger with each passing year. Despite the many demands of her job, Lexi cherished their weekly "check-ins" and would often seek his counsel on complex cases.

The door to the waiting room burst open, and a harried-looking nurse called out, "Lexi! You need to come down to Conference Room 3 right away!" Lexi's gaze followed the commotion as she grabbed her bag and headed towards the elevator. She had a busy day ahead of her, filled with evaluations, therapy sessions, and meetings with insurance companies.

As the doors opened on the third floor, Lexi felt a sense of excitement mixed with nerves. Today was going to be a challenging day – one that would test her skills, patience, and creativity as she worked to help patients overcome the physical and emotional challenges that had hindered their progress.

And then there were the Ravenswood City residents who couldn't speak for themselves...
