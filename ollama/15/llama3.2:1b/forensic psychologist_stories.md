Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel about a forensic psychologist:

In the sleepy town of Ravenswood, nestled in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains, Dr. Emilia "Mia" Grant stood at the threshold of her office, gazing out at the mist-shrouded valley below. The rain had stopped hours ago, leaving behind a peaceful stillness that was tempered only by the faint hum of the town's old mill on the outskirts. It was a place where time seemed to stand still, where the past and present converged in ways both tangible and intangible.

Mia's office, with its faded wooden paneling and walls lined with framed photographs of her family, felt like a nexus point between the worlds that existed just beyond the town's sleepy façade. As a forensic psychologist, she had spent years honing her skills in the human mind, but this quiet corner of Ravenswood was where she began her days, a place to grapple with the complexities and contradictions of the human psyche.

As she stepped inside, a faint scent of lavender and old books enveloped her, transporting her back to summers spent exploring these very woods as a child. It was here, amidst the ancient trees and whispering streams, that Mia had discovered her passion for the human mind, her fascination with the dark corners and hidden recesses of the human heart.

But Mia's own life wasn't without its secrets – not yet, at least. She had just received an invitation to meet a patient who would change everything she thought she knew about the darkness that lurked within Ravenswood itself...
