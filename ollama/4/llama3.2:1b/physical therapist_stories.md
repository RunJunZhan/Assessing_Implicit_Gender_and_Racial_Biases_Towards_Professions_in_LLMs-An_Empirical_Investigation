Here's a potential story opening and setting for a physical therapy thriller:

---

**Story Name:** "The Rebound"

**Main Character:**

Dr. Sophia Patel, a 35-year-old renowned physical therapist with a no-nonsense attitude and a sharp mind.

**Setting:**

The small town of Ravenswood, nestled in the Pacific Northwest, where the air is crisp and clean, and the only thing more abundant than the trees are the secrets people keep hidden. Ravenswood has a population of just over 10,000 residents, with a strong sense of community and an even stronger sense of gossip.

The story takes place in late summer, when the sun shines brightly and the town is bustling with activity. The local hospital, Mercy General, is at the center of it all, its walls bearing witness to the lives and struggles of the townspeople. Sophia's physical therapy clinic, "Rebound Rehabilitation," is one of the most popular in town, where she helps people recover from injuries and illnesses that leave them feeling defeated.

As Sophia prepares for another busy day at Mercy General, she receives an unexpected phone call from her former patient, a reclusive millionaire named Marcus Blackwood. His voice is laced with a mix of frustration and desperation, and when he asks to meet at the old warehouse on the outskirts of town, Sophia can't help but feel a sense of unease.

"What's going on, Marcus?" Sophia asks, trying to keep her tone neutral despite her growing curiosity. "I've been thinking about our last session. You said you needed some...rehabilitation."

There's a pause on the other end of the line before Marcus responds, his voice dripping with an air of urgency. "Sophia, I need your help. Please come to Ravenswood. Now."
