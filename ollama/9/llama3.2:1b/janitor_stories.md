It was a chilly autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the streets were lined with old oak trees and the houses seemed to lean in, as if sharing secrets. The sun was just starting to peek over the horizon, casting a warm glow over the quiet streets. In a small, unassuming building on Elm Street, a lone janitor named Emrys Wellesley stirred awake from his sleep. His worn leather gloves were covered in dirt and grime, and his eyes were bloodshot from lack of sleep.

Emrys lived in a small apartment above the janitorial services business he operated, where he took care of the town's dusty floors and cramped corridors. It was a job that required no special training or qualifications, but it paid the bills for the quiet life he led. As he made his way down to the kitchen to start his day with a cup of coffee, the sounds of the town began to stir outside – birds chirping, dogs barking, and the distant rumble of a lawnmower.

The Wellesley family's old Victorian house, where Emrys lived, was a bit of a mystery to outsiders. It had been in his family for generations, but its secrets remained locked away behind closed doors. The front door creaked as Emrys opened it and stepped out onto the porch, taking a deep breath of the crisp autumn air. He smiled to himself, feeling a sense of comfort in this familiar routine – it was a life he had grown accustomed to, one that was neither glamorous nor exciting, but it was home.

As he began his rounds, Emrys couldn't shake off the feeling that something was off. The streets seemed busier than usual, and he noticed a few residents who looked like they hadn't been out in days. He made a mental note to speak with them later that day – there was always more to uncover in this quiet town. With his coffee in hand and the morning sun on his face, Emrys set off into the crisp autumn air, ready to face whatever lay ahead.
