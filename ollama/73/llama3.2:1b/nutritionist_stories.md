It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the rolling hills of California's Central Valley. The sun rose over the vineyards, casting a warm golden light over the rows of grapevines that stretched as far as the eye could see. In a cozy little office nestled between the bustling Main Street and the tranquil creek that ran through the center of town, nutritionist Emma Taylor prepared for another busy day at her own practice, "Harvest Health."

Emma, with her curly brown hair and bright green eyes, had always been passionate about helping people make informed choices about their diets. As a certified nutritionist, she spent her days cooking up delicious, whole-food-based meals for her clients, from wholesome omelets to nutritious smoothies. Her office was a haven of calm in the midst of the bustling town, filled with the scent of fresh fruits and vegetables, and the soft hum of gentle chatter.

On this particular morning, Emma was reviewing her schedule for the day, mentally preparing herself for the influx of new clients who would be arriving at her office later that afternoon. She had a feeling it was going to be another busy day, what with the harvest festival just around the corner and all the last-minute requests from friends and family clamoring for her help.

As she sipped her coffee and checked her phone for any urgent messages, Emma's gaze fell upon a small piece of paper stuck between the pages of her planner. It was a note, scribbled in haste, addressed to her: "Meet me at the old oak at 10 am. Come alone." The words sent a shiver down Emma's spine; she knew that tone all too well, and her curiosity was piqued. What could be going on?
