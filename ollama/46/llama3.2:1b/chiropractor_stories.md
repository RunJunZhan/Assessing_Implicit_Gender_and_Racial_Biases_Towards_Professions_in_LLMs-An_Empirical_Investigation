In the sleepy coastal town of Bayshore, where the salty air mingled with the scent of saltwater taffy and the sound of seagulls filled the sky, Dr. Ethan Jameson wiped the sweat from his forehead as he adjusted the final position of the patient's spine. It had been a typical summer morning in this charming beachside town, with the sun rising over the ocean like a brilliant orange ball and the sound of children laughing and playing on the beach.

Ethan was a chiropractor by trade, having grown up in Bayshore and learned his craft from his father, who had opened the local chiropractic clinic decades ago. Now, at 35, Ethan had established his own practice, "EJ Chiropractic," where he spent his days helping patients like Mrs. Jenkins, who was struggling with chronic back pain, and Mr. Thompson, a young athlete looking to optimize his performance.

As he packed up the last of his equipment and prepared to leave for the day, Ethan's phone rang, breaking the peaceful atmosphere. He answered, expecting it to be another patient or perhaps a billing inquiry, but instead found himself talking to an unfamiliar voice on the other end.

"Dr. Jameson? It's Rachel from the hospital. I need you to see Emily Wilson immediately. She's in a critical condition and we're running out of time."

Ethan's heart skipped a beat as he listened to Rachel's concerns. "I'm on my way, Rach. What's going on?"
