The sun was just starting to peek over the rooftops of Seattle as Jack McKenzie stepped out of his small workshop and into the crisp morning air. He stretched his weathered frame, feeling the familiar creaks in his back from years of working on these same steep roofs. It was going to be a big day – a high-rise apartment building needed its first repair since the winter storms, and Jack had been chosen for the job.

As he began to change into his uniform, Jack's mind wandered to his family, who were all busy with their own projects around town. His wife, Sarah, was a seamstress; his kids, Emily and James, were off at summer camp; and his parents, both retired teachers, were keeping house in the small town of Oakdale. Jack felt a pang of gratitude for this quiet life he'd built for himself on the outskirts of Seattle.

He finished getting dressed and headed out to meet his crew, led by the gruff but fair-minded Joe "The Bull" Morano. As they climbed into their truck, Jack couldn't help but think about the long day ahead – and the potential risks it would bring. Roofering was a tough business, but Jack had spent years honing his skills, and he knew that with caution and attention to detail, he could get the job done safely.

The conversation on the drive to the construction site was hushed, except for the occasional shout from one of the other workers or the distant rumble of thunder on the horizon. Jack's mind began to wander back to his childhood, growing up in a small town where everyone knew each other and life moved at a slower pace. It was a feeling he'd long since lost as he'd gotten older and taken on more responsibilities – but he held onto it tight, whenever possible.

As they pulled into the job site, Jack caught sight of the towering apartment building, its facade gleaming in the morning light. He felt a thrill of excitement mixed with a hint of trepidation – today was going to be a long day.
