It was a crisp autumn morning in New York City, the kind that made you want to wrap up in a warm coat and sipping a hot coffee as you stepped out of your high-rise office building on Wall Street. The sun was slowly rising over the Manhattan skyline, casting a golden glow over the bustling streets below.

Ava Morales adjusted her glasses for what felt like the hundredth time, making sure they were securely in place on her nose. As a top stock broker at Morgan Stanley, she had seen it all - the ups and downs of the market, the thrill of closing a lucrative deal, and the agony of watching a client's portfolio tank.

Ava lived in a sleek, modern apartment on the Upper East Side, with a stunning view of Central Park that seemed to match her own personality: sharp, polished, and always impeccably dressed. She was 32 years old, with long, dark hair and piercing green eyes that sparkled with intelligence and wit.

As she made her way to the kitchen to start her day, Ava's phone rang, shrill in the stillness of the morning. She answered it, her voice husky from years of talking on the phone.

"Good morning, Ava," a smooth voice said on the other end. "I hope I'm not interrupting anything important."

Ava raised an eyebrow. "Who is this?" she asked, her tone neutral.

"My name is Ethan Grant," the man replied. "I'm a client of yours from...a certain financial institution in London."

Ava's eyes narrowed. "And what do you want, Mr. Grant?"

Ethan smiled, his voice dripping with charm. "Let's just say I have a few questions for you about my investments. Shall we schedule a meeting?"
