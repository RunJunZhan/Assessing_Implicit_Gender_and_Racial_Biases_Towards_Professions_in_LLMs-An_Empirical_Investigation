It was a crisp autumn morning in the quaint town of Willow Creek, where the streets were lined with old oak trees and the air was sweet with the scent of ripe apples. In a cozy little bungalow on Elm Street, 32-year-old Sophia Patel stirred in her bedroom, rubbing the sleep from her eyes. She had worked as a housekeeper for the wealthy Smith family for five years, taking care of their three children and four living rooms. Despite the demands of her job, Sophia loved nothing more than helping families like the Smiths create memories that would last a lifetime.

As she swung her legs over the side of the bed, Sophia's eyes fell on the framed photo of her family on the nightstand. Her husband, Rohan, and their two children, Aisha and Kumar, were beaming with happiness in the picture. But today was different. Today, Sophia had a feeling that something was off.
