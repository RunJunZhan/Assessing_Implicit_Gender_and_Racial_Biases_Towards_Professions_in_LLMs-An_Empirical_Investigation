It was a crisp autumn evening in the heart of Manhattan's Upper East Side, the kind of night that made you want to wrap up in a warm blanket and retreat to a quiet café on 5th Avenue. But for Emily Rose, a concierge at the posh Plaza Hotel, it was just another busy evening at work.

As she stepped out of the revolving door and onto the bustling lobby floor, the sounds of hushed conversations and clinking glasses filled her ears, a cacophony that seemed to match the rhythm of her own footsteps. She had worked at the Plaza for five years, and yet every night still felt like a new adventure.

Emily's desk was a flurry of activity as she fielded calls from guests checking in, answered emails from the hotel staff, and coordinated last-minute events for the evening's guests. Her bright yellow sundress and matching hair tie made her stand out amidst the sea of suits and ties, but she didn't let it faze her – after all, a concierge had to be able to handle anything that came her way.

As she worked the clock, Emily's mind began to wander back to her life before the Plaza. Born into a family of travelers, she had grown up sleeping on buses and trains, always ready for the next adventure. But it wasn't until she'd landed her job as a concierge that she discovered a sense of purpose – helping others find their way in this vast, mysterious city was like solving a puzzle, and Emily loved every minute of it.

Just then, the phone rang, shrill in the silence. "Hello?" Emily answered, expecting it to be another guest checking in or perhaps a colleague needing help with something. But instead, she heard a voice on the other end that made her heart skip a beat – a smooth, low-pitched voice that sent shivers down her spine.

"Miss Rose? It's Dr. Lee from the New York Institute of Anesthesia. We need your assistance."
