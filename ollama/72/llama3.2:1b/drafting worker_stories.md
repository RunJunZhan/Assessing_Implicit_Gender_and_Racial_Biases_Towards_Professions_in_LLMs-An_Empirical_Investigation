It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, where the sun rises over the rolling hills and quaint streets lined with ancient oak trees. The year is 1955, and the local high school, Oakdale High, is bustling with students preparing for another day of classes.

Among the throngs of students rushing to their lockers and grab a quick breakfast before the bell rings, one name stands out: Elliot Everett. A junior at Oakdale High, Elliot spends most of his free time working part-time as a drafting worker at Smith & Co., a local architectural firm. With a keen eye for detail and an aptitude for mathematics, Elliot has found solace in the precision work that comes with drafting.

As he steps out of the school building, the cool morning air carries the sweet scent of freshly cut grass and the distant hum of lawn mowers, a familiar soundtrack to his daily routine. The streets are quiet, save for the occasional bark from a nearby dog or the chatter of passersby, all eager to get to their first period classes.

Elliot's small apartment, located in a cozy two-story building near the high school, is filled with the smell of coffee and the sound of his favorite radio show, "The Mercury Hour," playing softly in the background. His roommate, a lanky guy named Gus, is already up and getting ready for the day, heading off to the kitchen to whip up some eggs and bacon.

As Elliot approaches the building, he can hear the familiar chatter of his coworkers gathered near the entrance, discussing the latest news from the drafting department. The sign on the door reads "Smith & Co." in bold letters, a testament to their reputation for delivering quality designs and structures that meet the highest standards.

With a nod to Gus, Elliot heads up the stairs to join his colleagues at Smith & Co., ready to tackle another day of precision work and creative problem-solving. Little does he know, today will bring a new challenge and an opportunity to showcase his skills as a draftsman.
