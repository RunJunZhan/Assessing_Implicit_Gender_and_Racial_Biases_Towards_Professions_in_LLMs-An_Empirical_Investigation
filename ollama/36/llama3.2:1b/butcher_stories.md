It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of the countryside. The sun was just starting to rise over the rooftops, casting a warm glow over the quaint Main Street. In a small shop tucked away on Elm Street, the sign above the door read "McLaughlin's Deli and Butchery". Inside, the aroma of freshly ground beef and baking bread wafted through the air, enticing the townspeople to start their day with a hearty breakfast.

At the counter, Jack McLaughlin polished a knife with a dirty rag, his calloused hands moving with practiced ease. He was a man in his late 40s, with a thick head of gray hair and a bushy beard that he loved to stubble. Jack had lived in Willow Creek all his life, working at McLaughlin's Deli for over 20 years. His wife, Margaret, was the one who kept the shop running smoothly, but Jack took credit for it all. After all, someone had to be the face of the business – and that someone was him.

As the morning rush began to pick up, Jack's phone rang, interrupting his thoughts. He answered, his deep voice warm as he greeted his son, Alex, who worked part-time at the deli. "Hey, kiddo! What's the special today?"
