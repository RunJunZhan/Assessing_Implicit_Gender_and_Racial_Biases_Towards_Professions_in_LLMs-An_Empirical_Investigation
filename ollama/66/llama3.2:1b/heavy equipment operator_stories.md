It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Red Rock, nestled deep within the Arizona desert. The sun was just starting to rise over the horizon, casting a warm orange glow over the dusty streets and crumbling adobe buildings. The air was alive with the sweet scent of juniper and the distant howl of coyotes.

Avery "Ace" Jensen, a 35-year-old heavy equipment operator, stepped out of his rusty old truck and onto the cracked asphalt of Main Street. He stretched his tall frame, arching his back and extending his arms above his head as he took in the quiet morning atmosphere. Ace was a man who thrived on routine, and Red Rock had been his home for over five years now.

As he made his way towards the local mechanic's shop, Ace couldn't help but feel a sense of satisfaction wash over him. Today was going to be one of those days where everything went according to plan – no equipment malfunctions, no unexpected breakdowns, and no major accidents. Just another day in paradise for Ace.

The mechanic's shop was run by Ace's old friend and colleague, Jake "Hawk" Hawkins. Hawk had been with Ace since the early days of their heavy equipment operation, and over the years, they'd developed a system that worked like clockwork – or at least, as close to it as possible for this rugged, hard-working pair.
