It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of the Pacific Northwest. The misty veil that shrouded the town seemed to cling to the streets like a damp embrace, casting an eerie glow over the quaint shops and houses. In this charming town, where everyone knew each other's names, 25-year-old programmer, Ethan Everett, sat huddled in his small apartment, staring blankly at his computer screen.

Ethan had always been fascinated by technology, spending countless hours as a child tinkering with gadgets and software. After graduating from the local university with a degree in computer science, he had landed a job at a small tech firm on the outskirts of town, where he worked as a junior software engineer. But as much as he enjoyed his work, Ethan felt unfulfilled, like there was something missing – a sense of purpose that drove him to create something from nothing.

As he worked through the morning rush hour traffic, Ethan's mind began to wander to the strange message on his phone from an unknown number: "Meet me at the old warehouse on 5th and Main at midnight. Come alone." The words sent a shiver down his spine, and he couldn't shake off the feeling that his life was about to take a dramatic turn.
