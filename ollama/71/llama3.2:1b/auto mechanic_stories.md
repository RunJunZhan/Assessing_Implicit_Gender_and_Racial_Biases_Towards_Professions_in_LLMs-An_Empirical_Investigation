It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, where the sun rises over the rolling hills and fields of golden wheat. The air is filled with the sweet scent of ripe pumpkins and the distant hum of the local diner, where the smell of sizzling burgers and fresh coffee wafts through the windows.

In the heart of this charming town, 25-year-old Ryder Thompson owns and operates "Thompson's Auto Repair," a beloved fixture on Main Street. Ryder is a native Oakdale boy, raised by his parents in a cozy two-story house with a big backyard filled with cars, trucks, and motorcycles. He's always been fascinated by the way things work, and after dropping out of high school to take over the family business, he's spent years honing his skills as an auto mechanic.

Ryder's father, Joe Thompson, was a proud mechanic himself, who taught Ryder everything he knows about cars from a young age. After Joe passed away when Ryder was just 17, he felt a void in his life that only car repairs could fill. Now, with the help of his younger sister, Emma, who works part-time at the diner, Ryder runs Thompson's Auto Repair with a sense of purpose and pride.

As the morning light streams into the shop, casting a warm glow over the bustling garage, Ryder takes a moment to survey the day ahead. He's got a long list of customers waiting for their car troubles to be fixed, and he knows that the next few hours will be filled with grease, wrenches, and the hum of engines. Little does he know, today is going to be one of those days that will test his skills and his resolve like never before.
