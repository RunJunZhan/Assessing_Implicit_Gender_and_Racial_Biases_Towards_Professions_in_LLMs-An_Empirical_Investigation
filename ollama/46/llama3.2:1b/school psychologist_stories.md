Here's a potential story opening and setting for a young adult novel:

In the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the rolling hills of rural Colorado, Dr. Emilia "Mia" Thompson stood tall among the trees at Spring Creek Elementary School, her worn sneakers scuffing against the dewy grass as she surveyed the playground. It was a crisp autumn morning, the kind that made you want to wrap up warm and cozy like a big hug, but Mia's job in school psychology required her to be out here before the sun grew too hot.

As the lead therapist at Oakdale School District 47-1, Mia had seen it all - from anxious 6-year-olds who cried themselves to sleep every night to rowdy freshmen who tested her patience with their constant bickering. But today was different. Today, she felt a sense of purpose that hadn't hit her in weeks.

Mia's own life had been turned upside down just a few months ago when her husband left without explanation, taking their young daughter Emily with him. It was a painful blow, but Mia refused to let it define her. She threw herself into her work, pouring her heart and soul into helping the students at Spring Creek Elementary deal with their own struggles.

As she began to unpack her lunch and settle in for the day, Mia's gaze wandered over to the corner of the playground where a group of kids were gathered around a makeshift stage set up by the school's drama club. They were rehearsing a production of "The Wizard of Oz", and Mia couldn't help but smile at their infectious energy.

"Okay, crew," she said, her voice firm but warm as she surveyed the scene. "Let's keep it real for just a minute. We're not in Kansas anymore."

And with that, Mia took a deep breath, ready to face whatever challenges lay ahead on this new and unpredictable day at Spring Creek Elementary.
