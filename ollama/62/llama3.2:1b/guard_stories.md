It was a chilly autumn evening in the small town of Ravenswood, nestled deep within the heart of the Whispering Woods. The trees, adorned with vibrant foliage, cast long shadows across the crumbling stone walls of the local jailhouse, where one man stood watch: Ethan Blackwood. A grizzled and seasoned guard, Ethan had served Ravenswood for nigh on ten years, and in that time, he had seen it all. From petty thieves to escaped convicts, Ethan had a keen eye and a sharp mind for detection.

As the sun dipped below the horizon, casting a warm orange glow over the jailhouse, Ethan stood at the entrance, his eyes scanning the dimly lit interior with a mixture of routine and anticipation. The air was thick with the smell of sweat, stale coffee, and the faint hint of desperation that clung to so many of Ravenswood's residents. It was a smell that Ethan knew all too well, one that made his stomach turn ever so slightly.

Ethan's thoughts, however, were not on the mundane details of his job. His mind was elsewhere, lost in memories of his own past, and the countless nights he had spent sleeping in the cold, damp cellblocks of Ravenswood. A young woman, no more than seventeen at the time, had been his cellmate that fateful night. Her name had been Sophia, and she had vanished without a word, leaving behind only a cryptic note etched into the wall of her cell.

The note read: "I will be waiting for you." Ethan's instincts told him that something was amiss in Ravenswood, and he couldn't shake the feeling that he was being watched. As the darkness deepened, Ethan raised his eyes to the shadows, his hand resting lightly on the butt of his revolver, a habit born from years of training and instinct. Tonight was going to be another long night, one that would test his mettle as a guard, and perhaps, just perhaps, reveal secrets he had kept hidden for far too long.
