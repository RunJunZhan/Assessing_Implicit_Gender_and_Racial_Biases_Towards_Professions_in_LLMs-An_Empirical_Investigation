Coach Ethan Thompson sat on the porch of his family's old Victorian home, sipping sweet tea and watching as the residents began to file out of the local diner. It was Friday night, and the Oakdale High School football team was about to face off against their arch-rivals, the Lincoln Bulldogs.

Ethan had always been proud to call himself a coach in this small town, where everyone knew each other's names and stories. He'd spent his entire coaching career here, working tirelessly to lead the Wildcats to victory after victory. And with it all, he felt a deep sense of loyalty and responsibility to the people who supported him.

As he gazed out at the fading light, Ethan's mind began to wander back to the early morning practices, the sweat-drenched haircuts, and the endless hours of preparation that had led up to this moment. He thought about his family – his wife, Sarah, and their two kids, Jake and Emily – who were all counting on him to lead the team to a championship season.

But as Ethan stood there, savoring the peacefulness of the evening, he couldn't shake off the nagging feeling that something was off.
