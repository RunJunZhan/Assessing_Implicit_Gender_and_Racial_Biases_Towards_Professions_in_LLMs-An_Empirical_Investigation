The sun was just starting to rise over the vast, rolling hills of the ranch where Jesse "Jett" Thompson had lived and breathed for as long as he could remember. The air was crisp and cool, carrying the scent of fresh-cut grass and the distant hint of smoke from the nearby town's early morning barbecue pits. Jett rubbed the grit from his eyes, a worn leather flannel shirt clinging to his broad shoulders as he swung his legs over the side of the worn wooden porch that overlooked the ranch. He took a deep breath, feeling the familiar comfort of the wide open spaces and the sense of responsibility that came with caring for this land.

It was a crisp spring morning in late April, and Jett had been up since before dawn to start his day's chores. The ranch, which he'd inherited from his father a few years back, was a sprawling operation that covered over 5,000 acres of working cattle and grazing land. It was a tough life, but Jett loved it – there was something special about being his own boss, about the independence and freedom that came with owning your own land.

As he made his way out to the barn, Jett's thoughts turned to the upcoming livestock auction at the local town fair. He had a feeling that this year's herd would be particularly profitable, thanks to a new shipment of high-quality cattle from Texas that his brother had brought in just last week. It was a good feeling, knowing that his ranch was going to do well – it was a hard life, but Jett wouldn't trade it for anything.
