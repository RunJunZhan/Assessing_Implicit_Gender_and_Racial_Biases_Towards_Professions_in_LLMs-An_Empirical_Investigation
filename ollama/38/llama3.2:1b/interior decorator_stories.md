Here's a potential story opening and setting for you:

In the heart of Manhattan, where the Chrysler Building pierced the sky like a shard of glass, 25-year-old interior designer Evelyn Rose Wellington was preparing for her most daunting project yet: transforming the opulent penthouse suite at the luxurious Bel Air Towers. The owner, the enigmatic billionaire Henry Langley III, had hired Evelyn to bring his sprawling estate into the modern era, but with one catch: he wanted it done in a single month.

As Evelyn descended into the tower's gleaming lobby, she was greeted by a sea of polished marble and polished steel, the hum of air conditioning vents, and the faint scent of freshly polished wood. The soft glow of string lights cast an ethereal light on the walls, where vintage posters advertising yesteryear's glamour advertised themselves with gaudy flair.

Evelyn took a deep breath, mentally preparing herself for the challenge ahead. She had worked with Langley before, but this project was different – she would have to distill his extravagant tastes into something tasteful and elegant, all within a mere fortnight. Little did she know that her work would not only transform the space but also unravel secrets hidden beneath its gleaming surface.

How's that for a starting point?
