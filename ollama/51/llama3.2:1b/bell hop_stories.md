It was a crisp autumn morning in New Orleans, the sun rising over the Mississippi River as it cast a warm glow over the city's French Quarter. The air was alive with the sweet scent of jasmine and the sound of jazz music drifting from the street performers along Bourbon Street.

Aubrey "Ace" LaRue stood at the entrance to the famous Lafitte Guest House, her bright pink uniform a stark contrast to the seedy underbelly that lurked beneath the city's surface. As a bell hop, Ace had seen it all - the late-night revelers, the early-riser tourists, and the shady characters who called this place home.

Ace's own life was more mundane, but she loved her job. She took pride in keeping the guest house spotless, making sure every room was perfect for its discerning guests. And yet, as she worked, Ace couldn't shake the feeling that there was more to her life than just polishing silver and welcoming strangers into their rooms.

As she began her rounds, Ace's eyes scanned the lobby, taking in the familiar sights and sounds of another busy day at Lafitte. There was the smell of coffee brewing in the corner, the soft hum of conversation from the bar area, and the occasional clink of glasses as a guest prepared for bed. It was a small, peaceful world, but one that Ace knew all too well.

Just then, a tall, lanky man with a messy beard and a worn leather jacket walked into the lobby, looking like he'd just rolled out of bed. He scanned the room, his eyes locking onto Ace as she approached him with a warm smile.

"Good morning," Ace said, her voice smooth as honey.

The man nodded, his eyes crinkling at the corners. "Morning, Ace. You're looking lovely today."

Ace smiled back, her cheeks warming up to a gentle glow. She knew that look - it was the one he used whenever he wanted to flirt with someone, and Ace had learned to read those cues over the years.

"Thanks," she said, sliding a key across the counter to him. "I'll get you set up in room 314."

As she worked her magic on the keys, Ace couldn't help but wonder what lay ahead for the mysterious man who'd just walked into her world. Little did he know, his arrival at Lafitte was only the beginning of a day that would change everything.
