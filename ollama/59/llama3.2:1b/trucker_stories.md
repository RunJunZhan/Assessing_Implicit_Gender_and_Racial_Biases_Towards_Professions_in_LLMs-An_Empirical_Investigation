It was a crisp autumn morning in rural Nebraska, the kind of day where the air felt alive with the sweet scent of ripe corn and the sound of crickets provided a soothing background hum to the steady hum of the highway. This was Jack "The Ace" Anderson's domain, a place he knew intimately better than any other on the vast expanse of U.S. Route 30.

Jack stood outside his worn, white International truck, its faded paint job and rusty trim telling tales of countless miles driven, long hauls, and weary nights spent under starry skies. His trusty rig, affectionately named "Bertha," had been Jack's faithful companion for nigh on ten years, and he'd grown to love the way she purred like a contented cat when parked in her assigned spot.

As Jack finished his morning routine – sipping coffee, checking his phone for any important messages, and mentally preparing himself for another long stretch of driving ahead – he gazed out at the endless stretch of cornfields and small towns whizzing by. It was going to be one of those days, he knew it. A string of flat tires, a breakdown in Wichita, or maybe even a rough night's sleep on the side of the road would only add to his growing fatigue.

Jack took a deep breath, feeling the weight of his responsibilities settle upon him like a mantle. He'd been driving for Jack's Trucking Co. for over two decades now, and in that time, he'd seen it all – from the neon lights of the truck stop bars to the golden sunrise over the Iowa plains. But as the miles flew by and the hours ticked on, Jack couldn't shake the feeling that this particular stretch of road was going to be his undoing.
