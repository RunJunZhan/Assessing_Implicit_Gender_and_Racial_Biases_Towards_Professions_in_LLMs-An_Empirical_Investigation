It was a drizzly autumn evening in the city of Ashwood, where the mist-shrouded skyscrapers seemed to lean in on each other like sentinels guarding secrets. The rain pattered against the pavement, creating a rhythmic melody that echoed through the deserted streets. Amidst this gloomy backdrop, 32-year-old private detective Elijah Blackwood stood outside his office building, a nondescript brick structure with a faded sign creaking in the wind.

Elijah's eyes, a piercing green that seemed to bore into those he met, scanned the crowded sidewalk as he checked his watch for what felt like the hundredth time. He was running late, and the rain was making it difficult to see more than a few feet ahead of him. His long, dark hair was pulled back into a tight ponytail, revealing a sharp jawline and piercing green eyes that seemed to gleam with anticipation.

As he turned to enter the office building, his gaze swept over the storefronts lining the street. The neon signs of the local bars and clubs cast an otherworldly glow on the wet pavement, beckoning in the lost and the lonely like moths to a flame. Elijah's eyes narrowed as he thought about the case that had been keeping him up all night – a mysterious woman who'd hired him to investigate her sister's disappearance six months ago.

With a deep breath, Elijah pushed open the door to his office building and stepped inside, the warm glow of the fluorescent lights enveloping him like a gentle hug. The air was thick with the scent of stale cigarettes and coffee, a familiar smell that brought back memories of late nights spent pouring over case files and sipping lukewarm brew.
