It was a crisp autumn evening in the bustling city of New Haven, the kind that made your skin prickle with anticipation. The sun had long since set, casting a golden glow over the towering skyscrapers and narrow streets, but it was not enough to light up the dark alleys and cramped bars that lined the sidewalks. Instead, it seemed to dance across the rooftops, casting an otherworldly sheen over everything.

This was home for Jack "Mac" McCarthy, a private detective with a reputation for getting the job done, no matter the cost or consequence. Mac had been running his own operation from a small office above a dimly lit deli in the Tenderloin district for years, taking on cases that others wouldn't touch. And he did them well, with a keen mind and a quick wit that often got him out of sticky situations.

As he sat at his desk, sipping on a lukewarm cup of coffee and staring out into the night, Mac's phone rang, shrill in the quiet of the office. He picked it up, his eyes narrowing as he listened to the voice on the other end.

"Mac, I need your help," the voice said, low and urgent. "Meet me at Club Europa tonight, 23rd floor."
