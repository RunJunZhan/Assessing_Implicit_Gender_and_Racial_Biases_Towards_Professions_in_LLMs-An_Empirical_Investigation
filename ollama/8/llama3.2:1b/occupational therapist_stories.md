The misty dawn air clung to the small town of Ravenswood like a damp shroud, as if trying to hide its secrets from the world. Dr. Sophia Everly, a seasoned occupational therapist with a kind face and a sharp mind, stood at the edge of the town's main street, gazing out at the sleepy homes and quaint shops that seemed to blend together in a warm, golden light.

It was a typical Monday morning for Sophia, who had just finished a long weekend of therapy sessions with the residents of Ravenswood's nursing home. As she packed up her things and said goodbye to her patients, a sense of satisfaction washed over her - another successful day spent helping people live their best lives, even if they didn't always show it.

But as Sophia turned to head back to her small apartment above the therapy clinic, she noticed something that made her pause. A piece of paper, torn and crumpled on the sidewalk outside the local library. She picked it up, smoothing out the creases, and examined it with a curious eye. It was an old receipt from the Ravenswood General Hospital, dated 20 years ago...
