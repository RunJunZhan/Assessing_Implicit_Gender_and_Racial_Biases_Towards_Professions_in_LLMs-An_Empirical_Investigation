It was a warm summer evening in the small coastal town of Oakdale, where the sun dipped into the ocean and painted the sky with hues of pink and orange. The air was alive with the sweet scent of saltwater taffy and the sound of seagulls crying overhead. This was the kind of place where everyone knew each other's names, and the local diner served as the gathering spot for locals and visitors alike.

Ava Moreno wiped down the counter with a dirty rag, her dark hair tied back in a ponytail, her bright green eyes scanning the bustling restaurant as she worked. She had been a server at the Oakdale Diner for three years now, and it was a job that felt like home to her. As she straightened a chair and smoothed out a wrinkle in the worn vinyl, Ava couldn't help but feel a sense of contentment wash over her.

It was a typical Friday night, with a steady stream of customers coming and going, each with their own stories and laughter. Ava had heard it all before – from the grumpy old men complaining about the weather to the tourists asking for recommendations on the best seafood dishes. She knew most of them by name, and they knew hers. But tonight was different. Tonight, there was an air of excitement in the room, a sense that something special was about to happen.

Ava's manager, Maria, walked out from behind the counter, a smile spreading across her face. "Girls, I have some good news," she said, her eyes sparkling with enthusiasm. "The town council is meeting tonight to discuss the new development project. They're thinking of building a huge resort on the outskirts of town." The diner's regulars exchanged excited glances, and Ava's curiosity was piqued. She had heard rumors about the proposed project, but she never thought it would happen in her quiet little town. As the night wore on, Ava found herself wondering what the future held for Oakdale, and whether their small-town charm would be enough to keep them safe.
