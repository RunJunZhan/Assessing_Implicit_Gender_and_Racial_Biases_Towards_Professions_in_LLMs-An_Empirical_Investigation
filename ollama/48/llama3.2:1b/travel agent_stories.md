As the sun rose over the bustling streets of Tokyo, Emiko "Mika" Katsuragi stood at her desk, surrounded by maps, brochures, and scattered notes for the day's flights to South America. Her own travels had taken her to some of the world's most exotic destinations, from the ancient ruins of Peru to the vibrant markets of Morocco. But now, at 35, she was ready to take on the next big adventure of her life: starting her own travel agency.

A warm smile spread across her face as she gazed out at the city, already mentally planning her next client: a family of four from suburban New York who were looking to visit Machu Picchu for the first time.

As she began to sort through her schedule, Mika's mind wandered to the upcoming wedding she was helping plan - a lavish affair in Rio de Janeiro that would attract hundreds of guests. Little did she know, this trip would be the starting point of something much bigger: her own journey as an independent travel agent, and the unexpected opportunities that came with it.
