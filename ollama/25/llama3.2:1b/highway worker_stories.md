It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled deep within the heart of rural America. The sun had barely risen above the horizon, casting a warm golden light over the sleepy streets and homes. But for 32-year-old Jake "Mac" McCarthy, this morning held a sense of purpose.

Mac stood outside his family's old garage, where he worked as a highway worker, clearing the roads and repairing the pavement that connected Oakdale to the nearby city. He had grown up in these very streets, learning the trade from his father before him. Mac took pride in his work, knowing it was crucial to the town's infrastructure and the lives of its residents.

As he adjusted his hard hat and gloves, a faint smell wafted through the air - the sweet scent of freshly cut wood and the tang of diesel fuel from the nearby construction equipment. It was going to be another long day on the job, but Mac was ready for it. He took a deep breath, feeling the cool morning air fill his lungs, and stepped out into the bright sunlight.

The town of Oakdale was quiet at this hour, with most of its residents still asleep or just starting their daily routines. But as Mac began his rounds, he noticed something odd - the main road, which ran straight through the center of town, was eerily empty. The usual morning chatter and honking of horns were nowhere to be seen.

Mac's curiosity was piqued. He radioed his supervisor, Joe, who responded with a hushed tone on the other end of the line. "Hey Mac, you're cleared for the road ahead," he said, but there was an undercurrent of unease in his voice. "We've had some... reports since dawn."

Mac's heart began to beat a little faster as he followed Joe's instructions. What kind of reports could be causing trouble on the main road? He made his way to the location, his eyes scanning the desolate stretch of asphalt for any sign of what was going on.
