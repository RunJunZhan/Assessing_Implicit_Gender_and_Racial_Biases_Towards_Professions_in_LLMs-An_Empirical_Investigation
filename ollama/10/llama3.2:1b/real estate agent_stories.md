It was a crisp autumn morning in the small coastal town of Ravenswood, where the smell of saltwater and freshly baked bread wafted through the air, mingling with the sound of seagulls crying overhead. The sun was just starting to rise over the horizon, casting a warm glow over the quaint Main Street, lined with old-fashioned shops and restaurants that seemed to be frozen in time.

Ava Moreno stood outside her agency, "Morales & Associates," a small but respected real estate firm that had been family-owned for generations. Ava's own mother had started the business when she was just a teenager, and Ava had taken over at 22 after completing her degree in real estate. She loved nothing more than helping families find their dream homes in Ravenswood, which was known for its beautiful beaches, lush parks, and historic architecture.

As Ava adjusted her name tag on her windbreaker, she took a deep breath, feeling a sense of contentment wash over her. It had been a slow morning so far, but the phone hadn't stopped ringing all day, and there were several offers on properties that she was working on. She loved this time of year in Ravenswood - the town was bustling with activity as people began to look for their summer homes or second stories to add onto their existing houses.

Ava's gaze fell on a piece of paper clutched in her hand, a cryptic message scrawled on it in red ink. "Don't trust him." The note had been slipped under her door earlier that morning, and Ava's curiosity was piqued. Who could have written this? And what did they mean by "don't trust"? She showed the note to her assistant, a young woman named Lily, who read it quickly before returning a reassuring smile.

"What do you think it means?" Ava asked, her brow furrowed in concern.

"I'm not sure," Lily replied. "But I'd say we should keep looking into it."

Ava nodded thoughtfully. She had dealt with her fair share of mysterious messages and untrustworthy clients, but there was something about this one that felt off. And now, as she stood on the porch of Morales & Associates, watching the sun rise over Ravenswood, Ava couldn't shake the feeling that her day was about to get a lot more interesting.
