It was a warm summer evening in the small coastal town of Ravenswood, where the air was sweet with the scent of saltwater and the sound of seagulls filled the air. The sun had just set, casting a golden glow over the quaint shops and restaurants that lined the main street. Inside the local watering hole, "The Raven's Nest," bartender and owner Alexandra "Alex" Thompson polished a glass with a white towel, her dark hair tied back in a ponytail as she surveyed the bustling room.

The bar was a favorite haunt for locals and tourists alike, its rustic wooden beams and stone fireplace warming patrons on chilly evenings like this. The smell of freshly brewed coffee wafted from the kitchen, where Alex's sister, Emma, expertly flipped pancakes and scrambled eggs. The soft hum of conversation and clinking glasses filled the air as Alex took a deep breath, feeling the stress of the day melt away.

As she worked, a group of rowdy tourists stumbled in, laughing and joking with each other as they made their way to the bar. They were dressed in bright summer clothes, their eyes shining with excitement as they clinked glasses together for a toast. Alex smiled warmly, shaking hands with the lead beer-hander and offering a friendly welcome. She had been running "The Raven's Nest" for three years now, and she knew these regulars by name. But tonight, something felt off.
