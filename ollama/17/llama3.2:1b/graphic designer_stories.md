The city of Ashwood lay shrouded in a perpetual haze of smoke and grime, its once-vibrant streets now worn down by decades of neglect and decay. Amidst this dystopian backdrop, 25-year-old Luna Nightingale adjusted the straps on her signature black leather jacket, the soft clasp of which held together the tangled mess of her curly brown hair. A graphic designer by trade, Luna's latest project was a go-to gig for clients seeking bold, edgy visuals that reflected their own rebellious spirits – and she took pride in delivering just that.

As she stepped out of her small studio apartment above the bustling café on the corner of 5th and Main, Luna gazed out at the towering skyscrapers that seemed to stretch up to the clouds like skeletal fingers. She had grown up in Ashwood, where the air was thick with the smell of exhaust fumes and desperation. But it was also a place where art and creativity knew no bounds – or so she liked to think.

Luna's phone buzzed with an incoming text from her best friend, Jax, a fellow graphic designer who worked at a rival studio down the street. "Hey, Lu! We're having a meeting with our new client tonight. Want to come?" The message was brief, but Luna's curiosity was piqued – what could this mysterious project be about? She quickly grabbed her bag and headed out into the rain-soaked evening, the sound of the city's perpetual hum growing louder as she made her way to the café.

As she slid onto a stool at the counter, Jax raised an eyebrow over the rim of his cup. "You know what this is, right?" he asked, his voice low and conspiratorial. Luna nodded, and Jax began to explain: "Meetings with 'The Council.' They're always pushing us to create something revolutionary – something that'll change the game... or get us killed."
