It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of rural Pennsylvania. The sun rises over the rolling hills and farmland, casting a warm glow over the quaint Main Street, lined with old-fashioned diners, antique shops, and family-owned businesses.

In the midst of this charming community, 32-year-old Ryder Jenkins is already up and getting ready for another busy day at his family's auto repair shop, "Jenkins' Garage". Ryder's father had started the business years ago, and Ryder has taken over when he passed away suddenly a year ago. He's dedicated himself to carrying on the tradition of providing top-notch service and attention to detail to the residents of Oakdale.

As Ryder sips his coffee and checks the day's schedule, he glances out the window at the familiar faces passing by outside – Mrs. Thompson from 123 Main Street, Mr. Johnson from the local diner, and the Watsons from across the street, who always seem to need a new set of tires or brake pads.

The air is filled with the sweet scent of freshly baked apple pie wafting from the bakery on the corner, enticing everyone in town to come in and indulge. Ryder can't help but smile as he thinks about the long day ahead, full of oil changes, tire rotations, and the occasional breakdown that needs fixing.

Little does Ryder know, his day is about to take an unexpected turn, one that will put his skills as a mechanic to the test like never before.
