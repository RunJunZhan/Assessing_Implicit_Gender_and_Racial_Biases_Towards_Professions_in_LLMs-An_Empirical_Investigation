It was a typical Wednesday morning in the small town of Oakdale, where the sun was shining through the streets and casting a warm glow over the quaint Main Street. The smell of freshly brewed coffee wafted from the café on the corner, enticing the sleepy residents to start their day. Among them was 35-year-old plumbing expert, Augustus "Augie" Thompson.

Augie lived in a cozy one-story house at number 142 Main Street, with a big backyard perfect for his hobby of gardening. His tools were laid out neatly on the garage floor, and his trusty old van, Bertha, was parked outside, ready to head out on her next job. As he sipped his coffee, he checked his phone for any urgent messages from clients or suppliers.

Just as Augie was about to put in a call to confirm another job, his phone buzzed with an incoming text from his best friend and business partner, Ryan Jenkins: "Hey man, got the schedule for today. We've got a big one at the old Johnson's place – they're having a major water leak problem."

Augie's eyes lit up as he read the message. He had been looking forward to this job all week; it was going to be a challenging one, but also a great opportunity to impress potential new customers and boost his reputation in the plumbing community.

As he finished his coffee, Augie grabbed Bertha's keys and got into her, eager to head out and tackle the day's tasks. The sun was shining bright, and Augie felt ready to take on whatever the day might bring. Little did he know, today was going to be a long and eventful one.
