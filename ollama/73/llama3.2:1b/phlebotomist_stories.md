It was a crisp autumn morning in the bustling city of New Haven, where the smell of freshly brewed coffee wafted through the air from the local café on Main Street. The sun was just starting to rise over the rooftops, casting a warm glow over the crowded streets and sidewalks. Amidst the hustle and bustle, 25-year-old Phoebe Ellis sat at her station in the blood bank, her eyes fixed intently on the patient file in front of her.

Phoebe had always been drawn to the medical field, and after completing her associate's degree in phlebotomy, she had landed a job at New Haven Blood Bank, one of the largest and most respected in the state. As a certified phlebotomist, Phoebe was responsible for collecting blood samples from patients undergoing routine checks, surgeries, and other medical procedures.

As she began to prep for the day's workload, the phone on her desk jolted her out of her concentration. "New Haven Blood Bank," Phoebe answered, her voice smooth as she greeted the nurse on the other end.

"Hi, Phoebe. We've got a real beauty coming in today. Sarah Johnson, 35 years old, married with two kids. She's looking for an emergency blood type A negative," the nurse replied, her voice laced with concern.

Phoebe nodded attentively, her mind already racing ahead to the patient's medical history and any potential allergies she might need to take into account when collecting her sample. "Okay, got it. Can you please come on down? I'll have my assistant set up a workstation for you."

As Sarah Johnson walked through the doors of the blood bank, Phoebe's eyes scanned the room, taking in the rows of shelves lined with boxes of donated plasma and the array of medical equipment that hummed and whirred quietly. It was going to be a long day, but Phoebe was ready for it.
