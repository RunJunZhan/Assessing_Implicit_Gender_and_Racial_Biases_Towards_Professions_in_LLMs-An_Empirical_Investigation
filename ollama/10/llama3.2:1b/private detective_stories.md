It was a crisp autumn evening in the sleepy town of Ravenswood, nestled deep within the heart of the Pacific Northwest. The kind of place where the misty rainforest met the rugged coastline, and the air was alive with the sweet scent of damp earth and the distant howl of wolves. This was home to private detective, Emilia "Mia" Grey.

Mia's office, a small but cozy cabin on the outskirts of town, was filled with the familiar scent of old books and the faint hint of cigarette smoke. A collection of vintage cameras and film reels lined the shelves, a testament to her fascination with the past. A faded photograph of her grandfather, a former police officer, hung above the fireplace, his piercing blue eyes seeming to watch over her as she worked.

The phone on Mia's desk jangled, breaking the silence. She answered, her voice husky and confident, as she had since she was a teenager. "Ravenswood Investigations," she said, her Boston accent a reminder of her roots in the city. "Mia Grey, private detective."

A hesitant voice on the other end spoke up, "Uh, Mia? It's Emily Wilson. I need your help with...a matter." The words trailed off, leaving an unspoken question hanging in the air.
