But amidst all this progress, one thing remained constant: the dedication of the medical professionals who called New Haven home.

Dr. Ava Moreno was a physician's assistant at the prestigious Oakwood Hospital, where she worked alongside some of the city's top doctors to care for its most vulnerable populations. With her short, dark hair and piercing green eyes, Ava exuded an air of calm confidence that put even the most anxious patients at ease. She had spent years honing her skills in the OR, as a med student, and was now on the cusp of becoming a full-fledged physician herself.

As she scrubbed out of her latest procedure, Ava took a moment to glance out the window of her small apartment above the hospital's urgent care center. The sun was setting over the city, casting a warm orange glow over the rooftops and streets below. It was a view she never tired of, but one that always made her feel both grateful and restless.

Ava's thoughts were interrupted by the beep of her phone, summoning her to the hospital's main lobby for a meeting with Dr. Rachel Kim, her mentor and the hospital's chief of residency program. Ava gathered her things and headed downstairs, feeling a sense of anticipation building in her chest as she prepared to embark on what promised to be another busy day at Oakwood Hospital.
