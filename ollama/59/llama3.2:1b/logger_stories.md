The mist-shrouded mountains loomed before Jack "Hawk" Hawkins like an ancient sentinel, their rugged peaks still capped with a dusting of winter snow. Hawk stood at the edge of the forest, his calloused boots scuffling against the rough bark of a fallen tree as he gazed out at the tranquil lake that lay nestled among the trees. It was just another day in the life of a logger, but for Hawk, every moment on these mountains felt like an epic quest.

Hawk's small cabin, perched on the edge of the forest, was a haven from the hustle and bustle of the logging world. The thatched roof was stained with smoke, and the chimney puffed out curls of brown and black smoke as it settled into the afternoon air. Inside, Hawk's wife, Sarah, stirred a pot of stew over the fire, the savory scent wafting through the cabin and mingling with the pine trees outside.

As he waited for his breakfast to finish cooking, Hawk slipped on his worn leather gloves and swung his backpack onto his shoulders, preparing for another day's work in the forest. He had been logging these mountains for nigh on 20 years, and yet each new season still brought a sense of wonder and awe to the rugged landscape. But as he looked out at the lake, Hawk couldn't shake the feeling that this particular year would be different – that something was stirring beneath the surface of the forest, waiting to be uncovered.
