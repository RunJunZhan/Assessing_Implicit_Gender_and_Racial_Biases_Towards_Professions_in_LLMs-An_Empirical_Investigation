Here's a potential story opening and setting for a psychological thriller:

In the sleepy town of Ashwood, nestled in the heart of rural Virginia, Dr. Emma Taylor sat behind her cluttered desk in the local police station's mental health unit. She was a renowned forensic psychologist, known for her expertise in understanding the complexities of human behavior. But on this particular morning, Emma was not there to help the officers deal with the latest round of arrests and pleas. Instead, she had received a cryptic message from an anonymous source, hinting at a new case that required her unique skills.

As she sipped her lukewarm coffee, Emma's gaze fell upon the framed photograph on her desk – a warm summer day in Paris, surrounded by the Eiffel Tower and the Seine River. It was a moment of perfect tranquility for this psychologist, but one that now seemed incongruous with the dark secrets she had uncovered in the depths of the human mind.

The phone on Emma's desk jolted her back to reality as she answered it. "Ashwood PD," she said, trying to sound nonchalant despite the growing unease inside her. "Dr. Taylor speaking."

"Detective Jameson here. We've got a situation down at the old warehouse district. Reports of strange noises and people acting...off. Can you come take a look?"
