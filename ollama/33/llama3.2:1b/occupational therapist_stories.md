Here's a potential story opening and setting for an occupational therapy-themed novel:

The misty morning air clung to the streets of Ashwood, like a damp shroud that refused to lift. Dr. Emilia "Mia" Thompson stood at the window of her small, cluttered office in the city's oldest district, watching as the first light of dawn crept over the rooftops. It was a typical day in late autumn, with the leaves rustling and swirling outside like a chorus of nature's own melody.

Inside her office, a riot of color and sound assaulted Emilia's senses: the hum of the city's traffic, the chirping of birds, and the soft murmur of her patients' gentle voices as they worked through their sessions. But amidst this symphony of sounds, Emilia's mind was already racing ahead to the day's schedule: a group therapy session with a young boy named Jaxon who struggled with dyspraxia and anxiety.

As she turned away from the window, Emilia's gaze fell upon the worn, wooden desk that dominated one corner of her office. It had seen its fair share of rough days, from frantic phone calls to overwhelmed parents, but it had also borne witness to countless moments of triumph: first steps taken, skills mastered, and dreams realized.

With a deep breath, Emilia gathered her thoughts and began to prepare for the day ahead. Little did she know, today's patients would bring more than just challenges – they would also reveal hidden strengths, spark new ideas, and push her to be her best self as an occupational therapist.
