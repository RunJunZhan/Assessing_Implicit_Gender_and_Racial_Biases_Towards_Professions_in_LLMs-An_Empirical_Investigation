Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel about a roofer:

The small town of Willow Creek lay shrouded in a perpetual veil of mist, as if the very earth itself was trying to conceal its secrets from the outside world. Nestled deep within the heart of the Appalachian Mountains, Willow Creek was a tight-knit community where everyone knew each other's names and stories. It was here that Jack "Hawk" Hawkins had made a name for himself as one of the town's most skilled and respected roofers.

Jack stood at the edge of his family's old hardware store, gazing out at the misty landscape with a mixture of nostalgia and trepidation. He had just been handed the keys to the store, and the weight of responsibility was settling in like a cold stone. As he adjusted his worn denim jacket and surveyed the familiar faces of the townspeople, Jack couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and purpose. This was his home, after all, and it was time to pass on the torch to the next generation.

The store itself had been in Jack's family for generations, and its wooden sign creaked gently in the breeze as he unlocked the door with a rusty key. Inside, the familiar scent of sawdust and fresh paint filled his nostrils, transporting him back to countless afternoons spent fixing roofs and talking business with his father before him. The store was a reflection of Jack's own rugged individualism – a no-frills, get-the-job-done kind of place where everyone was equal in their own way.

As he began to sort through the inventory, Jack's mind wandered back to the last job of the season, when a sudden storm had rolled in and left the town's residents scrambling for shelter. He remembered the feel of the wind whipping his hair into a frenzy as he leapt onto his trusty old rig, the one with the faded paint job and the worn-out tires that seemed to have more character than a person. The drive back to Willow Creek had been a blur of rain and sleet, but Jack's skills had kicked in, allowing him to navigate the treacherous roads with ease.

Now, as he gazed out at the misty landscape once again, Jack felt a sense of anticipation building inside him. This would be his last season on the job before retirement, and it was time to think about what came next. Would he stay in Willow Creek, or would he venture into the wider world beyond? The questions swirled in Jack's mind like the mist that clung to the mountainsides, leaving them shrouded in mystery until they were just a memory.
