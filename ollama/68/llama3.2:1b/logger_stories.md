The sun was just starting to peek over the horizon, casting a warm glow over the dense forest of Redwood National Park. Logger Jack "Hawk" Hawkins stood at the edge of the clearing, his eyes scanning the terrain as he took in the familiar sights and sounds of the forest. It was going to be another long day on the job, but Hawk had grown accustomed to the rhythms of logging – the creaking of trees, the chatter of birds, the roar of the chainsaw.

As he adjusted his hard hat and cinched his leather belt around his waist, Hawk's gaze landed on the trailhead in the distance. His truck, a rickety old thing with a faded logo on the side panel, was parked at the edge of the clearing, ready to take him into the woods. He grinned to himself as he mentally prepared for another day of hauling logs and battling the elements.

Hawk's full name was James Andrew Hawkins, but everyone in these parts knew him as Jack "Hawk" Hawkins – a rugged, no-nonsense logger with a reputation for getting the job done, no matter what the weather threw his way. He had been logging these woods for over 20 years, and he knew them like the back of his hand.

Little did Hawk know, today's day would be different from all the others. As he set off into the forest, he would encounter a mysterious stranger who would change everything...
