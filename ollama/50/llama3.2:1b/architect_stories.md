It was a crisp autumn morning in New York City, the sun peeking through the towering skyscrapers of Manhattan as Architects-in-Residence program participant, Julianne "Jules" Thompson, stepped off the subway train at 59th Street – Lexington Avenue. Jules had just turned 35 and was eager to make a name for herself in the competitive field of architecture, having graduated with honors from the University of Pennsylvania's School of Design five years ago.

As she made her way through the crowded streets, the sounds of car horns, chatter, and music filled the air, a stark contrast to the silence that usually reigned in the city's high-rise buildings. Jules felt a thrill of excitement and nerves as she navigated through the maze-like sidewalks, her eyes fixed on the imposing façade of the skyscraper where she was supposed to meet her colleague, Dr. Rachel Kim, an esteemed professor at Columbia University's School of Architecture.

Jules had been assigned to work alongside Professor Kim on a project to design a sustainable community center in Brooklyn, but so far, they hadn't made much progress. As she turned onto Lexington Avenue, Jules spotted the imposing building that was supposed to be their meeting point. She quickened her pace, her heels clicking on the pavement as she pushed open the door and stepped into the lobby of the Beaux-Arts-style skyscraper.
