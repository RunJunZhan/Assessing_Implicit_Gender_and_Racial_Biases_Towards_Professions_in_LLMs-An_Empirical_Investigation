It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, where the leaves on the oak trees rustled with an earthy sweetness as the first light of dawn crept over the horizon. Dr. Sophia Ellis, a school psychologist with years of experience working with students from all walks of life, stood outside her office window, sipping her coffee and gazing out at the familiar streets of Oakdale High School.

As she took in the scene before her – the worn lockers, the faded paint on the hallways, and the sea of curious faces rushing to start their day – Sophia felt a sense of calm wash over her. She had spent countless hours inside these walls, helping students navigate the ups and downs of adolescence, guiding them through academic struggles and personal crises. But today was different. Today, she had a new case, one that would challenge everything she thought she knew about human behavior.

Sophia's phone buzzed on her desk, breaking the spell of her thoughts. She picked it up to see a text from her colleague, Dr. Rachel Kim: "Meet me at the old warehouse on 5th and Main at 9am. I think we need to talk." Sophia's heart skipped a beat as she read the message – what could be going on that required an emergency meeting with another doctor? She quickly typed out a response: "Okay, I'm there. What is it?"
