It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the leaves on the trees turned a vibrant shades of orange and red before falling to the ground. The air was filled with the sweet scent of ripe pumpkin and the sound of leaf blowers humming as the townspeople prepared for the annual Harvest Festival.

In a small drafting shop on Main Street, owned by 25-year-old Ethan Thompson, the sounds of machinery and chatter filled the air. Ethan sat at his desk, staring blankly at his computer screen as he tried to meet an impossible deadline. He was working on a project for the town's new civic center, designing a state-of-the-art arena for the local ice hockey team.

Ethan had always been fascinated by architecture and design, but after a stint in the military, he found himself drawn back into the world of drafting. It was a field that required precision, attention to detail, and a keen eye for aesthetics – skills he hoped would serve him well as he navigated his early 20s.

As he worked, Ethan's phone buzzed with a text from his best friend, Jake, who was also working on the civic center project. "Dude, we need to get moving," Jake typed. "Time is ticking."
