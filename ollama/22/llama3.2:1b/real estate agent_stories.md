Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel about a real estate agent:

The sun was setting over the bustling streets of downtown Los Angeles, casting a warm orange glow over the towering skyscrapers and trendy boutiques that lined the sidewalks. But amidst the glamour of it all, one person stood out - Aurora "Rory" Thompson, a 35-year-old real estate agent with a sharp mind and an even sharper wit.

Rory stood outside her sleek, high-rise office building, sipping on a lukewarm cappuccino as she gazed out at the sea of faces rushing to and fro. She was used to this kind of chaos, having spent years navigating the complex world of Los Angeles real estate. But there was something about this particular evening that felt different - maybe it was the way the light danced through the neon signs of the city's nightlife district, or maybe it was the sense of restlessness in her own chest.

As a top agent with one of the city's most prestigious brokerages, Rory had seen it all. From luxury condos to fixer-uppers, she'd worked with clients from all walks of life - and always seemed to find a way to make their dreams come true. But tonight was different. Tonight, she felt like she was on the verge of something big, something that could change everything.

With a deep breath, Rory pushed open the door to her office and slipped inside, the cool air enveloping her like a slap in the face. She let out a sharp yelp as she knocked over her water glass, spilling it all over the Formica countertop - but smiled sweetly at the receptionist, who was watching her with a mixture of amusement and concern.

"Sorry about that," Rory said, bending down to clean up the spill. "Just a little too much coffee this morning."

The receptionist raised an eyebrow as she handed Rory a glass of water. "You're going to be okay, Ms. Thompson," she said quietly.
