It was a crisp autumn morning in the small coastal town of Ravenswood, where the salty air was filled with the sweet scent of blooming crabapple trees. Dr. Sophia Patel stood outside her office, gazing out at the fog rolling in off the ocean. The misty veil obscured the horizon, casting an otherworldly glow over the sleepy streets. As a physical therapist, Sophia's job was to help people overcome injuries and disabilities, but on days like this, she couldn't help but feel a sense of melancholy wash over her.

Sophia had always been drawn to the ocean, and as a child, she would spend hours building sandcastles and collecting seashells along the beach. Growing up in Ravenswood, she knew every nook and cranny of the town's tight-knit community, but on days like this, it felt like a world away from her own life. She was about to start her third day as a physical therapist at the local hospital, working with patients who had been hurt in accidents or illnesses that left them feeling broken and battered.

As she unlocked the door to her office, Sophia was greeted by the familiar sight of medical records and equipment scattered across the floor. She took a deep breath, letting the scent of antiseptic and fresh coffee fill her lungs, and began to prepare for another day of helping her patients heal and move forward.
