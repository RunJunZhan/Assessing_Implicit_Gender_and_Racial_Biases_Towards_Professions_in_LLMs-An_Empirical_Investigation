It was a crisp autumn morning in New Haven, Connecticut, where the leaves on the ancient oak trees rustled softly in the gentle breeze. The smell of freshly brewed coffee wafted through the air from the cozy café on Elm Street, enticing passersby to come and indulge in a warm cup. Amidst the bustle, 22-year-old bell hop Ember Wellesley worked with precision and care, her bright pink shirt and matching hat a familiar sight to the locals.

Ember lived in a small apartment above the café, where she shared a tiny room with her best friend and confidant, 19-year-old waitress Lily Patel. The two had grown up together, exploring the streets of New Haven and sharing secrets in the cramped quarters of Ember's apartment. As bell hop, Ember was well-versed in the ins and outs of customer service, having learned the ropes from her parents, who owned a small antique shop on the same street.

Just as Ember was starting to get into her morning routine, a frantic look escaped Lily's eyes, followed by a wave of relief as she rushed back into the café. "Ember, I need your help," Lily exclaimed, hardly containing her excitement. "One of our regulars is in an accident on the way here, and we're short-staffed. Can you please check on them for us?"
