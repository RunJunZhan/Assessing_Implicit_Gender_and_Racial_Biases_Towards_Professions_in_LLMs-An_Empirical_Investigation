It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Raven's Peak, nestled deep within the heart of the Pacific Northwest. The sky is a deep shade of indigo, with just a few wispy clouds scattered about like cotton candy tufts. The air is cool and damp, carrying the scent of fresh-cut pine and decaying leaves.

At the local lumberyard, "McTavish's Timber", logger Jack McKenzie sits at his workbench, his rugged features etched into a mixture of sleep and concentration. His dark hair is mussed, and his worn denim overalls are covered in splatters of sawdust and dirt from another long day on the job. A leather glove rests atop his messy brow, protecting his calloused hand as he tinkers with the broken chain on his chainsaw.

As Jack works, the sounds of the yard fill the air: the creaking of logs, the hum of engines, and the occasional shout of a coworker calling out to him. The smell of wood smoke wafts through the windows, mingling with the scent of fresh coffee brewing in the kitchen below. It's a smell Jack knows well – it's his home away from home, the place where he can escape the confines of his small town and lose himself in the rhythms of the forest.

But on this particular morning, something feels off. The trees seem to be leaning in, as if listening to some unheard whisper. The wind carries a faint sense of unease, like it's trying to convey a message that only Jack can hear. He sets down his tools and stares out at the forest, his eyes narrowing as he tries to make sense of the feeling. Little does he know, tonight will bring more than just another day on the job – it will mark the beginning of something much bigger.
