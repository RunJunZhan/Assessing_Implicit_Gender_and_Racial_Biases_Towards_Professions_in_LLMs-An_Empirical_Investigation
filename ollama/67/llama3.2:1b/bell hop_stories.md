It was a crisp autumn morning in the bustling city of New Haven, where the sounds of car horns and chatter filled the air. The historic train station, with its ornate ironwork and grand clock tower, was abuzz with activity as commuters prepared to head out for their daily jobs. Amidst the chaos, 22-year-old bellhop, Emrys "Evie" Thompson, hurried through the winding corridors, his bright blue uniform a stark contrast to the drab city surroundings.

Evie had grown up in New Haven, but after leaving college, he'd drifted from job to job, never staying in one place for too long. His passion lay in helping others, and as a bellhop, he got to spend his days interacting with people from all walks of life – be they business executives, families on vacation, or students rushing to class. Despite the monotony, Evie loved his job; it was his chance to make a difference in someone's day, and for that, he was truly grateful.

As he navigated the crowded station, Evie expertly juggling his pace with the constant flow of passengers, his eyes scanning the waiting area for any sign of his next assignment. His phone buzzed with an incoming text from his manager, reminding him to finish cleaning up the lounge car before the morning rush began. With a smile, Evie took a deep breath, mentally preparing himself for another day of service and connection with the people who came through his doors.
