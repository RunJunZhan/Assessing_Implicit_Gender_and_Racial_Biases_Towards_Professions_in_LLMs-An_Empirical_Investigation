In the bustling city of Portland, Maine, where the rugged coastline meets the tranquil waters of Casco Bay, Dr. Sophia Alexandra "Sophie" Thompson stood at the edge of the 12th floor of her office building, gazing out at the grey, overcast sky. The smell of freshly brewed coffee wafted through the air from the café downstairs, mingling with the sound of honking cars and chirping seagulls. It was a typical Portland morning, yet Sophie felt a sense of unease wash over her as she began to pack up her things for the day.

Sophie was a physical therapist at Mercy General Medical Center, where she specialized in helping patients recover from injuries sustained on the job or in accidents. She had always been passionate about helping others heal and move on, but lately, Sophie couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. She had been experiencing strange visions and nightmares since her last therapy session with a new patient – a young man named Alex who had been involved in a horrific accident while working on a construction site. The images she saw in her dreams were hauntingly familiar, and Sophie couldn't help but wonder if there was more to the case than met the eye.

As she turned to grab her bag, Sophie noticed a piece of paper stuck between the pages of her journal. It was an old prescription pad with scribbled notes from her first patient – Alex. The handwriting was messy, but one phrase stood out: "Meet me at 5th and Main."
