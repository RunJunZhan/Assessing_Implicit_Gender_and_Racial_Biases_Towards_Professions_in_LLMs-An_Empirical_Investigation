The misty dawn air clung to the streets of Ravenswood like a damp shroud, casting a grayish hue over the quaint medical center where Dr. Ava Morales had spent the past six months working as a physician's assistant. The smell of freshly brewed coffee wafted from the hospital cafeteria, enticing Ava as she walked down the main hallway, her crisp white scrubs a stark contrast to the worn denim jacket slung over her shoulder.

Ava had always been drawn to the quiet rhythm of life in Ravenswood, where every resident knew each other's names and stories. As a physician's assistant at Oakwood Medical Center, she'd gotten used to the rhythms of small-town living: the early morning sunrise that painted the sky with hues of pink and gold; the smell of freshly baked bread wafting from the community bakery; and the sound of laughter carried on the breeze as neighbors gathered in the town square for Sunday church services.

But tonight was different. Tonight, Ava felt a sense of unease settle over her like a shroud. She'd been receiving strange messages on her phone since early that morning – cryptic warnings about an outbreak that seemed to be spreading rapidly through Ravenswood's small community. The messages were vague, but they sent a shiver down Ava's spine.

As she walked into the hospital, Ava was greeted by the warm smile of Dr. Patel, who handed her a steaming cup of coffee and whispered, "You look like you could use this."
