It was a typical Wednesday morning in the bustling city of New Haven, with the sounds of car horns and chatter filling the air as people rushed to get to work. Amidst the chaos, 28-year-old software engineer Emily Patel sat at her desk, staring blankly at her computer screen. She had been working on a top-secret project for weeks, but something felt off. The code she was trying to debug was starting to throw errors left and right, and she couldn't shake the feeling that she was being watched.

Emily's apartment was just a few blocks away from the office building where she worked as a freelance programmer, and it was a cozy little place with high ceilings and large windows that let in plenty of natural light. A collection of books and tech gadgets lined the shelves, giving the space a bit of a geeky vibe. Despite her love of coding, Emily had always been more of an introvert, content to spend hours alone at home tapping away on her keyboard.

As she sat there, trying to focus on the code in front of her, Emily's mind began to wander back to her family and friends. Her parents were still living in India, and her younger brother was getting into some kind of trouble in college. She felt a pang of guilt and responsibility, knowing that she had always been able to provide for them financially.

Just as Emily was starting to get sucked back into the world of coding, there was a knock at the door. It was her neighbor, Maria, who lived next door. "Hey, Em! I saw you staring at that computer screen again," Maria said with a knowing smile. "What's going on? You look like you're in a daze."
