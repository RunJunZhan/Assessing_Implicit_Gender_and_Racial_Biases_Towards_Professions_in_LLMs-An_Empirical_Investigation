Here's a potential story opening and setting for a psychological thriller:

In the sleepy town of Ashwood, nestled in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains, Dr. Emily Mayfield stood tall, her piercing green eyes scanning the bustling street with a practiced air of intensity. As a renowned forensic psychologist, she had spent years studying the minds of serial killers and analyzing their crimes, but nothing could have prepared her for the horror that was about to unfold.

The town's annual summer festival was in full swing, with people from all over the region flocking to Ashwood's picturesque main street. Emily had arrived early, sipping coffee at a small café on Main Street, where she met with local sheriff's department detective, James "Hawk" Hawkins. A rugged, no-nonsense man in his late 40s with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes, Hawk was the last person Emily expected to trust.

As they exchanged pleasantries, Emily couldn't shake off the feeling that something was off. The festival's festivities seemed eerily quiet, with nary a crowd in sight. The usually vibrant colors of the decorations now felt muted and forlorn. And then, as she turned to leave, she noticed it: a large, handwritten sign on the town hall door, featuring a single phrase that made her blood run cold:

"We are watching."
