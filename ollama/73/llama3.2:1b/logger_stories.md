It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Ravenswood, nestled deep within the heart of the Redwood National Forest. The air is alive with the sweet scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, carried on the gentle breeze that rustles through the towering conifers. In a small cabin on the outskirts of town, logger Jack McKenzie sips his coffee, gazing out at the forest he's spent his entire life exploring.

Jack's family has lived in Ravenswood for generations, and his father was one of the last remaining loggers in the area. After his father's passing, Jack inherited the cabin and a share of his father's old equipment. He spends most days working from the cabin, preparing for the upcoming logging season. But as he prepares to head out into the forest, he can't shake the feeling that this year will be different.

The previous season was marked by intense rainfall and high winds, causing widespread damage to the trees and making it harder than ever to harvest the timber. Jack has been struggling to find work, and his bank account is dwindling. He's forced to consider selling some of his family's old equipment or taking on odd jobs to make ends meet.

As he steps out into the forest, the sunlight casts dappled shadows across the trail, and the sound of birds chirping fills the air. Jack takes a deep breath, shouldering his backpack and slinging his axe over his shoulder. He's headed to meet with his old friend and colleague, Mike Reynolds, who's looking to start a new logging crew. Jack hopes that this year will be better than the last, but he can't help feeling a sense of trepidation as he sets off into the unknown.
