It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of the countryside. The sun was just starting to rise over the horizon, casting a warm glow over the quaint streets and homes. Probation Officer Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stood outside the local community center, sipping her coffee and surveying the scene before her.

The building, once a thriving hub for social services and youth programs, now stood as a testament to the town's fading vitality. Lexi had been assigned to this case six months ago, tasked with monitoring the release of convicted felon, Marcus Jackson. A former gang member, Marcus had been granted early parole but still maintained a strong grip on his old life.

As Lexi stepped inside, she was greeted by the familiar scent of stale air and yesterday's coffee. The community center's offices were cluttered with dusty files and faded posters advertising local events. At the front desk, a harried-looking secretary clutched the phone, her eyes darting nervously around the room.

"Can I help you?" she asked Lexi, barely above a whisper.

"I'm here to meet with Marcus Jackson," Lexi replied firmly, her voice carrying across the room. "What's his status?"

The secretary nodded hastily and called out into the chaos of the office, "Marcus is in holding cells! He'll be ready for your meeting shortly."

Lexi made her way to the detention area, where she found a cell block filled with Marcus's belongings and the occasional visitor trying to catch his eye. Lexi spotted him sitting on a bunk bed, watching her approach with a mixture of curiosity and hostility.

"Marcus," she said calmly, her hand resting lightly on the grip of her gun.

Marcus raised an eyebrow, but didn't respond.
