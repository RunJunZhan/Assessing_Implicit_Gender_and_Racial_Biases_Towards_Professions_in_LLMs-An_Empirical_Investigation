Here's a potential story opening and setting for a psychological thriller:

It was a chilly autumn evening in the small town of Ravenswood, nestled in the heart of the Pacific Northwest. Dr. Aurora "Rory" Thompson sat at her desk, sipping on a lukewarm cup of coffee as she stared out the window of her office, a modest stone building on Elm Street. The once-quaint town was now a labyrinth of twinkling streetlights and the distant hum of the highway, its residents' lives forever intertwined with the dark secrets that lurked in every shadow.

Rory's office, a cramped but cozy space filled with the musty scent of old books and the faint hint of her own anxiety, was her sanctuary. She had spent years honing her craft as a forensic psychologist, working with the darkest corners of human nature to unravel the tangled threads of trauma and deception that bound them together.

As she gazed out into the fading light, Rory's mind began to wander back to the latest case files on her desk – a string of seemingly unrelated murders that had been plaguing Ravenswood for months. The victims all shared one thing in common: they had been found with no signs of forced entry or struggle, yet their deaths seemed to have no apparent connection to each other.

Rory's eyes refocused on the task at hand as she scribbled notes on her pad, a habit honed from years of intense focus and intellectual curiosity. She was determined to unravel the tangled web of motives and conspiracies that lay beneath the surface of Ravenswood's seemingly idyllic facade.

But as the clock ticked closer to midnight, Rory couldn't shake the feeling that she was being watched – not just by her patients, but by something else, lurking just beyond the periphery of her perception.
