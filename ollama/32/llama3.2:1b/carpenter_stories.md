The small town of Willow Creek lay shrouded in a gentle mist, its residents going about their daily lives with a quiet ease that belied the secrets and struggles that simmered just beneath the surface. In this tranquil setting, one individual stood out - Elijah Ryder, a skilled carpenter with hands as much as eyes, who had returned to his hometown after years away.

Elijah's worn leather boots and faded jeans were a testament to his rugged profession, but it was the silver trim on his tool belt that caught the attention of the townsfolk. A veteran of countless construction sites, Elijah had built a reputation for his attention to detail and unwavering dedication to every project he took on.

As Elijah stepped off the worn wooden dock and onto the dew-kissed grass of his family's old homestead, the mist swirled around him like a living entity, casting an otherworldly glow over the sleepy town. The air was alive with the sweet scent of blooming lilacs and the soft chirping of birds, a symphony that seemed to match the gentle lapping of the creek against its banks.

Elijah's thoughts, however, were far from peaceful. His mind was consumed by the words of an old colleague, who had confided in him just before passing away: "The woods are calling, Elijah. Don't ignore it."
