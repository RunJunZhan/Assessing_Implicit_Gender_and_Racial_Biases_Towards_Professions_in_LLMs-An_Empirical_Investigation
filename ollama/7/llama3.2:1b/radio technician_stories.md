It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of rural Illinois. The sun was just starting to rise over the rolling hills and fields of golden wheat, casting a warm orange glow over the sleepy streets. In the midst of this tranquility, a sense of unease settled over 25-year-old radio technician, Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson.

Lexi lived in a small two-story house on Elm Street, with her grandfather's old Victorian-style mansion as her bedroom window perch. The house was filled with the familiar scent of freshly baked cookies and the soft hum of antique radios, which had been passed down through generations of Thompsons. Lexi loved nothing more than spending hours tinkering with her grandfather's equipment in his workshop, listening to vintage broadcasts on the old radio, and exploring the town's hidden corners.

As she stepped out into the crisp morning air, Lexi felt a shiver run down her spine. She couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The local news report from the night before had mentioned an eerie cold front moving in, with reports of strange occurrences throughout the town. Some folks were acting strangely, others were going missing, and the police were stumped.

Lexi's phone buzzed in her pocket, breaking the stillness. It was her best friend, Rachel, calling to check in. Lexi answered, her heart racing as she listened to Rachel's concerns about the strange happenings in Oakdale. As they chatted, Lexi couldn't shake the feeling that something was watching her from the shadows, waiting for its moment to strike.
