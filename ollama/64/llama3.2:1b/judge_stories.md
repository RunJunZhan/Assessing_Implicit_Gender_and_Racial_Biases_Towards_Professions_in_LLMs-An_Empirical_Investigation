Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel featuring a judge:

The year was 2055, and the city of New Haven was a vastly different place from the one that once stood before its walls. The streets were clean and efficient, the buildings sleek and modern, and the people... well, the people were something else entirely.

Judge Maya Blackwood presided over the high court, her sharp mind and piercing gaze commanding respect from even the most hardened defendants. But Maya's life was about to take a dramatic turn - one that would challenge everything she thought she knew about herself, her duty as a judge, and the very fabric of society itself.

It began on a typical Monday morning, with a new case coming in for arraignment. The defendant, a young woman named Emily Lee, sat nervously in the courtroom, her eyes fixed on Maya's stern face as she droned on about the charges against her. But as the proceedings began, something strange started to happen - the air in the room seemed to thicken, like a fog was rolling in off the city streets.

Maya's eyes narrowed as she listened to Emily's testimony, but it wasn't just the defendant's words that were causing trouble - it was something more. A subtle hum, almost imperceptible, began to build in intensity, until suddenly, the lights flickered and died.

In the darkness, a figure materialized before Maya's eyes. It was Emily Lee, or at least, it looked like her - but there was something off about her face, something that didn't quite add up.

And then, in a voice that sent shivers down Maya's spine, Emily spoke: "I'm not here to be judged."
