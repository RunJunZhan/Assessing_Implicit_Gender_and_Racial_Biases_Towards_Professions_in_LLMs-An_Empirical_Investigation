It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains. The sun was just starting to rise over the rooftops, casting a warm glow over the quaint streets and homes. In a small electrical contracting company, owned and operated by Jack "Hawk" Hawkins, the day had already begun.

Jack, a ruggedly handsome man in his mid-40s with a thick beard and piercing blue eyes, was sipping on a cup of coffee as he sat at his desk, going over the day's schedule. His company, Hawk Electric, had been running for over 20 years, and Jack took pride in being one of the most reliable and respected electricians in the region.

As he scanned the list of jobs to be completed that day, a knock came at the door from his assistant, Emily Patel, a bright-eyed young woman with short black hair and a warm smile. "Good morning, Jack! I've got everything ready for today's appointments," she said, holding up a stack of papers.

Just then, the phone on his desk rang, shrill and insistent. Jack picked it up, rubbing the sleep from his eyes. "Hawk Electric, this is Jack. Who's calling?"
