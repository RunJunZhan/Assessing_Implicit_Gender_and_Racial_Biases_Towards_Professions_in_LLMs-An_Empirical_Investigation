In the mist-shrouded city of Ashwood, where the River Styx curved through its winding streets like a vein in the earth, Justice Elianore Quasar presided over the twilight court with an air of quiet authority. Her name, once synonymous with precision and fairness, had taken on a slightly tarnished sheen after years of overseeing the city's most contentious cases. Despite this, her reputation as a wise and impartial arbiter remained unwavering.

Elianore's imposing figure stood out amidst the crowded courtroom, her silver hair pulled back into a neat braid and her piercing blue eyes fixed intently on each defendant as they spoke. Her sharp jawline was set in a determined expression, and her slender fingers drummed a staccato rhythm against the armrest of her chair, a habit honed from years of concentrating on even the most complex cases.

As she listened to the latest petition for habeas corpus, Elianore's thoughts drifted back to the summer before last, when the city had been rocked by riots sparked by a series of mysterious attacks. A young woman named Ava Morales had been among those who had lost her life in the chaos, and Elianore had found herself at the forefront of the investigation into the crimes. Despite her best efforts to remain detached from the case, she couldn't shake the feeling that she had failed somehow – a nagging sense of inadequacy that would continue to haunt her even as she presided over the twilight court.
