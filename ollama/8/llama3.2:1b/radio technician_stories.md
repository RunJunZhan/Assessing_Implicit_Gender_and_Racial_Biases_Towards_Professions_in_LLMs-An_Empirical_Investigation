It was a crisp autumn evening in the small town of Oakdale, nestled deep within the heart of rural New England. The sky was painted with hues of orange and pink as the sun dipped below the horizon, casting a warm glow over the quaint streets and homes. In the midst of this picturesque setting, 22-year-old radio technician, Ethan James, stood at the edge of town, gazing out at the darkness.

Ethan had always been fascinated by the mysteries that lay beyond the reach of his small-town life. As a child, he spent countless hours listening to amateur radio broadcasts from the woods, uncovering tales of far-off lands and unknown technologies. After completing his degree in electronics and telecommunications, Ethan landed a job at Oakdale's local community radio station, Radiodale FM. It was there that he honed his skills as a radio technician, learning to navigate the complex world of shortwave transmissions and satellite communications.

On this particular evening, Ethan was busy configuring a new antenna for the station's next transmission. He worked efficiently, his fingers flying across the control panel as he fine-tuned the signal. The hum of the equipment filled the air, accompanied by the sweet chirping of birds outside. It was moments like these that Ethan felt most at home, lost in the gentle rhythm of the radio signals and the peacefulness of the night.

As he finished the final adjustments, a faint voice crackled over the radio, a soft whisper from an unknown location. "Radiodale FM... this is Echo-7 reporting. We come in loud and clear..." Ethan's eyes narrowed, his curiosity piqued. Who was this mysterious entity, and what were they trying to convey? He felt a thrill of excitement mixed with a hint of unease as he wondered what the night had just taken him into.
