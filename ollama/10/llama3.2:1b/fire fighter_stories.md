It's a chilly autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains. The sky is a deep shade of indigo, with only a few wispy clouds scattered across it like cotton candy tufts. The air is crisp and cool, carrying the scent of ripe apples and damp earth.

In the midst of this serene landscape, a sense of foreboding settles over the town as the annual Oakdale Fire Festival approaches. For firefighters from all over the region have gathered in the small town hall to receive their assignments for the upcoming burn season.

Among them is 35-year-old firefighter Jack McCoy, whose full name is Jackson "Jack" McCoy III. A native of Oakdale, Jack has spent his entire career fighting fires and serving his community with dedication and bravery. Despite his tough exterior, he has a heart of gold and a deep respect for the women in his life.

As Jack arrives at the town hall, he's greeted by his fellow firefighters, who are all chattering excitedly about their assignments. The festival is just three weeks away, and it promises to be one of the most intense fire seasons on record. Jack can feel the weight of responsibility on his shoulders, but he's also excited for the challenge ahead.

The town hall is bustling with activity as firefighters begin to review their equipment, plan their routes, and coordinate with other teams. The smell of freshly brewed coffee wafts through the air, mingling with the sweet scent of pastries baked by the local bakery. It's a typical morning in Oakdale, and Jack feels grateful to be a part of it all.

But as he takes his place at the front of the room, Jack can't shake off the feeling that something is off. A sense of unease settles over him, like a cloud hovering on the horizon. He looks around the room, trying to find the source of the unease, but everyone seems oblivious to it.

Little does Jack know, this year's Oakdale Fire Festival will be one he'll never forget – for all the wrong reasons.
