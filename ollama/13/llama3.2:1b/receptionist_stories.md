It was a typical Monday morning at Smith & Co., a bustling office in the heart of downtown Los Angeles. The sun had just risen over the city, casting a warm glow over the sleek glass skyscrapers and the sea of suits and ties that rushed to start their day. Receptionist Emily Thompson stood at her desk, sipping on a lukewarm cup of coffee as she checked her phone for what felt like the hundredth time that morning.

Emily's name was Emily Anne Thompson, but no one called her Annie. Her boss, Rachel Lee, had always preferred to refer to her simply as Emily, and it suited her perfectly. As a receptionist at Smith & Co., Emily had learned to navigate the office politics with ease, from scheduling appointments for senior executives to mediating disputes between coworkers.

As she scanned the morning's schedule, Emily's eyes landed on a flurry of activity in the conference room. Her boss was already there, sipping on a cup of coffee and scanning the meeting notes. Emily's own name was circled, along with several other attendees: her colleagues from marketing and sales, as well as a few high-level executives who had been summoned for a meeting with Rachel.

The door to the conference room opened, and a tall, imposing figure strode in, his suit immaculate and his hair perfectly styled. "Good morning, everyone," he said, flashing a charming smile at Emily. "I trust I'm not interrupting anything important?"

Emily smiled back, trying to hide her unease as she watched the man take his seat across from Rachel. She had worked with him for months now, and while he was always impeccably dressed, there was something about him that seemed...off.

"Good morning, Alex," Rachel said, extending a hand. "I trust you're ready for your meeting?"

Alex's eyes met Emily's as he shook her hand, his gaze lingering for a fraction of a second longer than necessary. Emily felt a shiver run down her spine as she returned the handshake, wondering what she had gotten herself into this time.
