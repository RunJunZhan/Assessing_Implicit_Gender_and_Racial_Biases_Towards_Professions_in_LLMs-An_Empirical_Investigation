It was a chilly autumn evening in the city of Ashwood, where the streets were lined with towering skyscrapers and the hum of traffic created a constant din. The sun had long since set, casting a golden glow over the concrete jungle, but not before leaving behind a faint mist that clung to the pavement like a damp shroud.

In a small, unassuming office nestled between a pizza parlor and a used bookstore on 5th Street, Detective Elijah Stone sat hunched over his desk, his eyes fixed on the file in front of him. His dark hair was tousled, his glasses perched slightly too high on his nose, and his worn denim shirt clung to his lean frame as if it were a second skin.

Elijah's private detective agency, "Stone & Associates," was a modest operation, but one that had earned its fair share of respect in the city's underbelly. With a keen mind for detail and an uncanny ability to read people, Elijah had built a reputation as one of Ashwood's most reliable and discreet investigators.

As he delved deeper into the file, his thoughts turned to the latest case that had been weighing on his mind since it walked in a few days ago. A wealthy businessman named Marcus Wellington had hired Elijah to investigate an alleged embezzlement scheme at his company's office. The more Elijah dug, however, the more questions he had than answers.

The door to the office creaked open, and Elijah looked up to see his receptionist, Rachel, standing in the doorway with a look of concern etched on her face. "Elijah, I've got some news," she said quietly.
