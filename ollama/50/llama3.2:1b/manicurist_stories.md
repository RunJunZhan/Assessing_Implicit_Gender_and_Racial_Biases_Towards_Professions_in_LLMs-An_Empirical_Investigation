It was a drizzly Sunday morning in Portland, Oregon, and the streets were emptying out as the first waves of the city's hipsters and artists began to trickle in for their weekly coffee runs. Among them was Emilia "Mia" Thompson, a 25-year-old manicurist at local salon "Piggy P's Polish," who had spent the morning perfecting her signature gel polish designs on a string of satisfied customers.

As she carefully crafted a intricate nail art design on a client's pinky finger, Mia couldn't help but feel a sense of contentment wash over her. She loved her job, not just for the creative freedom it gave her to express herself through beauty, but also for the sense of connection she felt with each and every one of her clients.

The salon was a cozy, eclectic space filled with the sweet scent of nail polish and the soft hum of jazz music playing in the background. The walls were adorned with vintage mannequins dressed in 1950s-inspired outfits, and the floors creaked beneath Mia's feet as she moved around the salon, her long, curly brown hair tied up in a ponytail.

Just as Mia was finishing up a particularly tricky design on a client's ring finger, there was a knock at the door. It was Emily Wilson, a busy marketing executive who had stopped by to pick up a new pair of shoes for her upcoming wedding. As Mia handed Emily a fresh set of gel polish, she noticed that Emily seemed a bit...off.

"Hey Em, what's wrong?" Mia asked, her brow furrowing with concern as she took in the frazzled look on Emily's face.

Emily hesitated, glancing around the salon nervously before speaking in a hushed tone. "I just got engaged to my fiancé, but I'm feeling really anxious about the whole thing. He wants everything to be perfect, and I feel like we're not even compatible anymore."
