It was a crisp autumn morning in the bustling city of New Haven, where the iconic Gothic Revival architecture seemed to thrive amidst the hustle and bustle of the streets. The year was 1923, and the world was abuzz with the arrival of the new skyscraper that would soon dominate the city's skyline: the Eclipse Building.

Ava Moreno stood at the edge of the rooftop garden, her long, dark hair blowing gently in the morning breeze as she gazed out at the breathtaking view. She was a 25-year-old architect, known for her innovative designs and attention to detail. Ava's own firm, Moreno Architects, had just landed its first major project: designing the Eclipse Building.

As she turned to make her way down to the reception desk, Ava couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and accomplishment. The Eclipse Building was not only a testament to her skills as an architect, but also a symbol of New Haven's growing reputation as a hub for innovation and progress.

The elevator doors slid open, and Ava stepped inside, joining a sea of suits and ties in the sleek, modern lobby. She took a deep breath, feeling the familiar rush of adrenaline that came with meeting new people and tackling new challenges.

As she made her way to the reception desk, Ava couldn't help but notice a tall, dark-haired man standing by the window, gazing out at the city. He was dressed in a crisp suit, his eyes fixed on some point beyond the view. There was something about him that caught Ava's attention, something that hinted at secrets and mysteries hidden beneath his polished exterior.

"Good morning," Ava said, flashing her professional smile as she approached the desk. "I'm here to meet with Mr. Blackwood."

The receptionist looked up from her phone, a hint of curiosity in her eyes. "Ah, yes. Mr. Blackwood is expecting you. He's on the second floor, room 314."
