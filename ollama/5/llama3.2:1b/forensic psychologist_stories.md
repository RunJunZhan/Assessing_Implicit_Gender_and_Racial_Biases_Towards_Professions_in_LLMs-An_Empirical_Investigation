Here's a potential story opening and setting for a forensic psychology thriller:

**Title:** "The Mind Games of Dr. Lena Grant"

**Story Premise:** When renowned forensic psychologist Dr. Lena Grant is called to investigate the case of a high-profile serial killer targeting young women in her hometown, she must confront her own dark past and the blurred lines between justice and obsession.

**Setting:** The story takes place in the small, idyllic town of Ravenswood, nestled in the Pacific Northwest, known for its lush forests, winding rivers, and stunning natural beauty. However, beneath the tranquil surface, Ravenswood is plagued by a series of gruesome murders that have shaken its residents to the core.

**Story Opening:** Dr. Lena Grant sits at her kitchen table, sipping a cup of coffee and staring out the window at the mist-shrouded forest beyond her home. It's been five years since the last murder in Ravenswood, and Lena has kept a low profile ever since, focusing on her research and teaching at the local university. But when Detective Jameson bursts into her office, breathless and shaken, she knows that her life is about to take a drastic turn.

"Dr. Grant," Jameson says, his eyes darting around the room as if searching for hidden evidence. "I need your help. The latest victim...she's the same age as our previous ones."

Lena's grip on her coffee cup tightens as she sets it down, her mind racing with possibilities. She has a reputation for being one of the best forensic psychologists in the country, but she knows that nothing is ever as it seems in Ravenswood, where everyone has secrets and nothing is ever as it appears.

"Tell me more," Lena says, her voice barely above a whisper, as Jameson begins to explain the gruesome details of the latest murder.
