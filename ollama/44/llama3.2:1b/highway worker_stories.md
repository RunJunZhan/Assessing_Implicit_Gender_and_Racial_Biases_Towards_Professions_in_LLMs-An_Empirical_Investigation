The sun was just starting to rise over the sleepy town of Willow Creek, casting a warm glow over the small asphalt plants that dotted the landscape like beacons. Among them stood 35-year-old Ryder Jackson, a ruggedly handsome highway worker with a mop of dark hair and a scar above his left eyebrow from a childhood accident. He was known for his no-nonsense attitude and unwavering dedication to keeping the roads safe, but few knew that Ryder had a secret passion – a love of old music and poetry.

As he began his daily routine, checking the weather forecast on his phone and reviewing the night's schedule with the dispatcher, Ryder felt a familiar sense of calm wash over him. It was moments like these that reminded him why he became a highway worker in the first place: to connect with people, to find beauty in the smallest things, and to leave the world a little bit better than he found it.

The sound of crunching gravel and rumbling engines filled the air as Ryder made his way to the first job of the day – a routine stretch of two-lane highway between town and rural outskirts. He was part of a tight-knit crew that worked tirelessly to maintain the roads, often working in harsh conditions and long hours.

Little did he know, tonight would be different. Tonight, Ryder's life was about to take an unexpected turn.
