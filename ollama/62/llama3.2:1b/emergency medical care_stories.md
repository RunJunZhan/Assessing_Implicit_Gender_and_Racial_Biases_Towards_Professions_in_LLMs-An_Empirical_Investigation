The fog rolled in off the Pacific like a damp shroud, casting a grayish hue over the small coastal town of Ravenswood. It was a typical Wednesday morning in late summer, with the kind of weather that made you want to stay inside and forget about the world outside. But for Dr. Maya Singh, a seasoned ER nurse at the local hospital, it meant one thing: another day, another batch of emergencies waiting to be addressed.

Maya stood at the window of her small apartment, gazing out at the misty streets as she sipped her coffee and checked her phone for any messages from her crew. She was 32 years old, with a shaved head and piercing brown eyes that seemed to hold a thousand worries already etched on their face. She had worked in emergency medicine for five years, ever since her younger brother had been saved by the same kind of medical care she provided now.

As she turned away from the window, Maya's thoughts turned to the chaos that was about to erupt outside. The hospital was about to fill up with frantic patients, and Maya was ready – or at least, as ready as anyone could be when you've seen it all before. She took a deep breath, mentally preparing herself for another long day of saving lives.

Little did she know, tonight would bring something different. Something that would test her skills, her judgment, and her very soul.
