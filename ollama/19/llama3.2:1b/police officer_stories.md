It was a chilly autumn evening in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of rural Massachusetts. The sun had just set, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. Detective Augustus "Augie" Thompson sat at his desk, sipping on a cup of cold coffee, staring out the window at the dense forest that surrounded the town. It was a typical Friday night in Oakdale, with the usual assortment of residents filtering home from their day jobs.

Augie, a seasoned police officer in his late 40s, had lived in Oakdale all his life. He knew its rhythms and secrets better than anyone. As he gazed out into the darkness, he couldn't shake off the feeling that something was off. A sense of unease had been building up inside him all day, ever since he received a call from the local diner about a possible disturbance at the old abandoned mill on the outskirts of town.

The mill, once a bustling hub of industry and commerce, now stood as a crumbling monument to the town's industrial past. Its towering structure loomed over the surrounding landscape, casting long shadows that seemed to stretch and twist in the fading light. Augie's gut told him that this case was going to be different from any others he'd handled before - there was something more sinister at play, something that required his unique set of skills and experience as a detective.

With a determined look on his face, Augie reached for his phone and dialed the number of the Oakdale Police Department's dispatch center. "Dispatch, this is Detective Thompson," he said into the receiver. "We've got a possible disturbance at the old mill. Requesting backup."
