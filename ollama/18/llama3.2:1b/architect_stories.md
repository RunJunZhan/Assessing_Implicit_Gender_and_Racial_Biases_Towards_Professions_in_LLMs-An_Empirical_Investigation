It was a crisp autumn morning in New Haven, Connecticut, as Maya Singh stepped out of her sleek, silver car and onto the sun-kissed streets of the city's historic district. Her eyes, a deep shade of indigo, scanned the crowded sidewalk, taking in the vibrant murals and bustling cafes that seemed to come alive with the arrival of the new school year.

Maya, a 35-year-old architect by training and owner of her own design firm, Maya Singh & Associates, was already making her way through the throngs of students gathered outside the prestigious Yale University Art Gallery. Her dark hair was pulled back into a tight ponytail, revealing her sharp cheekbones and piercing green eyes that seemed to bore into those she met.

As a leading expert in sustainable architecture, Maya had earned a reputation for pushing the boundaries of innovative design, and her firm's latest project – a cutting-edge eco-friendly campus for Yale University – was set to be unveiled at the gallery's grand opening ceremony later that day. But as she navigated through the sea of students, Maya couldn't shake off the feeling that something was amiss. She had received a cryptic message from an unknown source earlier that morning, hinting at a potential design flaw in one of her firm's latest projects...
