It was a warm summer evening in August when Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stepped off the plane at New York's John F. Kennedy International Airport, her eyes scanning the bustling terminal as she collected her luggage and made her way to the baggage claim area.

Lexi had been working as a flight attendant for over five years, but she still felt like a kid on her first day of work every time she donned her crisp white uniform and grabbed her clipboard. As one of the newer attendants at Delta Airlines, Lexi was determined to make a good impression on her colleagues and passengers alike.

She had grown up in the small town of Oakdale, Illinois, where everyone knew each other's names and the local diner served as the community hub. After graduating from high school, Lexi had gone on to study hospitality management at the University of Illinois, where she honed her skills in customer service and learned how to handle even the most delicate situations with poise.

Now, as she made her way through the airport, Lexi felt a sense of excitement and nervousness. She was about to embark on her first long-haul flight, flying from New York to Los Angeles, and she had been assigned to fly a plane full of rowdy college students home for break. It wouldn't be an easy ride – but Lexi was ready for the challenge.

As she settled into her pre-flight briefing with her captain, Lexi couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and purpose. She knew that today would be just another day on the job, but she also knew that it was going to be a day she would never forget – at least, not until the end of her flight.
