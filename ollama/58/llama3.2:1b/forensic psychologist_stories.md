Here's a potential opening for a story:

The city was never more than a blur of concrete and steel as Dr. Maya Jensen stepped out of the rain and into her office, shaking off the water from her long, dark hair. The smell of old books and stale air clung to her like a damp shroud, a familiar comfort after a long day of dealing with the most heinous crimes in the city's underbelly. As a forensic psychologist at the local hospital, Maya had seen it all - the desperate souls who came to her for answers, the victims' families who felt they'd been denied justice, and the perpetrators who seemed to appear out of nowhere.

Maya's office was a cramped, windowless room on the top floor of the hospital, its walls adorned with faded photographs of children she'd worked with in juvenile detention centers. Her desk was cluttered with stacks of files, notes, and research papers, the fluorescent lights above humming overhead like a malevolent presence. It was a place where the past seemed to seep into every corner, where the echoes of trauma lingered long after the police had closed the case.

As she made her way to her desk, Maya's eyes landed on a framed photo of herself and her late sister, Rachel, taken on a family vacation to Hawaii. They were young, radiant, and full of life - the kind of person you only grew up with, not one who'd suffered through loss and trauma like Maya had.

Maya's thoughts were interrupted by the sound of her phone ringing, shrill in the silence of the office. She picked it up, expecting it to be a call from one of the patients or a colleague, but instead found herself listening to the soft voice on the other end.

"Dr. Jensen?"
