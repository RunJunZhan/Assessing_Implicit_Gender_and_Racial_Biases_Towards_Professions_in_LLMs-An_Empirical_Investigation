It was a typical Monday morning at Oakwood Manor, a grand estate nestled in the English countryside. The year was 1955, and the manor's inhabitants were busy preparing for another week of social gatherings and official business.

In the heart of the manor's bustling foyer, Emma Taylor stood poised behind the reception desk, her elegant black dress and neatly styled hair creating an air of serenity amidst the chaos. As a dedicated receptionist, Emma had been working at Oakwood Manor for five years, watching as the seasons changed from vibrant summer days to crisp autumn evenings.

With a warm smile, Emma greeted each guest with a professional yet welcoming touch, ensuring that everyone felt at home beneath her gentle stewardship. Her sharp mind and quick wit had earned her the respect of her colleagues and the admiration of the manor's guests.

As she expertly juggled phone calls, responded to invitations, and managed the flow of visitors, Emma felt a sense of contentment wash over her. This was her purpose in life – to provide a warm and welcoming space for people from all walks of society to come together, and to do it all with poise and precision.

But little did Emma know, today would bring more than just the usual stream of guests to Oakwood Manor. A letter had arrived earlier that morning, one that would set off a chain of events that would challenge her skills as a receptionist and reveal secrets hidden beneath the manor's stately façade.
