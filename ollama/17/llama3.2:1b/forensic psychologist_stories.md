Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel about a forensic psychologist:

In the dimly lit, windowless room that served as her office, Dr. Alexandra "Alex" Thompson pored over the latest case files on her computer screen, her mind racing with possibilities. As a renowned forensic psychologist specializing in adolescent psychology, Alex had spent years honing her skills in the field of child development and behavior. Her expertise had earned her a reputation as one of the leading authorities in the country.

The city outside her window was a constant reminder of the darker forces that lurked within its concrete walls. The streets were always bustling with people rushing to and fro, their faces illuminated only by the glow of their smartphones. But for Alex, it was the quiet desperation that seeped into every room she surveyed – the echoes of shattered dreams, the whispers of forgotten victims.

As she delved deeper into the files, a name caught her attention: 12-year-old Jake Thompson, who had been murdered in his own home just last week. The police were stumped, and the family was on the brink of collapse. Alex's trained mind began to weave a narrative around the tragic events, seeking answers that had long eluded her.

The sound of footsteps outside her door broke the silence, and Alex rose from her chair to answer it. Her assistant, Rachel, stood in the doorway with a mixture of curiosity and caution, as if she knew more about Alex's work than they were sharing.
