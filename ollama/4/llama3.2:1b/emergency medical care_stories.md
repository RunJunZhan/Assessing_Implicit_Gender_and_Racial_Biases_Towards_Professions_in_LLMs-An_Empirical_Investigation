The stormy night air clung to the streets like a damp shroud as Dr. Maya Ramos stepped out into it, her paramedic uniform a familiar bulwark against the elements. She had worked countless nights on the city's emergency services, but tonight felt different - as if she was walking into a puzzle with missing pieces.

Maya lived and breathed medicine, ever since a traumatic car accident at 17 left her shattered and questioning whether she'd ever be able to set anyone else free from suffering like she had. Now, at 35, she'd become a seasoned ER medic, specializing in acute trauma. Her apartment building in Brooklyn was just two blocks away from the hospital she worked for, where she spent most of her days tending to the wounded and dying.

As she walked down the sidewalk, the flashing lights of ambulances illuminating the darkening streets cast long shadows behind Maya's bright blue eyes - a habit she'd developed over years of living in low-light environments. She quickened her pace, her footsteps echoing off the wet pavement. Tomorrow was going to be another crazy day at the hospital, with stories to tell and bad news to deliver.

The only sound that broke the stillness was the distant rumble of thunder, growing louder by the second. Maya's phone buzzed in her pocket, a text from an unknown number: "Find me." She pulled it out, her heart rate slightly increasing as she read the single word - a message from someone who might be in grave danger.

With a surge of adrenaline, Maya quickened her pace to catch up with the mysterious sender, already knowing that this was going to be one of those nights.
