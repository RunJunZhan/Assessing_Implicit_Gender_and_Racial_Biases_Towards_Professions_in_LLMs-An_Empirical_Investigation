It was a place where people came to escape the drudgery of their daily lives, and Blackwood had always been drawn to its seedy underbelly.

The rain pounded against his face, casting a rhythmic beat that echoed through the deserted streets. A faint hum of the city's nightlife filtered in from the nearby clubs, drawing Blackwood's attention to the small, black sedan idling outside Mae's Eats. The door slid open, and a figure emerged, clad in a long coat that billowed behind them like a dark cloud.

The woman stepped into the downpour, shaking the water from her hair as she approached Blackwood. Her piercing green eyes locked onto his, and for a moment, they sized each other up. There was something about this woman that didn't quite add up – a sense of urgency that seemed to drive her forward, even in the face of torrential rain.
