Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel:

Professor Emilia "Mia" Thompson stood at the edge of the mist-shrouded campus, her eyes fixed on the towering spires of Ashwood University's historic quadrangle. The autumn air was crisp and cool, carrying the scent of ripe fruit and damp earth, and Mia felt a sense of peace wash over her as she breathed it in. It had been five years since she'd returned to her alma mater after a decade-long absence, and yet the campus still seemed to hold secrets and whispers of its own.

Mia was a professor of mythology at Ashwood, a position she'd held for nigh on ten years. She taught courses on ancient cultures and legends, and had established herself as one of the leading experts in her field. As she gazed out at the misty dawn, Mia felt a sense of purpose wash over her – another day, another chance to share her passion with the next generation of students.

But despite her professional success, Mia's personal life was far from stable. Her marriage had ended three years prior, and she'd been struggling to find love again. As she turned away from the quadrangle, her eyes fell on the familiar sight of the university's chapel, its stained glass windows casting kaleidoscopic patterns across the stone floor.

The story can unfold from here, exploring Mia's relationships with her colleagues and students, her own personal demons, and the secrets that still linger in Ashwood University.
