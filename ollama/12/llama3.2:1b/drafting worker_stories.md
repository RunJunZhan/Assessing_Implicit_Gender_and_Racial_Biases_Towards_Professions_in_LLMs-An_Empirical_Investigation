It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of rural America. The sun was just starting to rise over the horizon, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint Main Street. In a small office tucked away on Elm Street, 25-year-old Jake Thompson sat hunched over his drafting table, staring blankly at the computer screen in front of him.

Jake's company, Thompson Drafting Co., was a family-owned business that had been around for decades, specializing in designing and building custom homes and buildings. Jake's father had founded the company when he was just a young man, and now Jake was following in his footsteps, working alongside his parents to keep the business thriving.

As he tapped away at the keyboard, Jake's mind wandered back to the long hours he'd spent as a child helping out on the farm with his brothers. He remembered the feel of the cool dew on his skin after a morning of riding horses and fixing fences. But now, as a drafting worker, Jake was stuck inside most days, staring at spreadsheets and computer screens instead of feeling the wind in his hair or the sun on his face.

Despite the monotony of his job, Jake loved working with machines and drawing designs. He took pride in his work, making sure every house and building he designed was perfect. But as much as he enjoyed his job, Jake couldn't shake the feeling that something was missing. He felt like there was more to life than just numbers and lines on a computer screen. Little did he know, this morning would bring him an opportunity to explore the world outside of Thompson Drafting Co. and discover a new passion that would change everything.
