It was a crisp autumn morning in the bustling city of New Haven, where the smell of freshly baked pastries wafted from the local bakery on every corner. Amidst the vibrant streets and eclectic shops, one building stood out - the medical district of Oakwood Hospital, home to the esteemed St. Mary's Medical Center. Within its walls, a young professional was about to embark on a day that would challenge her skills and test her patience.

Ava Morales, a 25-year-old phlebotomist at St. Mary's, adjusted her crisp white lab coat as she made her way through the crowded hallways. With dark hair tied back in a ponytail and a gentle smile, Ava exuded a sense of professionalism that commanded respect from even the most skeptical patients. Her worn sneakers and comfortable jeans hinted at the long hours and late nights that lay ahead.

As she entered the bustling phlebotomy laboratory, Ava's eyes scanned the room for her patient of the day - a middle-aged businessman with a stern expression who waited anxiously in the reception area. The soft hum of fluorescent lights overhead illuminated the rows of workstations, where a steady stream of patients whizzed by, their veins being prepared for blood draws. Ava took a deep breath, mentally preparing herself for another long and demanding day ahead.
