It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of rural Pennsylvania. The streets are alive with the sound of hay wagons rattling down Main Street, and the sweet scent of apple cider wafts through the air as the townspeople begin their daily routine. At the local meat market, "Bull & Barrel," owned by the enigmatic and hardworking Henry Everett, the sun rises over the butcher shop's wooden sign, creaking in the gentle breeze.

Inside, Henry is already preparing for another busy day ahead. He carefully sorts through the day's shipment of fresh beef, selecting the finest cuts for his customers' meals. The dimly lit shop is warm and cozy, filled with the comforting aromas of curing meats and simmering stews. A soft hum of conversation and clinking knives fill the air as Henry expertly trims and wraps the morning's offerings.

As he works, Henry's thoughts turn to the upcoming harvest festival, which will be held in a week's time. He's been tasked with creating an exquisite beef tenderloin for the main course, something that will impress even the most discerning food critics from the city. Little does he know, however, that this year's harvest season has been marked by a series of unexplained occurrences – tools gone missing, strange noises in the dead of night, and an eerie feeling that settles over the town like a shroud.

Henry Everett is a man in his mid-40s, with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes that seem to see right through you. His dark hair is always perfectly styled, and he has a way of moving that's both confident and effortless. Despite his polished demeanor, however, there's a sense of quiet intensity about him – a feeling that he's carrying a weighty secret, one that could change everything he thought he knew about Willow Creek and its people.
