The mist-shrouded streets of New Haven were always at their most unforgiving in the early morning hours, but for 25-year-old Guard Elijah Stone, it was a familiar and comforting feeling. As he stepped out of the narrow alleyway where he'd spent the previous night huddled with his fellow guards, the chill of the dawn air slapped him awake. The city's imposing stone walls rose up on either side, their battlements weathered to a soft gray that seemed to absorb the light rather than reflect it.

Elijah rubbed the sleep from his eyes and gazed out at the sea of dark shapes that stretched across the rooftops. He was a guard at the heavily fortified New Haven Citadel, one of the most secure positions in the city-state. His duties were simple: keep the streets safe, enforce the laws, and maintain order whenever disputes arose. It wasn't a life Elijah would have chosen for himself, but it had been his family's for generations.

As he made his way to the Citadel's central courtyard, where his fellow guards were already gathered around a large map spread out on a stone table, Elijah felt a familiar sense of routine wash over him. He had been a guard for five years now, and yet everything still felt just as it had when he first joined the Citadel - the same tired routines, the same weary eyes, the same unspoken understanding with his fellow guards that they all shared one thing: a deep-seated suspicion that something was always just out of sight.
