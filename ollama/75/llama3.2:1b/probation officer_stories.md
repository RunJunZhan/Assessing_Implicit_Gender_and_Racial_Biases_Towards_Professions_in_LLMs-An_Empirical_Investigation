The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. But for 25-year-old Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson, the peaceful evening was overshadowed by a sense of unease. Lexi stood outside the office building where she worked as a probation officer, her eyes fixed on the familiar facade as if waiting for something to happen.

It had been six months since she'd graduated from law school and taken the job at the Willow Creek Probation Department. She had always dreamed of making a difference in people's lives, helping them turn their lives around after they'd messed up. Now, with a steady paycheck and a sense of purpose, Lexi was determined to prove herself as one of the best probation officers in the state.

As she gazed out at the office building, Lexi thought back to her early days on the job. She'd worked hard to earn the trust of the community, but it had been tough. The offenders were often hardened and uncooperative, and there were times when Lexi felt like giving up. But she refused to let their behavior define her. Instead, she chose to focus on helping them find their way back.

Lexi took a deep breath and stepped inside the building, her footsteps echoing off the walls. She had an early meeting with a young offender named Marcus, one of the first cases she'd worked on. Lexi's heart quickened as she made her way to the conference room, where Marcus was already seated, sipping on a cup of coffee.

"Hey, Lexi," he said without looking up. "Good timing."

Lexi smiled and took a seat across from him. "Good timing indeed. I'm here to give you some tough love."
