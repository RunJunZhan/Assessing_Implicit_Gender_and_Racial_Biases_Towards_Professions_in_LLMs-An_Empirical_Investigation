It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the rolling hills of the countryside. The air was alive with the sweet scent of ripe pumpkins and the gentle rustle of leaves as the trees prepared for their annual harvest festival. Amidst this picturesque backdrop, 25-year-old Ethan Ryder stood at the edge of his family's rural estate, taking a deep breath as he surveyed the grounds. His worn jeans and scuffed work boots seemed out of place among the immaculately manicured lawns and vibrant floral arrangements that dotted the landscape.

As a groundskeeper for the esteemed Oakdale Manor, Ethan had grown up learning the intricacies of gardening, horticulture, and outdoor maintenance from his father, who still worked tirelessly to keep the estate's 500 acres running smoothly. The Ryders had lived in Oakdale their entire lives, and Ethan felt a deep sense of pride and responsibility as he tended to the land that was both his family's legacy and a source of comfort for him.

As he began to inspect the gardens, a gentle tap on his shoulder made him turn to find his best friend, Jake Thompson, a rugged outdoorsman with a quick smile and a bushy beard, standing behind him. "Morning, Ethan! I see you're getting started early as usual," Jake said, eyeing the scattered tools and equipment that filled the shed.
