It was a warm summer evening in the small coastal town of Tidal Cove, where the sun had just dipped below the horizon and painted the sky with hues of orange and pink. The air was alive with the sweet scent of saltwater taffy and the sound of seagulls crying overhead. Amidst this picturesque backdrop, 25-year-old bartender, Jesse "JD" Davenport, polished a mug with a white towel, his eyes scanning the bustling bar as he took a deep breath to calm his nerves.

JD's small town had always been tight-knit, and Tidal Cove was no exception. The locals knew everyone, from the gruff fishermen who came in for a cold beer after a long day on the water, to the families who settled here years ago, seeking a quieter life by the sea. JD had grown up in Tidal Cove, learning the ins and outs of running his family's bar, "The Cove", since he was old enough to pour a drink.

As he worked, JD's mind wandered back to the summer he'd spent working at The Cove as a teenager, listening to the tales of its owner, Jack Harris. Jack had been more than just a boss; he was a mentor, a friend, and a guardian angel to the town. His legacy lived on through JD, who carried on his work with a passion that rivaled Jack's own.

Just then, a group of rowdy tourists stumbled into The Cove, laughing and joking as they clinked glasses together. JD recognized some of them from the beach volleyball matches he'd organized for the local youth. They were here to celebrate their friend's birthday, and they were determined to make it one to remember. JD smiled wryly to himself; this was going to be an interesting night.
