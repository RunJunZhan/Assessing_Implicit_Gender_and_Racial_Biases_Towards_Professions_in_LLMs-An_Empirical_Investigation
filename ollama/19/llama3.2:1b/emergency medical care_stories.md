It was a chilly autumn evening in the small town of Oakdale, where 25-year-old Alexandra "Alex" Thompson had just finished her shift at Oakdale Medical Center. The hospital's doors were closing, and Alex was about to leave when she received a distress call from one of the ER nurses. A young mother, Sarah Jenkins, had been driving home from a late-night event when her car skidded off the road and into a nearby lake.

Alex arrived at the scene in her sleek black ambulance, sirens blaring as she rushed towards the hospital's emergency entrance. As she stepped out of the vehicle, the crisp night air greeted her, carrying the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves. A faint cry for help came from within the ER building, where Alex knew she would find Sarah.

As she made her way through the hospital's sliding glass doors, Alex was greeted by the warm glow of fluorescent lights and the hum of activity. The ER staff was already responding to the scene, tending to Sarah's shaken form. Alex flashed a reassuring smile as she approached the nurse who had called for help, her eyes scanning the chaotic atmosphere with a mix of concern and professionalism.
