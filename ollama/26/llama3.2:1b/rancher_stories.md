It was a crisp autumn morning on the vast ranchland of Red Rock Valley, Arizona, where the sun rose over the rolling hills and dusty canyons, casting a golden glow over the ranch's sprawling herd of cattle and horses. The year was 1885, and Jack McKenzie, a rugged and weathered rancher in his late 30s, sat on the porch of his family's old homestead, sipping coffee and watching as the mist rose from the valley below.

Jack had grown up on this land, learning the ins and outs of cattle ranching from his father and grandfather before him. He was a hardworking man with a fierce determination in his eyes, always putting the needs of his family and livestock above all else. As he rocked gently back and forth on the porch step, Jack's thoughts turned to the upcoming market in town, where he would be selling some of the ranch's finest beef to hungry customers.

The ranch had been in the McKenzie family for generations, and Jack took great pride in its reputation as one of the top producers of high-quality beef in the region. He knew every inch of this land, every creek and hill, and every cattle bugle that could be used to keep his herd healthy and happy. But despite his success on the ranch, Jack couldn't shake off the feeling that something was off. A sense of unease had been building inside him for weeks, ever since he'd received a cryptic letter from an old friend back East, hinting at a big change on the horizon.

As Jack stood up to stretch his arms and take in one last look at the valley before heading out to the ranch, a loud rumble shook the ground beneath his feet. The skies darkened as a storm was brewing, and Jack knew that this could be the beginning of something much bigger – or much worse.
