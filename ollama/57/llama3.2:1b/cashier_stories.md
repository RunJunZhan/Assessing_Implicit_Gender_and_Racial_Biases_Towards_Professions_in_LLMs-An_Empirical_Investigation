It was a typical Wednesday afternoon at the local supermarket, with the sounds of chatter and the rustle of bags filling the air. Behind the counter, Rachel Jenkins expertly scanned item after item, her fingers moving quickly as she tried to keep up with the demanding pace of the store.

Rachel was just 22 years old, but she had been working as a cashier at the supermarket for over three years. She had grown up in this small town and had always loved shopping at the local market, so it made sense that she would end up behind the counter ringing up groceries for her neighbors and friends.

As she worked, Rachel's mind began to wander back to the summer she was turning 22. She had spent her carefree days at the beach with her best friend, Emily, trying out new beach volleyball teams and getting tan lines that lasted long after the sun went down. They had laughed and joked, and for a brief moment, it felt like they were invincible.

But life had taken its toll on Rachel since then. She had dropped out of college to focus on her part-time job and her own small business venture – a small bakery she had started in her parents' garage. The bakery had been a hobby, but now that summer was over, it seemed like a distant memory.

As the afternoon wore on, the store began to wind down. Rachel finished scanning one last customer's groceries before calling out a thank you and packing up her register. She took a deep breath, feeling a mix of excitement and nerves as she prepared for the long night ahead.
