It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of rural Illinois. The sun rose over the rolling hills and fields of golden wheat, casting a warm orange glow over the sleepy community. In a small trailer on the outskirts of town, Jack "Hawk" Hawkins stirred awake to the sound of his alarm blaring in his ear. He rubbed the sleep from his eyes and swung his legs over the side of the bed, planting his feet firmly on the ground.

Jack had lived in Oakdale all his life, working for the local highway department as a maintenance worker since he was a teenager. He knew every nook and cranny of the roads, every pothole and curve, and every section of pavement that needed repairing. Jack took pride in his work, knowing it was what kept the town running smoothly.

As he made his way to the kitchen, Jack's stomach growled with anticipation. He had spent a long night on the job, fixing a crack in the guardrail on Highway 41. The smell of freshly brewed coffee wafted from the kitchen, and Jack couldn't wait to start his day.

"Good morning, Jack!" his wife, Sarah, called out as she poured him a cup of black coffee. "How's the road coming along?"

"Just need to get this section fixed," Jack replied, taking a sip of the hot brew before heading outside to tackle the task at hand.
