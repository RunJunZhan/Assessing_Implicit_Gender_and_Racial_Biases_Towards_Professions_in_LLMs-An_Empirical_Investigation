Here's a potential start to a story:

The sun was just starting to peek over the sleepy town of Ashwood, casting a warm glow over the quaint Main Street. The smell of freshly baked bread wafted from the nearby bakery, enticing the early risers with its sweet aroma. Among the crowd of sleepy townsfolk, one person stood out - Emrys "Mick" McCormack, proprietor of "Mick's Meat Market", a family-owned butcher shop that had been a staple in Ashwood for generations.

As Mick prepared for another busy day ahead, he couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and ownership over his business. He had learned the trade from his father before him, and had passed it down to his own son, Finn. But now that Finn was off at college, Mick found himself struggling to keep up with the demands of running the shop on his own. The phones were ringing nonstop, and the meat prices were getting pricier by the day.

Just as Mick was starting to lose hope, a ruggedly handsome stranger strode into his shop, sending a jolt of electricity through the air. He was dressed in worn leather and had a look of quiet confidence about him that made Mick's instincts perk up. "Mick McCormack," he said, his deep voice husky as he wiped his hands on a grease-stained apron. "I've heard a lot about your shop."

Mick raised an eyebrow, intrigued by the stranger's presence in Ashwood. Who was this mysterious visitor, and what did they want with him? He leaned against the counter, eyeing the stranger warily as he scanned the shelves of cured meats, his gaze lingering on the array of succulent sausages and tender steaks.
