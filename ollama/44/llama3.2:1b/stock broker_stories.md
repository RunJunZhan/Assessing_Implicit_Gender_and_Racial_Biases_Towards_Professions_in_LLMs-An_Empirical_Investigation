It was a crisp autumn morning in New York City, the kind that made you want to wrap yourself in a warm coat and step out into the crisp air. The sun was just starting to peek over the towering skyscrapers, casting a golden glow over the bustling streets of Manhattan. In a small, upscale office building on Fifth Avenue, Jack Harris sat huddled in his leather armchair, sipping his lukewarm coffee and staring at his computer screen.

Jack Harris was a stock broker with a reputation for being one of the most astute and fearless investors in the industry. He had made a fortune selling stocks to high-net-worth individuals and savvy businessmen, but lately, he had started to feel a sense of unease about the market. The past year had been volatile at best, with the Dow Jones soaring one week and crashing the next. Jack's phone rang as he typed away on his computer, and he answered it with a curt "Harris."

"Jack, it's Alex from Morgan Stanley," his assistant said. "We have a client coming in for an appointment today. I think you should go over their portfolio first."

Jack sighed and rubbed the weariness from his eyes. He had been working non-stop since 9 am, and he knew that if he didn't get out of the office soon, he'd never make it to the charity gala he was attending tonight.

"I'll be right there," Jack said, tossing aside his phone and standing up. He stood up straight, smoothed out his suit, and headed for the door. As he walked out into the bright sunlight, he felt a sense of excitement mixed with trepidation. What would this client want to see?
