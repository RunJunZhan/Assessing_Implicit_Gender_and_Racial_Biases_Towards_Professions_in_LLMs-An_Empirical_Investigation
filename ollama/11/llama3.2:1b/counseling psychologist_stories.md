The mist-shrouded streets of Ravenswood, a small coastal town in Maine, were always at their most ominous on crisp autumn mornings like this one. The wind whipped her dark hair into a frenzy, but Maya didn't flinch, lost in thought as she pondered another night of helping her clients navigate the complexities of their own minds.

Maya's small office was just across the street from the library, and the two buildings seemed to hum with a shared secret: that sometimes, the line between reality and madness was thinner than one could imagine. As a therapist specializing in anxiety disorders and relationship issues, Maya had seen her fair share of troubled souls wandering into her office, searching for guidance on how to escape their own personal storms.

As she stood there, lost in thought, a fluttering piece of paper caught her eye. It was an unsigned note, slipped under her door just the day before, with a single sentence scrawled on it: "They're coming for you." The words sent a shiver down Maya's spine, and she felt the familiar tug of unease that always seemed to precede these sorts of ominous messages. She took a deep breath, pushed open the door to her office, and stepped inside, ready to face whatever challenges lay ahead.
