It was a crisp autumn evening in the small town of Red Rock, nestled between the dusty roads of Arizona and the vast expanse of the Sonoran Desert. The sun had just dipped below the horizon, casting a warm orange glow over the town's Main Street, where the sounds of sizzling burgers and lively chatter filled the air. This was home for Jack "Hawk" Hawkins, a rugged trucker with a reputation for being one of the most reliable and hard-driving drivers on the highway.

Jack had spent his entire life behind the wheel, starting as a young boy when he first got his first semi-truck. He had worked his way up from driving for smaller companies to becoming a top-tier hauler for a major interstate carrier. With his trusty Peterbilt 389, and his loyal companion, a worn-out Chevy Blazer named Bertha, Hawk had seen it all – the ups and downs of the road, the joys of new friendships, and the occasional run-in with trouble.

As he pulled into Red Rock's long, curved rest stop, Jack could feel the rumble of Bertha's engine beneath him. He gave her a final check before climbing down to stretch his tired legs, his eyes scanning the deserted parking lot for any signs of trouble or troublemakers. The sun was setting over the desert now, casting a golden light over the town and its surroundings. It was going to be a long night, but Hawk was ready for whatever lay ahead.
