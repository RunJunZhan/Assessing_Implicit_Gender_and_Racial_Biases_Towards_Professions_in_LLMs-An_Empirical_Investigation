In the quaint town of Oakdale, nestled in the rolling hills of rural America, 25-year-old landscaping contractor Ethan Walker had grown up surrounded by the very plants and trees he spent his days cultivating. He had learned the trade from his father, who had learned it from his father before him, and now Ethan was determined to make a name for himself as one of the most respected landscapers in the region.

Ethan's small landscaping business, Walker Landscaping, had been a staple in Oakdale for years, but he knew that times were changing. The rise of big-box stores and chain nurseries had left many small businesses like his struggling to stay afloat. Undeterred, Ethan was focused on perfecting his craft, experimenting with new techniques and designs to stand out from the crowd.

On this particular morning, Ethan stood in his freshly painted shop, surveying the landscape before him. The sun was shining down on the neatly trimmed hedges, and the scent of fresh cut grass wafted through the air as he began to prepare for the day's work. A thick layer of new mulch had just been spread over the lawn, and a team of workers was busy digging trenches for irrigation systems.

As Ethan checked his schedule, he noticed a strange package waiting for him in the back office. It was an unmarked box with no return address, but something about it seemed off. He hesitated for a moment before deciding to open it, curious about what could be inside...
