It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of rural America. The sun was just starting to rise over the horizon, casting a warm orange glow over the sleepy streets and fields that stretched as far as the eye could see. In a small yard just outside the local diner, 35-year-old Jack "Hawk" Hawkins was already up and getting ready for another long day of work on Highway 12.

Jack had been a highway worker for over a decade, and he knew every nook and cranny of the road like the back of his hand. He had grown up in Oakdale, working alongside his father and grandfather before him, and it was a job that brought him a sense of pride and purpose. But despite the comforts of small-town life, Jack's mind often wandered to the big city, where he dreamed of exploring the world beyond these quiet streets.

As he finished getting dressed, Hawk's coworker, a grizzled old truck driver named Cletus, came walking into the yard, his massive frame towering over the other two men. "Morning, Hawk," Cletus said, ruffling the hair on Hawk's head. "You ready to hit the road?"

Hawk nodded, shouldering his bag and slinging it over his shoulder. "Yeah, I'm fixin' to."
