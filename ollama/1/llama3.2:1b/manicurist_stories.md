It was a crisp autumn morning in the charming town of Willow Creek, where the leaves on the oak trees rustled gently in the breeze as they prepared for another beautiful fall day. The sun shone brightly through the windows of Beauty Bliss Nail Spa, casting a warm glow over the bustling salon. Among the chaos of nail polish and clinking tools, one woman stood out - her name was Colette "Co" Thompson, a 25-year-old manicurist with a passion for beauty and a penchant for getting into the most dramatic dramas.

As she expertly shaped and filed the delicate nails of a distraught client, Co. (as she liked to be called by her friends) couldn't help but think that this was her dream job - creating stunning looks for women like her, who appreciated the art of pampering themselves in the quiet hours of the morning. The scent of lavender and vanilla wafted through the air as she worked, transporting Co. back to a happier time, one where she didn't have to worry about adulting, just nail art and happy clients.

But little did Co. know, today was going to be one of those days that would test her skills, patience, and sanity - all while trying to keep her cool in the face of a demanding client with a tangled web of expectations, ego, and what seemed like an endless supply of drama.
