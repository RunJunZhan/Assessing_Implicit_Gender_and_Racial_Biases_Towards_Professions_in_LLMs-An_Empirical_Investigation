It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains. The leaves on the trees were just starting to change colors, casting a warm orange and red hue over the quaint streets and homes. In the midst of this picturesque setting, Mrs. Adelaide "Addie" Thompson stood outside the doors of Oakdale High School, her worn leather briefcase slung over her shoulder. Addie was a guidance counselor at the school, a position she had held for over 20 years, and knew every student's name, story, and struggle. She took a deep breath, savoring the familiar scent of pine and damp earth that filled her lungs as she made her way to her office.

As she walked down the hallway, Addie passed by the bustling lunchroom, where students chatted with each other, laughing and joking like they were old friends. She smiled to herself, remembering when she was a student here, feeling just as eager to make new friends and navigate the challenges of high school. Now, as a guidance counselor, she had seen it all - the triumphs and tribulations, the struggles and successes.

Addie's office was a cozy room on the second floor of the school building, filled with shelves upon shelves of books, counseling materials, and memories. It was her sanctuary, where she could retreat from the demands of the day and simply be present for those who needed her most. She pushed open the door to her office, releasing a hint of familiar comfort into the hallway.

The first student Addie would see today was Emily Wilson, a junior who had just transferred to Oakdale High from a neighboring school in the city. Emily was a bright and ambitious student, with a promising future ahead of her. But as she walked into the office, Addie's eyes scanned Emily's disheveled appearance, and her heart sank. Something wasn't quite right...
