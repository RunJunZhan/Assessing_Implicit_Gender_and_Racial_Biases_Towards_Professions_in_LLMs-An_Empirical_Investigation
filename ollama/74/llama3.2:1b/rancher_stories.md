The sun was just starting to rise over the rolling hills of the Pecos Valley Ranch, casting a warm orange glow over the sprawling estate. Ryder McCade stood at the edge of the property, his worn boots scuffling against the gravel driveway as he took in the sight before him. The air was crisp and cool, carrying the scent of fresh-cut grass and hay, and Ryder's stomach growled in anticipation of another long day ahead.

Ryder McCade was a man of simple tastes and hard work. He had spent his entire life running the Pecos Valley Ranch, passed down from his father to him, and he took pride in every aspect of the operation. From the daily herd management to the irrigation system, every detail mattered to Ryder. He was a rancher, not just a landowner.

As he gazed out at the vast expanse of grazing land, Ryder's thoughts turned to the challenges that lay ahead. The drought had been devastating to the local farmers and ranchers, and the water levels in the nearby reservoir were critically low. Ryder knew that this year would be one for the books – either a record-breaking crop or a disastrous one.

With a sense of determination, Ryder squared his shoulders and began to make his way across the property, already mentally preparing himself for the tasks ahead. It was going to be a long day, but Ryder McCade was ready for whatever came next.
