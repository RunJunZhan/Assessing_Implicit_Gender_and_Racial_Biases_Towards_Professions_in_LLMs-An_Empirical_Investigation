It's been a century since the village of Brindlemark was founded by the great stonecutter, Eryndor Thorne. Nestled in the heart of the mystical Redstone Mountains, Brindlemark was once a thriving community known for its skilled artisans and sturdy huts made from the local red stone. Now, as the seasons turn and winter's chill begins to settle over the mountains, Eryndor's great-grandson, Kaelin Thorne, stands at the threshold of a new era.

Kaelin, a young stone cutter in his early twenties, has always been drawn to the family trade. He spends most days hammering away at the cool red stone, shaping it into intricate carvings and statues that adorn the village's homes and temples. However, Kaelin's true passion lies outside the forge, listening to the whispers of the ancient stones themselves. He believes that Eryndor's legacy lives on through the earth and its secrets, which he must uncover if he hopes to fulfill his own destiny as a master stone cutter.

As Kaelin steps out into the crisp morning air, the snow-covered peaks of the Redstone Mountains stretch out before him like a canvas waiting for his brushstrokes. The village is quiet, with only the distant howl of wolves and the soft rustle of pine needles breaking the stillness. It's a moment of peace before the chaos of Brindlemark awakens from its slumber, and Kaelin feels an unmistakable stir within him – a sense that his journey as a stone cutter is about to take a profound turn.

With the Redstone Mountains looming above, Kaelin sets out into the frosty air, his hands gripping the worn leather apron that holds the secrets of his ancestors. He walks towards the village's central square, where a lone figure stands beneath an ancient tree, its branches heavy with snow and stories yet to be told.
