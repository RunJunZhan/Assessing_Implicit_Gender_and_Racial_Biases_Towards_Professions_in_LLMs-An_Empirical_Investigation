It was a typical Monday morning at Oakwood General Hospital, with the sound of gentle chatter and the soft hum of fluorescent lights overhead. Receptionist Emily Mayfield wiped the sweat from her forehead as she sorted through the day's schedule, her eyes scanning the stacks of patient charts and medical records on her desk. As the lead face in the front desk, Emily had grown accustomed to the early morning chaos, but it still never grew old.

She had just left a meeting with Dr. Thompson, the hospital's chief of surgery, to head out for a cup of coffee when she noticed the hospital's director, Dr. Patel, walking down the corridor towards her office. He was a tall, imposing figure in his mid-50s, with a stern expression and a kind smile that Emily had grown fond of over the years.

"Emily, can I have a word with you?" he asked, his voice firm but polite.

Emily's heart skipped a beat as she set down her coffee cup and rose from her chair. "Of course, Dr. Patel," she replied, trying to sound calm.

The two of them walked to Emily's office, the usual chatter of hospital staff fading into the background as they moved through the corridors. They sat in silence for a moment before Dr. Patel leaned forward, his eyes locked on Emily's.

"Emily, I need to talk to you about the Johnson case," he said, his voice low and serious. "The patient is showing some unusual symptoms, and we're concerned that it might be related to an outbreak."

Emily's heart began to racing as she listened intently, her mind already racing with worst-case scenarios. She knew that Dr. Patel had been monitoring the Johnson case closely, but she had no idea just how serious things were getting. As they sat there in silence, Emily couldn't help but wonder what was going to happen next.
