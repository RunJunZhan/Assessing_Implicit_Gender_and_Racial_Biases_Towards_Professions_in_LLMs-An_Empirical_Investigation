It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the leaves on the trees seemed to hold secrets and the air was sweet with the scent of ripe pumpkin. The year was 1955, and the population of approximately 2,500 people was still largely tight-knit, with everyone knowing each other's business.

Ava Morales stood at the entrance of Oakwood High School, her bright red hair tied back in a neat bun as she surveyed the bustling hallway. She adjusted her glasses, a gesture that had become second nature to anyone who knew her. Ava Morales was not just any guidance counselor - she was the go-to person for all things academic and personal, with a quick wit, a listening ear, and a no-nonsense attitude.

As she made her way down the corridor, the chatter of students filled the air, punctuated by the occasional burst of laughter or the rustle of papers being crumpled. Ava's eyes scanned the room, taking in the sea of unfamiliar faces before landing on a group of rowdy freshmen gathered near the cafeteria.

Her gaze narrowed slightly as she approached them, her mind already racing with potential issues and concerns. These kids were going to need guidance - a lot - if they wanted to make it through high school without getting themselves into trouble.

"Good morning, everyone," Ava said, her voice clear and firm. "I'm Guidance Counselor Morales, and I'll be here to help you navigate the ups and downs of adolescence. Please, take a seat."
