As she gazed out at the sea of smiling faces, Mae's mind wandered back to the long hours she'd spent coaching her team through countless practices, drills, and games. It was a job that had consumed her every waking moment for the past five years, and one that she loved in its own way. But as the coach of Oakdale's top-ranked girls' basketball team, Mae knew that this season would be different. This year, they were playing for more than just pride or bragging rights – they were fighting to make a deep run in the state tournament.

Mae took a deep breath, feeling the cool morning air fill her lungs as she surveyed the Oaks' makeshift practice court. Her players were scattered about, some practicing their jump shots, others running drills on the sidelines, while others hung back and chatted quietly among themselves. Mae smiled to herself – it was going to be a great day in Oakdale.

"Mae?" Emily said, her voice low and urgent. "I'm not sure if I can make it for the rest of practice. My ankle is killing me."

Mae knelt down beside Emily, examining the injury she had sustained during a particularly intense scrimmage the night before. She could see the concern etched on Emily's face, and she knew exactly what to do.

"Don't worry, Em," Mae said softly. "You're going to be just fine. We'll get you fixed up and back on the court in no time."

And with that, Mae Thompson, coach of the Oakdale Oaks, gave her team a reassuring smile, knowing that her players were in good hands – as long as she was around to guide them.
