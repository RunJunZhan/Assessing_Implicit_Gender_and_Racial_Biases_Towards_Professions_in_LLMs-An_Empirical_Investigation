In the dimly lit, smoke-filled backroom of the infamous Red Door nightclub, 22-year-old Luna "Lux" Valois adjusted her microphone stand, her bright pink hair styled in a messy bun as she waited for her next song to catch the attention of the rowdy crowd. As she gazed out into the sea of expectant faces, her gaze landed on the enigmatic manager, Marcus Blackwood, who stood off to one side, sipping a glass of amber liquid. His piercing green eyes seemed to bore into Lux's very soul as he gave her a subtle nod, signaling that it was time to begin.

The Red Door had been Lux's home for the past year, a haven where she could pour her heart and soul into her music without judgment or criticism. But with great success comes great pressure, and Lux knew that Marcus Blackwood was more than just a manager – he was a mentor, a confidant, and potentially something more.

As the clock ticked closer to the start of the set, Lux's nerves began to fray. She took a deep breath, letting the familiar melody of her song wash over her, and stepped into the spotlight, ready to take on the night and whatever lay ahead.
