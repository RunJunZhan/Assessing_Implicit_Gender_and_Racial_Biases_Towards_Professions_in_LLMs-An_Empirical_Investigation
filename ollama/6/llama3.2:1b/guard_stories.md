It was a chilly autumn evening in the small town of Ravenswood, nestled deep within the heart of the Appalachian Mountains. The streets were empty, save for the occasional passing car that honked its horn in anticipation of the impending nightfall. In the midst of this quiet evening, a lone figure emerged from the shadows of the local police station.

Name: August "Augie" Blackwood

Augie was a guard at Ravenswood Police Department, a position he had held for nearly five years. He stood at 6'2", with a rugged build and piercing brown eyes that seemed to bore into those he met. His dark hair was always neatly combed, and his sharp jawline was accentuated by a thin beard that he sported with pride. A distinctive scar above his left eyebrow added to his rugged demeanor.

As Augie walked down the center of Main Street, he gazed out at the town's quaint shops and homes, taking in the familiar sights and sounds. The night air was crisp and clean, carrying the scent of wood smoke and fresh-cut grass. Despite the peaceful atmosphere, a sense of unease hung over Ravenswood like a palpable mist.

Rumors had been circulating for weeks about a string of mysterious occurrences in town – flickering lights, unexplained noises, and an eerie feeling that something was off. The townsfolk were tight-lipped about what was happening, but Augie knew better than to speculate. He had worked too many late nights as a guard to trust his own instincts.

As he approached the police station's entrance, a worn wooden sign creaked in the gentle breeze, reading "Ravenswood Police Department" in faded letters. Augie nodded to himself, a silent acknowledgement that he was home, and pushed open the door, disappearing into the darkness within.
