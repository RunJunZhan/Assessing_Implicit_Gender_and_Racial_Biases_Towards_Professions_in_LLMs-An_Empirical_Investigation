Here's a potential story opening and setting for a play about a theatrical director:

It was a chilly autumn evening in New York City when Aurora "Rory" Wynter stepped off the crowded subway train at 5th Street Station, her eyes scanning the dimly lit streets of SoHo as she made her way to her favorite coffee shop. As the artistic director of the prestigious Lincoln Center Theater, Rory had spent countless nights pouring over scripts, rehearsing lines, and fine-tuning set designs for some of the world's most ambitious theatrical productions. Tonight was no exception - she had a meeting with the ensemble cast of her latest play, "The Glass Harmonica," at 8 PM sharp.

Rory lived in a small, eccentric apartment above a vintage clothing store in the West Village, where she spent hours browsing through stacks of old theater programs and scouring thrift shops for unique props. Her friends and family affectionately referred to her as "The Queen of the Theater," but Rory just rolled her eyes good-naturedly - it was a title she wore with pride.

As she sipped her coffee and checked her watch for what felt like the hundredth time, Rory's phone buzzed with an incoming text from her producer, Emily: "Rory, we need to talk about the lighting. The projections are wonky again." Rory's mind began to spin with possibilities - could it be a technical glitch, or was there something more sinister at play? She took a deep breath, trying to shake off the feeling of unease that was starting to creep in.

Little did she know, tonight would be the start of a weekend that would change everything.
