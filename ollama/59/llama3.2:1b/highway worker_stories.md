The sun had barely risen over the rolling hills of rural Tennessee when Jake "Hawk" Hawkins stepped out of his truck and onto the worn asphalt of County Road 12. The misty dawn air clung to him like a damp blanket, but Hawk was no stranger to this kind of weather. He'd spent countless mornings like this on the job, hauling heavy loads and navigating the endless stretches of rural road that crisscrossed his home state.

As he began his daily routine, Hawk's mind wandered back to the previous night's work. A section of new pavement had collapsed in a particularly high-traffic area, leaving him to deal with the mess and find replacement materials. He'd spent hours on the phone with the foreman at Smith Construction, trying to negotiate a solution that would get his crew back on track as quickly as possible.

Hawk's truck, a rugged Ford F-350 with a faded paint job and a worn seat, was parked by the side of the road, ready to roll. He tossed a few more tools into the bed and started the engine, feeling the familiar rumble of the V8 as he pulled out of his parking spot.

As he cruised down the road, Hawk's eyes scanned the landscape for any signs of trouble. He'd been working on County Road 12 for over a decade, and he knew these roads like the back of his hand. But you never knew when disaster might strike – or when someone would try to take advantage of the job that was already underway.

The GPS in Hawk's truck led him to the next scheduled stop: a small café along the side of the road, where his wife, Rachel, had left a note with some work orders and tips. As he pulled into the parking lot, Hawk felt a sense of gratitude for the warm meal and friendly chat he'd look forward to during his lunch break.

But little did Hawk know, today would be different – something was waiting for him on County Road 12, just beyond the café's parking lot...
