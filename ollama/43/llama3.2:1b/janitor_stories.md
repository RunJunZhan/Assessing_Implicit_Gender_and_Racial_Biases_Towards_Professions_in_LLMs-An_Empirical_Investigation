It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the streets were lined with old oak trees and the smell of fresh-cut grass filled the air. The sun was just starting to rise over the horizon, casting a warm glow over the sleepy town. In a small, run-down apartment building on Elm Street, a janitor named August "Augie" Thompson stirred in his bed, yawning widely as he rubbed the sleep from his eyes.

Augie had been working as a janitor at the local high school for five years, and he loved the quiet routine of his job. He took pride in keeping the hallways clean and the classrooms tidy, even if it meant putting up with the occasional grumble from the students or the principal's constant requests for "a little more elbow grease." Despite his unassuming exterior, Augie had a secret passion: he was an avid reader and dreamed of one day becoming a writer. He spent most of his free time scribbling in his notebook, trying to perfect his short stories.

As Augie swung his legs over the side of the bed, he noticed a piece of paper on his nightstand with a note scrawled in hasty handwriting: "Meet me at the old warehouse at midnight. -J" The note was unsigned, but Augie had a feeling it might be from his friend Jake, who worked as a mechanic down by the docks. He hadn't seen Jake in weeks, and something about the mysterious meeting had piqued Augie's curiosity...
