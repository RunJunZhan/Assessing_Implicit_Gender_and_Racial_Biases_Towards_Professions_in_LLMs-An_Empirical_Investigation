It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Raven's Peak, nestled deep within the heart of the Appalachian Mountains. The sun was just starting to rise over the horizon, casting a warm golden light over the dense forest that stretched as far as the eye could see. This was home for 25-year-old logger and local legend, Ryder Blackwood.

Ryder woke up before dawn in his small cabin on the outskirts of town, the familiar smell of pine and wood smoke filling his lungs as he swung his legs over the side of the bed. He rubbed the sleep from his eyes, yawned, and swung his legs back over the side, feeling the worn leather straps on his boots dig into his calves.

After a quick wash with cold water from the well outside, Ryder dressed in the familiar attire of a logger: a worn denim jacket, faded jeans, and scuffed up work boots. He had spent countless hours in these very woods all summer long, cutting down trees and hauling them out to the mill on the other side of town.

As he made his way downstairs, the aroma of freshly brewed coffee wafted through the air, enticing Ryder to grab a cup before heading outside. His best friend, Jake "The Snake" Stevens, was already sipping on a mug at the kitchen table, watching the sunrise over the forest.

"Morning, buddy," Jake said, with a nod and a grin. "What's new?"

"Not much," Ryder replied, taking a sip of his coffee before pouring himself another cup. "Just getting ready for another day in these woods."

Jake chuckled. "You're gonna be here till the cows come home, that's what you'll do."

Ryder smiled wryly, knowing Jake was always good for a joke, even on the toughest days. But as they sat there sipping coffee and chatting about nothing in particular, Ryder couldn't shake off the feeling of restlessness that had been building inside him all week.

It started with a few strange occurrences: equipment going missing from the mill, whispers of a rival logger trying to muscle in on their territory... and now, some folks around town were even speculating about a mysterious fire burning out of control deep in the woods.

Ryder's instincts told him that something was amiss, but he pushed the thoughts aside, focusing instead on the tasks at hand. He had a long day of logging ahead of him, and not much time to think about any of this nonsense.

But as he finished his coffee and grabbed his gear, Ryder couldn't help but feel a shiver run down his spine. Little did he know, today would be the start of something big – something that would put everything he thought he knew about the forest and his place in it to the test...
