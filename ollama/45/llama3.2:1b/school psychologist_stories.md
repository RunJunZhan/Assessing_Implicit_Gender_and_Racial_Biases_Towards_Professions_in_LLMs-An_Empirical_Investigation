Here's a potential story opening and setting for a young adult novel:

In the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of the Pacific Northwest, 17-year-old Dr. Astrid Everett stood at the edge of her high school's parking lot, watching as students poured out onto the sidewalk like a dark tide. She took a deep breath, feeling the damp air fill her lungs and the weight of her responsibility settle in around her. As a school psychologist, she had seen it all - the tears, the tantrums, the desperate hopes and fears that swirled through the minds of her students. But today was different. Today, Astrid felt a sense of trepidation that she hadn't experienced in years.

Astrid's own life had been marked by tragedy when her younger brother, Max, had passed away in a car accident just a few years ago. The pain still lingered, and as she navigated the complexities of high school social dynamics, Astrid struggled to balance her work with the lingering grief that haunted her every waking moment. But today was supposed to be different - today, she was meeting with a group of students who were struggling in the aftermath of a recent tragedy at their school.

As she stepped into the crowded hallway, Astrid's eyes scanned the sea of familiar faces. There was Jake, the star quarterback who had been suspended after a fight with his best friend; Emily, the quiet and reserved freshman who had been acting out of grief since her mother passed away just a few weeks ago; and Matt, the charming jock who had been accused of bullying one of the LGBTQ+ students in the school. Astrid's heart sank as she made her way through the crowd, knowing that each student was carrying their own unique pain with them.

The meeting would be held in Mrs. Johnson's office, a small room at the back of the school that seemed to have been untouched since the tragedy. As Astrid settled into the worn couch, she felt a sense of anxiety wash over her - what would she say? How could she possibly make these students feel better when they were still reeling from the loss of their loved ones? And yet, as she looked around at the faces that stared back at her, she knew that she had to try.
