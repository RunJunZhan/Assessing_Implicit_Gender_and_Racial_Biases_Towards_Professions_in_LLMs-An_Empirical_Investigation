It was a drizzly autumn evening in the bustling metropolis of New Haven, a city known for its rich history, vibrant arts scene, and, most notably, its endless supply of small-time crooks and con artists. The rain poured down on the cobblestone streets, casting a gloomy mist over the city's eclectic mix of old and new buildings.

In a cramped, smoke-filled office above a vintage clothing store in the trendy Bellwether District, Jack "The Ghost" Griffin adjusted his fedora and lit a cigarette, surveying the familiar landscape from his corner office. A private detective with a reputation for being able to navigate even the most treacherous of streets, Griffin had been hired by the enigmatic Lucy Wellesley, a wealthy socialite with a secret that could get her killed.

Lucy's full name was Lucinda Mae Wellesley, but she preferred to be known as the "Lady in Black" due to her reputation for being as elusive and mysterious as the dark fabric she wore. With piercing green eyes and raven-black hair, Griffin had seen his fair share of beautiful women in black, each with their own secrets waiting to be uncovered.

As he took a long drag on his cigarette, Griffin's earpiece crackled to life, and a voice from the back room whispered, "Griffin, we've got a job. Meet us at Club Europa on 5th Street."

The phone fell silent, leaving Griffin to wonder who was calling and what kind of trouble Lucy had gotten herself into this time.
