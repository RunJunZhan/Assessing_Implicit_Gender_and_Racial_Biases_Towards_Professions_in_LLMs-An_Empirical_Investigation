Here's a potential story opening and setting for an Emergency Medical Technician (EMT) novel:

The small town of Oakdale was bathed in a warm, golden light as the sun began to set on another long day. The streets were quiet, save for the occasional passing car or chirping of crickets, but it was clear that everyone was heading home after a busy day at work.

At Oakdale Emergency Medical Services (EMS), EMT Amy Johnson stood at the dispatch desk, sipping a lukewarm cup of coffee and scanning the radio frequencies for any new calls. She had been an EMT in Oakdale for five years, and it was a place she loved despite its small-town charm.

As she checked her watch for what felt like the hundredth time, Amy's radio crackled to life. " Dispatch, this is 23-1, we have a code 3 at 345 Main Street," a panicked voice said. Amy's heart skipped a beat as she grabbed her bag and headed out into the fading light.

The call was for a possible cardiac arrest on Main Street, just a block from where the Oakdale Elementary School was located. Amy arrived quickly, her lights flashing as she careened down the street in her squad car. She pulled up outside the house and leapt out, sprinting towards the door with her medical bag already clutched over her shoulder.

As she burst through the front door, Amy saw a frantic mom kneeling on the floor beside her 7-year-old son's bedside. "Amy! Oh, thank God you're here!" the mom exclaimed, tears streaming down her face.
