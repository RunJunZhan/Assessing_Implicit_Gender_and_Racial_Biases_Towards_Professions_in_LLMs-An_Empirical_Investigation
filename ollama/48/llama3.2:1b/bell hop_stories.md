It was a drizzly autumn evening in New Orleans, the kind that made you want to curl up with a thick cup of coffee and a good book. The historic streets of the French Quarter were coming alive with the sounds of jazz and laughter, but for 22-year-old Belle Fontaine, the only thing she was looking forward to was another night at work as the bellhop at the luxurious La Petite Orleans Hotel.

Belle had grown up in this city, learning the ins and outs of the hospitality trade from her mother, who still worked as a maître d'hôtel at the very hotel Belle now stood stationed outside. She had always taken pride in her role, ensuring that every guest felt like royalty as they made their way through the ornate hallways to their rooms. It wasn't just about serving drinks and answering phones; it was about creating an experience that would leave a lasting impression.

As she slipped on her crisp white uniform, the soft cotton fabric hugged her curves in all the right places. A silver name tag adorned with the hotel's logo dangled from around her neck, and Belle couldn't help but feel a thrill of excitement. This was her favorite part of the day – the first night of service for new guests, when she got to meet them and make their stay in New Orleans truly unforgettable.

She took a deep breath, smoothing out her hair as she began to make her way through the throngs of people streaming into the lobby. The scent of freshly brewed coffee wafted through the air, mingling with the sweet aroma of jasmine and sandalwood, transporting Belle to a place where time stood still – in this magical city that never slept.
