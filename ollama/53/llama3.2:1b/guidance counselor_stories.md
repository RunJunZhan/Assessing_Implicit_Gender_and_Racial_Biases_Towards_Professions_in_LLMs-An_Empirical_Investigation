It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of rural Michigan. The sun rose over the horizon, casting a warm glow over the quaint streets and homes, adorned with vibrant fall foliage. In the local high school, Oakdale High School, guidance counselor Emily Wilson stood outside her office, sipping on a cup of coffee as she gazed out at the sea of unfamiliar faces.

Emily had spent the better part of two decades working in education, but this was her first year as a guidance counselor at Oakdale. She had grown up in the same small town and knew its residents like the back of her hand, but this job required a different set of skills – understanding teenagers' struggles to find their place in the world. As she surveyed her surroundings, Emily felt a sense of nervousness wash over her. This was her first time working with a group of high school students, and she wasn't sure what to expect.

Just then, the door to her office burst open and a bright-eyed junior named Alex Thompson strode in, his backpack slung over his shoulder. "Hey, Em! I need to talk to you about the upcoming prom," he said, his eyes shining with excitement. Emily smiled and ushered him into her office, ready to tackle whatever challenges lay ahead for Oakdale High's guidance counselor.
