Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel featuring a roofer as the main character:

---

The sun had just begun to set over the small town of Oakdale, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint Main Street. The smell of freshly cut grass and baking bread wafted through the air, mingling with the sound of children's laughter and the clanging of hammer on metal as the local roofer, Jesse Everett, worked his trade.

Jesse was a fixture in Oakdale, known for his no-nonsense approach to roofing and his ability to get any job done right. He had grown up in the town, learned to fix roofs from his father, and never intended to leave. But after a painful divorce and a string of unfulfilling jobs on the outside, Jesse decided it was time to stick around and make Oakdale his home.

As he worked, Jesse's mind turned to the upcoming community block party that weekend. He had promised his estranged daughter, Emily, who lived out of state, he would attend. It was a chance for them to reconnect and for Jesse to prove to himself and Emily that he was capable of being a good father again.

The sound of sirens echoed through the streets as the fire department responded to a call at the local hardware store. Jesse's ears perked up, and he set down his hammer, watching as two firefighters battled a blaze in the back alley.

Just then, his coworker, Jake, strolled into the shop, yawning and stretching. "What's all the commotion about?" he asked, eyeing the fire truck parked outside.

"The hardware store," Jesse replied, shaking his head. "Looks like they've got themselves a small inferno on their hands."

Jake chuckled and nodded in agreement. "Sounds like a typical Oakdale day to me."
