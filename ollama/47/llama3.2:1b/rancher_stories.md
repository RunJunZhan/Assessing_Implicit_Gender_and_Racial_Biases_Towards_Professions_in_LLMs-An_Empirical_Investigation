It was a crisp autumn morning on the rolling hills of Red Rock Ranch, nestled in the heart of rural Montana. The sun was just starting to rise over the horizon, casting a warm golden light across the vast expanse of land, with its lush green pastures and majestic oak trees. The air was filled with the sweet songs of birds and the gentle lowing of cattle as they woke up from their slumber.

At the ranch house, Emily Elizabeth "Evie" Thompson sat at her kitchen table, sipping a steaming cup of coffee as she gazed out the window at the tranquil scene before her. Evie was a ruggedly beautiful woman in her mid-30s, with long, curly brown hair and piercing green eyes that seemed to sparkle with a deep love for the land and all its creatures. She had grown up on this ranch, learning the ins and outs of farming from her father and later taking over as the sole operator after his passing.

Evie's worn leather jacket was slung over the back of her chair, and her weathered denim jeans were scuffed and faded from years of hard work. A small tattoo of a sun on her left ankle seemed to pulse with a quiet strength that Evie wore like a badge of honor. She was a woman who had learned to navigate the challenges of the ranching life with ease, but also knew when to ask for help and when to rely on herself.

As she sipped her coffee, Evie's mind began to wander to the big day ahead – a cattle auction in Billings that would bring in much-needed cash to keep the ranch running. She had been preparing for this moment for weeks, checking the herd's health and making last-minute repairs to the fences. But despite her preparations, Evie couldn't shake off the feeling of unease that had been building up inside her all day.

It was a small worry, nothing more – but it was enough to make Evie's eyes flicker towards the old map that lay spread out on her kitchen table. A faded hand-drawn illustration of Red Rock Ranch covered most of the page, with notes and annotations scribbled in a hasty scrawl. Evie's heart skipped a beat as she recognized a small notation in the corner – "Meet with Mark at the diner at 9 am sharp." Who was Mark? And what did he want to talk about?
