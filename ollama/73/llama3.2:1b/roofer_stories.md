The sun was just starting to rise over the small town of Oakdale, casting a warm golden light over the worn streets and crumbling buildings that had been the home of Jack "Hawk" Hawkins for as long as anyone could remember. Hawk was a roofer by trade, and he stood on the edge of his family's old warehouse district, surveying the day ahead with a practiced air of calm.

The warehouse district, a labyrinthine network of narrow streets and dead-end alleys, had been in Hawk's family for generations - and as the oldest son of Jack "Hawk" Hawkins Sr., he was next in line to take over the business. His father had built the town's first successful roofing company from scratch, and Hawk had learned the trade on his father's knee.

As he gazed out at the streets, Hawk felt a sense of pride and ownership - this was his territory, and he knew every nook and cranny, every hidden alleyway and every rusty old sign. But as he turned to head back into the warehouse district, he noticed something that made him pause: a piece of paper stuck to the side of a building, with a faint scrawl on it that looked like some sort of cryptic message.

Hawk's instincts told him to ignore the note - but there was something about it that drew him in, like a moth to a flame. He tucked the paper into his pocket and headed back inside, his mind already racing with possibilities...
