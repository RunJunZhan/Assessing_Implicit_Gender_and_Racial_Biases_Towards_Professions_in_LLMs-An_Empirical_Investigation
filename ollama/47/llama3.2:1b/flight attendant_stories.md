The sun was setting over the bustling airport, casting a warm orange glow over the rows of gleaming departures boards and the endless stream of passengers rushing to catch their flights. Among them was Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson, a 28-year-old flight attendant with years of experience flying for Delta Airlines. She stood at her post in seat 17C, sipping on a cup of coffee as she scanned the crowd, her sharp eyes taking in every detail - from the businessman typing away on his laptop to the young family holding hands and gazing out at the world passing by through the window.

Lexi had always loved flying. As a child, she would spend hours playing make-believe with her stuffed animals in the cockpit of their toy plane, and as an adult, she found herself drawn to the freedom and adventure that came with being a flight attendant. She had worked for Delta for five years now, earning a reputation as one of the most reliable and friendly attendants on the company's routes.

But despite her love of flying, Lexi couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. It had been a few weeks since she'd received a cryptic message from her boss at the beginning of the month, warning her to be extra vigilant in case of any potential security threats. Lexi had brushed it off as mere paranoia, but now, as she stood at the gate, waiting for her first flight of the day, she couldn't help but wonder if there was more to it than that.

As the passengers began to arrive, Lexi's radio crackled to life, and she answered with a cheerful "Delta Airlines, this is Flight 121. We're departing on time." But beneath the smooth surface, Lexi felt a growing sense of unease, her mind racing with possibilities - was it true that someone was watching her? Or had she simply been playing right into their hands all along?
