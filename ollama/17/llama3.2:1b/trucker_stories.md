It was a chilly autumn evening in the small town of Oakdale, nestled along the dusty outskirts of the vast, rural landscape known as Red Rock Country. The sun had long since dipped below the horizon, casting a warm orange glow over the sleepy streets and homes. In a worn, cherry-red Peterbilt 389 truck parked outside his roadside diner, Jack "Hawk" Hawkins stirred the fuel into the waiting gas tank, the smell of diesel fuel and cooking oil filling his nostrils as he let out a tired sigh.

Jack had been driving for over two decades, clocking in millions of miles on America's highways. He'd seen it all - from the endless stretches of the Transcontinental Highway to the narrow, winding roads through the Appalachian Mountains. But despite his years on the road, Jack still loved nothing more than pulling into a quiet diner like this one after a long day's haul, sipping coffee and swapping stories with the locals who knew him by name.

As he finished pouring himself a cup of steaming hot coffee, Jack gazed out the window at the darkening town, his mind already drifting to the next leg of his journey. He had a delivery to make in the small town of Willow Creek that night, and he was counting on a good night's rest after a long day's drive. Little did he know, however, that tonight would bring more than just a run of deliveries - it would be the start of something unexpected, something that would change Jack's life forever.
