It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the leaves on the oak trees rustled softly in the gentle breeze like a chorus of whispers. The sun rose over the rooftops, casting a warm glow over the quiet streets and homes, signaling the start of another new week at Oakwood High School.

Ava Moreno stood outside the school's front doors, her long brown hair tied back in a neat ponytail as she adjusted her worn leather jacket for what felt like the hundredth time. She had been a guidance counselor here for five years, and she knew every student by name, every story they shared with her, and every struggle they faced. But today was different.

Ava's eyes scanned the crowd of students streaming towards her office, each one carrying their own worries and fears. Some were nervous about exams or interviews, while others seemed lost in thought, struggling to find their place in the world. Ava had seen it all before, but she took a deep breath as she welcomed the first student of the day with a warm smile.

"Good morning, everyone," she said, her voice soothing and reassuring. "I'm Ava Moreno, your guidance counselor. What brings you here today?"
