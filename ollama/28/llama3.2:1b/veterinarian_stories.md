It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the leaves on the trees rustled with an eerie golden hue as the sun struggled to rise above the horizon. Dr. Sophia Patel, a renowned veterinarian and owner of the local animal clinic, Sunny Meadows Veterinary Clinic, stepped out onto her front porch, sipping a warm cup of coffee as she gazed out at the sleepy streets. The clinic's sign creaked in the gentle breeze, reading "Sunny Meadows" in bold, golden letters.

Sophia had always loved this time of year, with its promise of new beginnings and fresh starts. As a veterinarian, she felt most alive during these quiet months, surrounded by the animals that filled her heart and home. But as she took another sip of her coffee, she couldn't shake off the feeling that something was different this year. A sense of unease had been growing inside her for weeks, ever since rumors began circulating about a mysterious illness affecting the local wildlife.

As she stood there, lost in thought, Sophia noticed a flurry of activity outside her clinic's large windows. The sound of chirping birds and chattering squirrels filled the air, but beneath it all, she could hear the faint whine of engines and the rumble of wheels on pavement. A black SUV had pulled up to the curb, its tinted windows reflecting the morning sun like a dark mirror. Sophia's instincts prickled with unease as she wondered what kind of visitor this was, and what they might want.
