Here's a potential story opening and setting for an occupational therapy-themed novel:

---

The misty dawn air clung to the small town of Willow Creek like a damp shroud, casting long shadows across the streets and homes. Dr. Sophia Ellis, a soft-spoken but sharp-witted occupational therapist in her late 30s, stood at the edge of the town's central square, gazing out at the sea of faces gathered there. The annual Summer Solstice Festival was in full swing, with colorful balloons and streamers floating above the crowd.

Sophia's eyes scanned the scene, her trained eye taking in the various attractions: a juggling club, a face-painting booth, and a makeshift obstacle course made from fallen branches and inflated tires. She smiled to herself as she thought about the children who had come to this festival for weeks, their faces alight with excitement and anticipation.

As an occupational therapist, Sophia had seen her fair share of traumas and injuries – but there was something special about helping people rebuild their lives after a fall or a fall-related incident. She took pride in her work, knowing that she could make a difference in the small towns like Willow Creek where everyone knew each other's names.

But as she stood there, lost in thought, Sophia felt a subtle shift in the air. A faint rustling sound caught her attention – someone was approaching from behind the crowd. She turned to see a young man with a messy mop of blond hair and a pair of worn jeans, his eyes scanning the scene with a mixture of curiosity and wariness.

"Can I help you?" Sophia asked, her hand instinctively going to the phone in her pocket.

The stranger hesitated for a moment before responding, "I'm just looking for... um... someone. A friend."
