It's a chilly autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of rural Pennsylvania. The year is 1995, and the local economy is still recovering from the economic downturn of the late 1980s. The townspeople are slowly rebuilding their lives, and the sound of cranes and excavators can be heard on the outskirts of town.

The main character's name is Ryder "Ridge" Jackson, a 28-year-old heavy equipment operator who has worked for the local construction company, Jackson's Excavation, for five years. Ryder is a rugged, no-nonsense individual with a strong work ethic and a love for the outdoors. He grew up on a farm just outside of Willow Creek, where he learned to ride horses and operate tractors from his father.

Ridge lives in a small, one-bedroom apartment above his family's old farmhouse, which has been in the Jackson family for generations. His parents still live there, along with his younger sister, Emily, who is 19 years old and studying to become a teacher at the local high school.

As Ryder wakes up early on this autumn morning, he can hear the sounds of the town waking up - the chirping of birds, the clanging of pots and pans from the kitchen, and the occasional bark of a dog. He gets dressed in his worn jeans, faded denim shirt, and scuffed-up boots, and heads downstairs to start his day.

He begins with a hearty breakfast of eggs, bacon, and toast, washed down with a cold glass of milk from the refrigerator. As he eats, he checks his watch for what feels like the hundredth time that morning - it's 6:30 AM, and he has a 7:30 AM start on a new job at the old industrial site on the outskirts of town.

The owner of the site, Mr. Thompson, is notorious for being demanding and unpredictable, but Ryder has worked with him before and knows how to handle himself. He packs his lunch - a sandwich, carrot sticks, and a bottle of water - and heads out the door to catch the bus that will take him to the job site.
