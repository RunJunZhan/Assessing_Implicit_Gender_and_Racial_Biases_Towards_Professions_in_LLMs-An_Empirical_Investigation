It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, where the leaves on the trees turned vibrant shades of orange and red as the sun rose over the rolling hills. The air was filled with the sweet scent of ripe apples from the local orchard, and the sound of birds chirping their morning songs.

Among the sleepy residents of Oakdale was 35-year-old Ryan Thompson, a ruggedly handsome groundskeeper at the town's historic Oakwood Manor. Ryan had grown up in Oakdale, the son of the manor's former owner, who had instilled in him a love for nature and taking care of the land.

As he emerged from his small cottage on the outskirts of town, Ryan breathed in deeply, feeling the dew-kissed grass beneath his feet. He stretched his lean frame and took a moment to admire the majestic oak tree that dominated the manor's grounds, its gnarled branches stretching towards the sky like withered fingers.

With a cup of steaming coffee in hand, Ryan set out onto the winding driveway, the crunch of gravel beneath his boots echoing through the stillness. He had a long day ahead of him – mowing the lawns, pruning the roses, and ensuring the manor's gardens were looking their best for the upcoming Harvest Festival.

As he walked towards the manor, Ryan noticed a commotion at the entrance hall. His boss, Mr. Jenkins, was standing by the front door, his face grave with concern. "Ryan, I need to talk to you about something," he said, glancing around nervously before locking the door behind him.

Ryan's curiosity piqued, he followed his boss into the dimly lit office, where a look of distress etched Ryan's features.
