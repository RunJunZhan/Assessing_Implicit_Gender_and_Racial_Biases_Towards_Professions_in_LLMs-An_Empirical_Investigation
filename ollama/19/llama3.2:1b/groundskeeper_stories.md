Here's a potential story opening and setting for a mystery novel featuring a groundskeeper:

The misty dawn crept over the crumbling stone walls of Ravenswood Manor, casting an eerie gloom over the overgrown gardens. It was a place where the past seemed to seep from the very soil, where the whispers of generations past echoed through the empty halls. This was the domain of Emrys "Mori" Blackwood, a groundskeeper with a passion for restoring beauty to the decaying grandeur of Ravenswood.

As Mori stepped out of his small cottage on the manor's outskirts, the dew-kissed grass beneath his feet squelched softly against the wooden boards. He breathed in deeply, taking stock of the day ahead. The estate was due for its annual spring clean, and Mori had spent countless hours preparing for this moment. But as he surveyed the gardens, a sense of unease settled in the pit of his stomach. Something felt off.

The previous night's storm had left a trail of destruction in its wake – broken glass, shattered vases, and torn fabric scattered across the pathways. Mori's eyes narrowed as he spotted a piece of paper caught in a bush near the north rose garden. He stooped to retrieve it, his mind already racing with possibilities. Who could have done this? And what did they want?

As he smoothed out the crumpled note, Mori's gaze fell upon the manor's portrait, hanging crookedly in its frame on the wall of the great hall. For a moment, their eyes met – and Mori felt an inexplicable jolt of recognition. The face that stared back at him was not his own...
