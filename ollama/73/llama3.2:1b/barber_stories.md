It was a drizzly Wednesday morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where Jack "The Cut" McAllister had spent his entire life. The kind of place where everyone knew each other's names and the local diner served as the hub for gossip and social gatherings. As he unlocked the door of his family-owned barber shop on Main Street, a warm bell above the entrance let out a cheerful ring.

Inside, the shop was a familiar sanctuary, lined with shelves filled with hair clippers, scissors, and an assortment of peculiar trinkets that Jack had accumulated over the years. The air was thick with the scent of bay rum and sweat as he began to prepare for another day of cutting hair, chatting with regulars, and dealing with the occasional prank war between his two young sons, who were always getting into mischief.

Jack's eyes scanned the shop, taking in the familiar sights: a faded "World's Okayest Barber" mug on the counter, a stack of newspapers with last week's edition tacked to the wall, and a peculiar contraption that Jack was pretty sure he'd never seen before – it looked like a cross between a hair dryer and a vacuum cleaner.

As he worked his magic on a particularly recalcitrant customer, Jack's phone buzzed with a text from an unknown number. The message read: "Meet me at the old warehouse on 5th Street if you want to know the truth about Willow Creek."
