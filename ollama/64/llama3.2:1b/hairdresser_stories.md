It was a crisp autumn morning in the bustling city of New Haven, where the sounds of sizzling breakfast dishes and chattering pedestrians filled the air. The sun was just starting to rise over the towering skyscrapers, casting a warm glow over the street. Amidst the chaos, 22-year-old hairdresser, Colette Dupont, stood outside her family-owned salon on Elm Street, adjusting her worn leather jacket and smoothing out her curly brown hair. She had spent countless hours perfecting her craft, learning the intricacies of color and cut from her mother and grandmother before her. As she prepared for another busy day ahead, Colette's mind wandered to her plans for the weekend – a much-needed girls' trip with her best friend, Emma, who worked as a graphic designer in the city's trendy arts district.

The scent of freshly brewed coffee wafted from the café across the street, enticing Colette to grab a quick cup before heading into the salon. She took a deep breath, feeling the invigorating aroma fill her lungs and prepare her for the day ahead. As she pushed open the door to her salon, a bell above it rang out, and the familiar hum of hair clippers and scissors filled the air, signaling that another busy morning was about to begin.
