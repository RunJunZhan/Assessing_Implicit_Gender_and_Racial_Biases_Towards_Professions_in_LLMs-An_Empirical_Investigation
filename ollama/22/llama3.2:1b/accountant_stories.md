Here's a potential start to a story:

---

The rain poured down on the streets of Ashwood, casting a gloomy mist over the small town where 32-year-old accountant Emily Taylor lived and worked. She stood at her kitchen window, sipping a lukewarm cup of coffee as she gazed out at the wet pavement. The sign above her garage door read "Taylor & Co." in elegant script, a testament to her family's legacy in the accounting firm that had been her home for the past five years.

Emily's mind was already elsewhere, her thoughts consumed by the looming deadline for her clients' annual tax returns. She took a deep breath, letting the cool morning air clear her head as she began to mentally review the complex spreadsheet on her computer screen. It was going to be a long day ahead of her, but she was ready.

Just then, her phone rang, breaking the silence of the rainy morning. Emily's heart skipped a beat as she answered, expecting it to be one of her clients calling to ask for last-minute adjustments to their return. But instead, it was someone else on the line - the CEO of Ashwood Industries, the town's largest corporation.

"Emily, I need to talk to you about something," the CEO said, his voice firm but measured. "I've been made aware of some irregularities in our financial records, and I'd like to schedule a meeting with you to discuss them."

Emily's eyes widened as she raised an eyebrow. Irregularities? That sounded serious. She leaned back in her chair, her mind racing with possibilities. What could be going on that required her attention from the CEO of Ashwood Industries herself?
