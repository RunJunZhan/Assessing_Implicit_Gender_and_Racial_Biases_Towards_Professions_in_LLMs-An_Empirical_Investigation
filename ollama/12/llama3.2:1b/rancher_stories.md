It was a crisp autumn morning on the sprawling Sackett Ranch, nestled deep in the heart of rural Montana. The air was alive with the sweet scent of ripe wheat and the distant howl of a coyote, as the sun rose over the vast expanse of rolling hills and dusty trails. Jack Sackett, a rugged and weathered rancher in his late 40s, stood on the porch of his family's old homestead, sipping a cup of black coffee and gazing out at the landscape he had worked tirelessly to build into a thriving operation.

The Sacketts had been living and farming this land for generations, and Jack took great pride in keeping the tradition alive. His parents had built the ranch from the ground up, and now Jack was carrying on that legacy with his own family. He had two kids, Emily and Ethan, who were both learning the ins and outs of ranching from a young age. But Jack's focus right now was on preparing for the upcoming harvest season, which would bring with it a influx of new customers, equipment needs, and the ever-present threat of natural disasters.

As he stood there, lost in thought, Jack's mind began to wander back to the summer when his younger brother, Caleb, had gone missing. The search party that followed had been fruitless, but Jack couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. He had always felt a deep connection to this land and its history, and now he wasn't so sure if he'd ever be able to find peace again.
