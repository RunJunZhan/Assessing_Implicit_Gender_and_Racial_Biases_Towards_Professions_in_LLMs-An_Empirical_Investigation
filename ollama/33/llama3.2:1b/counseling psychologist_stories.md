The sun was setting over the small town of Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint Main Street. Inside the local community center, Dr. Sophia Ellis, a seasoned counseling psychologist in her mid-40s with a kind face and gentle demeanor, prepared for another evening of talk therapy sessions with her clients.

As she polished a stack of dusty patient files on the conference table, Sophia's mind wandered to the upcoming weekend's schedule. She had promised her friends that she would join them for a hike at nearby Red Rock Canyon, but now she was regretting it. The long drive had been a nightmare, and the thought of another hour spent sitting in traffic was just too much.

The community center was a cozy spot, filled with comfortable couches and wooden tables where people could sit and talk in small groups. Sophia's office was situated at the rear of the building, overlooking the parking lot and the surrounding streets. It was her favorite place to practice, with its soothing natural light pouring in through the large windows.

As she finished tidying up the files, a knock on the door interrupted Sophia's reverie. It was Rachel, one of her regular clients, a young mother of two who had been struggling to cope with the stress of parenting and marriage. "Hey, Doc, can I talk to you for a minute?" Rachel asked, her eyes anxious.

Sophia smiled and beckoned Rachel over, knowing that this conversation would be just what she needed to get through the rest of the evening.
