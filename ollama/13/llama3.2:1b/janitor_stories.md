It was a chilly autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the sun struggled to peek through the dense clouds that had rolled in overnight. The streets were quiet, save for the occasional passerby hurrying to get to work on time. Amidst the stillness, 25-year-old Jesse "Jazz" Thompson made his way through the deserted hallways of Oakwood High School, his worn sneakers squeaking on the cracked linoleum.

As a janitor at the school, Jazz had grown accustomed to the musty smell of old textbooks and the faint tang of disinfectant that lingered in every corner. He took pride in keeping the building spotless, not just for himself, but for the students who called it home. With his thick-framed glasses perched on the end of his nose, Jazz adjusted his cap and surveyed the messy hallway, ready to tackle another day of scrubbing floors and sweeping up after the student body's energetic antics.

Jazz had always been a quiet kid, content to observe from the sidelines rather than participating in the excitement. However, after losing his father in a tragic accident when he was just a teenager, Jazz found it difficult to cope with the pain and loss. He turned to a new sense of purpose by taking over as the janitor at Oakwood High, hoping that working behind the scenes would allow him to stay connected to his community while also keeping his own heart open. Little did he know, this day would bring more than just dusty floors and late-night cleaning; it would set in motion events that would change Jazz's life forever.
