It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled along the winding curves of Highway 12. The sun was just starting to rise over the rolling hills and dense forests that surrounded the highway, casting a warm golden light over the landscape. In a small trailer parked near the edge of the road, Jack "Hawk" Hawkins stirred awake, the smell of freshly brewed coffee wafting from his thermos on the kitchen counter.

Jack was a 45-year-old highway worker, having spent the better part of two decades building and maintaining the highways that crisscrossed the country. He had grown up in Oakdale, where he learned to drive at a young age and quickly proved himself to be one of the most skilled and reliable truckers on the road.

As he got dressed and headed downstairs for breakfast, Jack couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. He had been noticing strange occurrences on the highway lately – equipment gone missing, weird noises in the night, and an increasing sense of unease among his coworkers. The town's sheriff had been tight-lipped about it all, but Jack had a gut instinct telling him that something big was brewing.

He poured himself a hearty bowl of oatmeal and sat down at the kitchen table to enjoy his breakfast while scanning the news on his phone. The headlines were always filled with stories of strange occurrences along the highway – reports of ghostly apparitions, unexplained lights in the woods, and bizarre occurrences involving abandoned trucks. Jack had seen it all before, but there was something about this latest batch of incidents that didn't sit right with him.

With a cup of coffee in hand, Jack headed out to his truck, a rusty old Peterbilt 359 named "Bertha" that he had lovingly restored over the years. As he drove down Highway 12, the sun climbed higher in the sky, casting long shadows across the landscape and illuminating the eerie feeling that something was waiting for him on the other side of the next bend.
