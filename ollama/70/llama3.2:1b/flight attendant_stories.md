It was a crisp autumn evening in New York City, the kind that made you want to wrap up in a warm coat and step outside onto the bustling streets of Midtown. The sun had just set over the towering skyscrapers, casting a golden glow over the concrete jungle. In a small office above the concourse of John F. Kennedy International Airport, Flight Attendant Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stirred restlessly in her plush bed. She lay on her back, staring up at the ceiling and trying to will herself into a more civilized state.

As she tossed off the covers and swung her legs over the side of the bed, Lexi's mind began to wander to the long flight ahead of her. It was a transatlantic journey from London to New York, and one that required her to be sharp, friendly, and alert at all times. She had been working as a flight attendant for five years now, and while she loved the freedom and adventure that came with it, she couldn't help but feel a twinge of boredom in these early morning hours before her shift.

Lexi threw off her robe and padded over to the window, pulling back the curtains to reveal the darkened cityscape. She gazed out at the twinkling lights of the skyscrapers below, feeling a sense of melancholy wash over her. It was moments like these that she wished she could escape, just for a little while, and leave behind the stress and responsibility of her job.

As she stood there, lost in thought, Lexi's phone buzzed with an incoming text from her best friend, Rachel. "Hey girl! How's it going? Need any help getting out of this city?" The message was brief, but Lexi knew that Rachel would know exactly what she needed - a much-needed escape from the confines of John F. Kennedy International Airport.
