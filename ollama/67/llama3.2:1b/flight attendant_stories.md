It was a warm summer evening in the small coastal town of Tidestone, where the salty air was sweet with the scent of sea salt and the sound of seagulls filled the sky. The year was 1995, and the sun had just set over the horizon, casting a golden glow over the quaint streets and buildings.

Ava Moreno, a bright and bubbly flight attendant for Tidestone Airlines, stood at the helm of Flight 121, preparing for its evening departure to San Francisco. She had worked at the airline for three years now, but this would be her last flight before she handed in her ticket stubs forever. Ava felt a mix of excitement and nervousness as she gazed out the window, watching the townspeople wave goodbye as the plane took off into the night sky.

As Flight 121 soared above Tidestone, the engines roaring like a lion's roar, Ava couldn't help but think about her life. She was 25 years old, with a small apartment in the town center and a group of close friends who were all slowly drifting apart as they entered their late twenties. Her parents were getting older, and it seemed like no matter how hard she tried, they just wouldn't seem to connect with the modern world anymore.

But for now, Ava was content to escape into her work, pouring her heart and soul into every passenger she met, every smile she gave, and every drink she poured. She had always dreamed of flying high, just like her idol, Amelia Earhart – soaring above the clouds and living life on the edge. And as she flew over the Pacific Ocean, Ava felt a sense of freedom that only came from pushing off the ground and taking to the skies.
