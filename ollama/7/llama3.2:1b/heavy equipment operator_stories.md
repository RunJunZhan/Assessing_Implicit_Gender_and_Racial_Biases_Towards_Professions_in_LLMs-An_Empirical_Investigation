It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of rural Montana. The sun was just starting to rise over the vast expanse of rolling hills and verdant farmland, casting a golden glow over the sleepy community. In the midst of this tranquil scene, 22-year-old Ryder Jackson stood atop a worn-out dump truck, his bright orange vest emblazoned with the name "Bull Rider" emblazoned on the back in black letters.

Ryder was a heavy equipment operator for Jackson's Heavy Hauling Co., a family-owned business that had been in operation for generations. He lived and breathed machinery, and his hands were as familiar with the rugged landscape of Montana as his own eyes. As he gazed out at the endless fields of corn and wheat, Ryder felt a sense of contentment wash over him. This was his happy place – where the only thing that mattered was getting the job done.

But on this particular morning, something felt off. The air was thick with an unsettling energy, like a whispered secret shared among the townspeople. Ryder's instincts told him to keep a close eye on things, but he had spent countless days working in these fields without incident. Still, as he climbed down from the truck and began his pre-shift routine, Ryder couldn't shake the feeling that something was waiting for him just beyond the horizon...
