It was a typical Saturday morning at "Cutting Ties," the bustling hair salon on Elm Street. The sun was shining through the large windows, casting a warm glow over the neatly arranged chairs and the sound of chatter filled the air. The smell of freshly brewed coffee wafted from the café downstairs, enticing customers to start their day.

At the helm of it all was 25-year-old hair cutter, Jasmine "Jazz" Thompson. Jazz had been cutting hair since she was a teenager, learning the trade from her mother who owned a small salon in town. After completing an apprenticeship and working for several years, Jazz had decided to strike out on her own, opening "Cutting Ties" with a vision of creating a welcoming space where people felt comfortable and confident in their hair.

As she expertly styled a customer's bob, Jazz's phone rang, breaking the peaceful atmosphere. She picked up, her eyes scanning the salon as she listened to the voice on the other end. It was her best friend and co-owner, Sofia Rodriguez. "Jazz, I have some great news," Sofia said, her enthusiasm evident over the phone. "The boutique that just opened down the street has been looking for a stylist with your style and expertise. They're offering you a meeting to discuss the opportunity."
