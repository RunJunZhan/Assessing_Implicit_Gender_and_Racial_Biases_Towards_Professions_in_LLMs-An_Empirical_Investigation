The sun had barely risen over the sleepy town of Ravenswood, casting a warm glow over the quaint Main Street. The smell of freshly brewed coffee wafted from the local diner, enticing the sleepy residents as they slowly began to stir. But amidst the calm morning atmosphere, one figure stood out - 25-year-old electrician, Jesse Everett.

Jesse lived in a small, weathered bungalow on Elm Street, surrounded by tall trees and overgrown gardens. His bright blue eyes scanned the street, taking in the usual morning routines: the early risers sipping coffee on their porches, the delivery trucks rumbling down Main Street, and the occasional jogger pounding the pavement.

As Jesse made his way to his toolbox in the garage, he couldn't shake off the feeling that something was off. It had been three days since his best friend, Mike, had gone missing while working a job on the outskirts of town. The police had given up searching for him, but Jesse's anxiety lingered, like a low hum in the back of his mind.

With a deep breath, Jesse swung open the creaky garage door and stepped inside, his eyes adjusting to the dim light within. He began to organize his tools, his hands moving with a practiced ease that belied the turmoil brewing inside him. The task at hand was simple - repair a faulty electrical circuit in one of Mike's old houses on the outskirts of town. But for Jesse, it was so much more than just a routine job; it was a reminder of what he had lost, and a warning that this case might be more complicated than he initially thought.
