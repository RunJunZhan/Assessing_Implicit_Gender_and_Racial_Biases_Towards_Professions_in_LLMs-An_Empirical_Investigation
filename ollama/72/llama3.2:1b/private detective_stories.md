It was a misty dawn on the bustling streets of New Haven, the kind that made you want to pull your hair out in a good way. The city's oldest district, known for its ancient architecture and winding alleys, was alive with the hum of early-riser vendors and the distant wail of sirens from the police cars parked along the curb.

Enter Julian Blackwood, a man in his late 40s with a mop of graying brown hair and piercing green eyes that seemed to see right through you. A private detective by trade, Julian had been operating on the fringes of New Haven's law enforcement for over a decade, taking on cases that no one else wanted – or could get away with.

Julian stood outside his modest office building, a nondescript two-story in a rundown section of town. The sign above the door read "Blackwood Investigations" in faded letters, and the windows were boarded up, as if to shield him from the world inside. A small, leather-bound journal lay open on the counter, filled with scribbled notes and cryptic symbols that seemed to pulse with a life of their own.

As he lit a cigarette, a knock at the door broke the stillness. Julian's gaze narrowed, his eyes narrowing further as he recognized the voice on the other side – it was Rachel Morse, his former partner and confidante from the old days.

"Julian, I need to talk to you about some business," Rachel said, her voice low and urgent. "I know you're busy, but I have a feeling this one's going to be different. Something's off."
