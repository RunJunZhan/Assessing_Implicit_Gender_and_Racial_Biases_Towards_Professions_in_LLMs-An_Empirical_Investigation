The misty dawn crept over the small town of Willow Creek, shrouding its quaint Main Street in a damp, gray veil. The air was heavy with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, a smell that seemed to cling to everything it touched. Amidst this soggy landscape, plumber Emrys "Echo" Everett stood at the edge of town, his worn leather boots squelching in the muddy puddles as he gazed out at the gloomy horizon. It was a typical Wednesday morning for Echo, and he had just pulled an all-nighter to fix the broken pipes that had brought the town's water supply to a standstill.

As he took a long drag on his cigarette, Echo couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. The town's residents were huddled indoors, their faces etched with worry and concern. The local diner, where Echo usually stopped for breakfast before heading out to tackle another night's worth of plumbing repairs, was dark and silent. A chill ran down his spine as he wondered what had caused the sudden collapse of the water supply – and whether it would be a one-time thing, or something more sinister lurking just beneath the surface.
