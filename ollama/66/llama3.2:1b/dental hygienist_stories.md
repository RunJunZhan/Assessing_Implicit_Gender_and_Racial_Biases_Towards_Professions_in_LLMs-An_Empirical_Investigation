It was a crisp autumn morning in the small coastal town of Tidal Cove, where the sound of seagulls filled the air and the smell of saltwater and freshly baked pastries wafted from the local bakery. Dr. Sophia Ellis, a bright-eyed and ambitious dental hygienist, stood outside her cozy practice, Sunny Smiles Dental, sipping on a cup of coffee as she gazed out at the serene streets lined with historic beach houses and quaint shops.

The sun was just starting to rise over the horizon, casting a warm golden light over the town. A gentle breeze rustled the leaves of the trees, causing the sound effects of the ocean to echo through the air. Sophia took a deep breath, feeling invigorated by the morning's calm atmosphere, and began her day, preparing for another busy week at work.

As she finished getting ready, Sophia's phone buzzed with an incoming text from her best friend and colleague, Rachel: "Hey Soph, I'm running late for my shift. Traffic is a nightmare! Meet me outside the dentist's office in 10?" Sophia smiled to herself, knowing that Rachel was always punctual (as much as you can be when dealing with traffic on the North Shore). With a final check of her appointment book and a spring in her step, she set out into the bright morning light, ready to face whatever challenges came her way.
