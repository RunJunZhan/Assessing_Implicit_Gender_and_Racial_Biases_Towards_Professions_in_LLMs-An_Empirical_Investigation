The sun was just starting to peek over the horizon, casting a warm glow over the small town of Oakdale, where 25-year-old Jesse "Jazz" Thompson worked as a mechanic at his family's garage, Thompson's Towing and Repair. The smell of freshly cut grass and diesel fuel filled the air as Jazz began his morning routine, starting with a hot cup of coffee and checking the daily forecast on his phone.

Jazz lived in a small two-story house on Elm Street with his mom, his younger sister, Lily, and their beloved golden retriever, Max. Oakdale was a tight-knit community where everyone knew each other's names and stories. The local diner, Joe's Place, was always bustling with chatter about the town's latest gossip and events.

As Jazz finished his coffee, he glanced out the garage window to see the old oak tree in the center of town swaying gently in the breeze. It was a sight that never got old, and one that reminded him of why he became an auto mechanic in the first place – the thrill of tinkering with engines, the satisfaction of fixing problems, and the camaraderie of working alongside his dad, who ran the garage.

Little did Jazz know, today was going to be a day like any other...
