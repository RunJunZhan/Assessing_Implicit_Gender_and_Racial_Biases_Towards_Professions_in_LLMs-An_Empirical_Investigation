In the small town of Red Rock, nestled in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains, 25-year-old August "Augie" Thompson spent his days honing his craft as a stone cutter on the local quarry grounds. The sun-kissed hills and rolling creeks had been Augie's home for as long as he could remember, and he took pride in being one of the few remaining members of his family's traditional trade.

As he worked alongside his father and uncles, shaping rough-hewn boulders into gleaming slabs for local builders and craftsmen, Augie felt a sense of satisfaction that went beyond mere financial gain. There was something therapeutic about watching stone come alive under his hammer, its rough texture yielding to the smooth blows of his tool.

The quarry grounds were quiet this morning, except for the occasional bleat of a goat or the distant rumble of a train chugging out of Red Rock's small station. Augie took a deep breath, feeling the crisp mountain air fill his lungs, and began to carefully select a particularly stubborn boulder from the pile. Its rough surface was perfect for shaping into a sleek marble slab.

Little did Augie know, today would bring more than just a routine day of work – it would be one that would set him on a path he never could have imagined.
