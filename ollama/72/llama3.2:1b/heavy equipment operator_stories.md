It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of rural Montana. The sun rises over the rolling hills and vast ranchlands, casting a warm golden light over the quaint Main Street. The smell of freshly cut grass and wood smoke fills the air as the townspeople start their day.

In the midst of this picturesque scene, 25-year-old Ryder "Raven" Blackwood stands at the edge of town, clad in his rugged work gear. His heavy equipment operator's vest is emblazoned with the logo of Raven Equipment Rentals, a local company that has been a staple in Oakdale for decades. With a confident stride, Raven approaches the town's water tower, where the owner, Old Man Jenkins, awaits.
