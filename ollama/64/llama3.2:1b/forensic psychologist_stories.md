The sun had barely cracked the horizon over the sleepy town of Ashwood, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint Main Street. But Dr. Emilia Grey, a renowned forensic psychologist with a sharp mind and a keen eye for detail, was not here to enjoy the morning beauty. Instead, she stood outside the local police station, her eyes fixed on the nondescript building across the street, its beige walls seeming to blend seamlessly into the surrounding landscape.

As a expert in the field of behavioral analysis, Emilia had spent years studying the minds of convicted felons and calculating the most effective strategies for rehabilitation. Her latest case was no exception – a young woman named Sarah Jenkins, accused of murdering her ex-boyfriend in a heated argument at their shared apartment complex six months prior. The police had collected enough evidence to secure a conviction, but Emilia's work with Sarah hinted at something more sinister beneath the surface.

With a deep breath, Emilia pulled out her phone and dialed a number – the one she used for everything from research queries to confidential consultations. Her assistant, Rachel, answered on the first ring, her voice warm and familiar as always.

"Hey, Em. How's it going?" Rachel asked, already knowing the answer before Emilia could even finish speaking.

"It's going," Emilia replied, her eyes still fixed on the police station across the street. "I have a feeling that Sarah Jenkins is more than just a straightforward murderer."
