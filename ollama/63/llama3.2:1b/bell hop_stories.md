It was a crisp autumn evening in New York City, with the streets of Manhattan buzzing with activity as people made their way to and from the finish line of the annual Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade. The sun had just set over the towering skyscrapers, casting a warm orange glow over the crowds of revelers.

In a small hotel on Central Park South, bellhop Emily Maynard was busy preparing for another chaotic night ahead. She had been working at the Four Seasons Hotel for three years, and had grown accustomed to the constant stream of guests rushing to catch their trains or buses. But tonight was special - it was the night of the famous parade, and many of the hotel's guests were planning to stay up late watching the floats and performances.

Emily was a petite woman in her mid-twenties, with long brown hair and bright green eyes that sparkled with excitement whenever she talked about the parade. She had always loved music and dance, and spent every spare moment practicing her tap dancing skills or singing along to her favorite holiday tunes on the radio. As she polished the mirrors and made sure the rooms were ready for the night's guests, Emily felt a thrill of anticipation build inside her - tonight was going to be one for the books.

Just as she was finishing up in room 3141, a tall, lanky man with a messy mop of hair walked into the lobby. He was dressed in a bright red coat and matching hat, and a confident smile spread across his face as he scanned the crowd. "Hey there, Emily!" he called out, falling into step beside her. "My name is Max, by the way. I'm here for the parade."
