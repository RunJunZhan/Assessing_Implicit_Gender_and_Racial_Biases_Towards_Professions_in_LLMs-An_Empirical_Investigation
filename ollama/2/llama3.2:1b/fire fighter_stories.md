Here's a potential opening and setting for a story featuring a firefighter named Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson:

It was a crisp autumn evening in the small town of Oakdale, California, with a light dusting of fog rolling in off the Pacific Ocean. The town's residents were bustling about, preparing for the annual Halloween festivities, while the firefighters from Station 17 were gearing up to tackle whatever challenges the night might bring.

Lexi Thompson, a 32-year-old firefighter with a wild mane of curly brown hair and a contagious smile, was already suiting up at the local fire station. She had been training for months to prepare for this very moment - Halloween Night Operations (HNO). As she checked her equipment and mentally went through the checklist, Lexi felt a sense of excitement and nerves building in her chest.

She had always loved fighting fires, but it wasn't just about putting out flames – it was about making a difference in people's lives. And for Lexi, there was no greater joy than serving alongside her brothers and sisters in Blue, especially when they were facing off against the kind of supernatural threats that came with HNO.

As she made her way to the fire truck, Lexi caught sight of the town's mayor, Mrs. Jenkins, standing outside the local bakery, handing out candy to the trick-or-treaters. The sweet aroma of freshly baked pumpkin pies wafted through the air, mingling with the sounds of laughter and chatter.

Little did Lexi know, this Halloween Night was going to be one for the books...
