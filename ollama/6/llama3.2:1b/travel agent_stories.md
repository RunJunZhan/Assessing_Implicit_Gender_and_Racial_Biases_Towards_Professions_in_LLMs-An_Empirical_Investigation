Here's a potential story opening:
In the heart of Paris, where the Seine River winds its way through the City of Light, stood the exclusive Belle Époque Travel Agency, aptly named "Madame Colette's Voyages." The charming boutique hotel and luxurious train carriage were nestled between a quaint patisserie and a bustling café, creating a picturesque façade that seemed to whisper secrets to passersby. Within its walls, Madame Colette herself, the incomparable travel agent with an encyclopedic knowledge of European destinations, welcomed clients from all corners of the globe.

The year was 1922, and the world was brimming with excitement as the global economy continued to thrive after the Great War. As a renowned expert in fine dining, art collections, and bespoke itineraries, Madame Colette had built her reputation on delivering unparalleled experiences to discerning travelers. Her keen eye for style, coupled with an infectious enthusiasm, made her agency a go-to destination for those seeking authentic, off-the-beaten-path adventures.

As the sun set over the city's majestic landmarks, casting a warm golden glow over the cobblestone streets and historic buildings, Madame Colette prepared for another busy day of crafting unforgettable journeys for her esteemed clientele. Little did she know, however, that this particular summer season would bring with it a new kind of traveler – one who would challenge everything she thought she knew about the world and herself...
