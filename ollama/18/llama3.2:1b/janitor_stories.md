It was a drizzly autumn evening in the sleepy town of Willow Creek, where the streets were lined with worn brick buildings and the only sound was the distant hum of the local diner's neon sign. Amidst this quaint backdrop, 32-year-old Jack "Hawk" Hawkins stood at his post, sweeping up crumb-filled sidewalks as he gazed out at the rain-soaked world around him.

Jack had spent the better part of two decades working as a janitor in Willow Creek, earning a reputation for being one of the most reliable and hardworking individuals on staff. Despite his unassuming demeanor, Jack possessed a quiet strength and an unwavering commitment to keeping his community clean and safe. He took pride in his work, not just because it paid the bills but also because it gave him a sense of purpose.

As he finished up his task for the evening, Hawk's eyes scanned the wet pavement, searching for any signs of trouble that might require his attention. The diner was closing in about 30 minutes, and Jack had promised his wife, Sarah, he'd be home to help with dinner preparations. He lit a cigarette, feeling the familiar burn of tobacco on his tongue as he took a long drag before exhaling into the chilly night air. Little did he know, this ordinary evening would soon take an extraordinary turn.
