Here's a potential story opening and setting:

The rain-soaked streets of Seattle gleamed like polished onyx as Dr. Kathryn "Kate" McKenzie stepped out of her office building and into the chilly evening air. A renowned forensic psychologist with over two decades of experience, Kate had spent years studying the minds of serial killers and their victims. Her latest case was no exception - a gruesome murder that had left the police department stumped.

As she walked to her car, a sleek black sedan pulled up beside her on 5th Avenue. The tinted windows revealed only the features of the driver, but Kate could sense the eyes locked onto hers from across the street. She felt a familiar shiver run down her spine as she slid into the passenger seat.

The driver was none other than Detective Michael "Mike" Reynolds, a ruggedly handsome man with a sharp jawline and piercing blue eyes that seemed to see right through her. Kate had worked with Mike on several high-profile cases in the past, and their professional relationship had always been cordial but strained - a product of years of working together, yet never quite getting along.

As she settled into the driver's seat, Kate couldn't help but wonder what Mike was doing out so late, alone. She glanced at her watch - 10:45 PM. Only five more hours until the 12th precinct received their report on the latest murder. Kate pulled out her phone and dialed a number, her mind already racing with possibilities.

The voice on the other end of the line was Mike's - "Katie, I know you're here to take the lead on this one." Kate smiled wryly as she replied, "Just getting my thoughts together, Mike. This is going to be a tough one."

As they pulled away from the curb and into the crowded streets of downtown Seattle, Kate felt a sense of anticipation building inside her. She had a feeling that this case was going to be different - more complex, more twisted than any she'd worked on before. And with Mike by her side, she knew she could face anything that came their way.
