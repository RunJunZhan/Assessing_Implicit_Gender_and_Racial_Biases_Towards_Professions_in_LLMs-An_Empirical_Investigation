It was a crisp autumn evening in the small town of Ravenswood, nestled deep within the English countryside. The leaves on the trees were ablaze with color, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and homes. The local pub, "The Red Lion," stood at the center of it all, its fire crackling and spitting on chilly nights like this.

Guardian James Winter, a ruggedly handsome man in his late forties with piercing blue eyes and chiseled features, stood watch outside the pub's main entrance. He had been a guard in Ravenswood for over a decade, serving under three different captains before becoming one himself. Now, as the owner of "The Red Lion," he was determined to make the town a welcoming place for all who walked through its doors.

James took a deep breath, feeling the cool night air fill his lungs, and surveyed the surroundings. The street was quiet, save for the distant hum of a tractor driving down the main road that ran through Ravenswood. He knew every inch of this town, every hidden corner and every suspicious character. It was a job he had grown to love, if not relish.

As he scanned the area, his eyes locked onto a figure darting around the corner of the pub's entrance. His heart quickened slightly, but James pushed the feeling aside. He had seen that figure before – it was usually just one of the town's young people getting into trouble, but sometimes it turned out to be something more sinister.

"Welcome to The Red Lion," James said softly to himself, a hint of a smile playing on his lips. "I'm Guardian James Winter, and I'll see you in the morning."
