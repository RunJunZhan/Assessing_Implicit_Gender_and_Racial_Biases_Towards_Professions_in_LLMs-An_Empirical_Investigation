It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the sun rose over the rolling hills and the streets were lined with the familiar faces of locals. The air was alive with the sweet scent of ripe apples and the distant rumble of the local railroad, which would soon transport hundreds of cars full of produce to markets across the country.

In a small workshop nestled in the heart of town, 22-year-old drafting worker, Ethan Thompson, stirred awake from his afternoon nap. He had been working non-stop since dawn to meet the deadline for the town's annual festival, and he was determined to get it done before the sun dipped below the horizon.

Ethan lived in a cozy apartment above his family's hardware store, which was owned by his parents and grandparents who had built the house from scratch. His room was cluttered with drafting tools, blueprints, and worn-out books on architectural design. A large wooden desk dominated one wall, covered in papers, sketches, and unfinished projects.

As he swung his legs over the side of the bed, Ethan's mother poked her head into his room, a steaming cup of coffee in hand. "Rise and shine, baby! You've got a big day ahead of you."

Ethan yawned, rubbing the sleep from his eyes. "Morning, Mom. Got my gear ready." He nodded towards the workshop below, where his father was already up, tinkering with a broken engine.
