It was a drizzly autumn evening in the city of Ashwood, where the skyscrapers seemed to stretch on forever like skeletal fingers reaching for the grey sky. The kind of night that made you want to stay indoors with a good book and a hot cup of tea, but for private detective Emilia Grey, it was just another day on the job.

Emilia stood outside her office building, a nondescript high-rise in the heart of Ashwood's financial district, and took a deep breath as she stepped inside. The air was thick with the scent of stale coffee and worn leather, a familiar smell that always brought her back to a place of comfort and familiarity. She slipped on her fedora and grabbed her umbrella, which was already clutched in her hand, before heading out into the wet night.

The streets were empty, save for the occasional lit cigarette or the soft glow of a streetlamp casting a warm light over the pavement. Emilia walked swiftly to the designated meeting point, a small café on the corner that she knew like the back of her hand. She pushed open the door and stepped inside just as the rain began to let up, creating a brief moment of calm before it picked up again.

As she waited for her contact to arrive, Emilia took a seat at the counter and ordered a whiskey neat, watching the rain-soaked patrons through the steamy glass as they chatted in hushed tones. She was waiting for Marcus Blackwood, a wealthy businessman with connections to some of Ashwood's most influential families. Rumors had been circulating about a mysterious project that Marcus was working on, one that Emilia was eager to investigate further.
