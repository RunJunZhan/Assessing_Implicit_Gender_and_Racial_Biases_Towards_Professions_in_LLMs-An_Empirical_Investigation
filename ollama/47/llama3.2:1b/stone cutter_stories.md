It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Ravenswood, nestled deep within the rolling hills of rural Colorado. The air is alive with the sweet scent of ripe apples from the local orchards, and the sound of chainsaws and pickaxes echoes through the valleys as the townspeople begin their daily preparations for harvest season.

Among them is Elijah Blackwood, a 35-year-old stone cutter who has made Ravenswood his home for the past decade. He stands at the edge of town, his eyes fixed on the old quarry where he works, its walls covered in a thick layer of rust and decay. The quarry has been his livelihood since he was a young man, but times have changed, and the demand for high-quality stone has shifted away from traditional building materials.

Elijah's rugged features and weathered skin are testament to his years spent working outdoors, his fingers stained with dirt and his eyes weary from the physical labor. He wears a faded denim shirt and a pair of scuffed-up work boots, and his dark hair is tied back in a ponytail, revealing a few missing strands that always seem to escape his attempts to tame them.

As he takes a deep breath of the cool mountain air, Elijah feels a sense of nostalgia wash over him. He remembers the days when Ravenswood was a thriving community, when people came from all over to work on the quarry's vast deposits of marble and granite. Now, it seems like a forgotten relic, a reminder of a time long past.

Elijah's thoughts are interrupted by the sound of his truck pulling into the old hangar behind the quarry. He steps out of the vehicle, stretching his tall frame as he surveys the familiar landscape. His wife, Emily, has stopped by to check on him before she heads back to town, and Elijah can't wait to see her smile and hear her stories about what's happening in Ravenswood.

As he approaches the hangar, a figure steps out from behind the truck – it's his best friend and business partner, Caleb. Caleb is a few years older than Elijah, with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes that seem to bore into those he meets. He's always been driven by a sense of adventure and a love of innovation, which has led them to experiment with new techniques in their quarrying operations.

"Elijah, man!" Caleb exclaims, clapping him on the back as he approaches. "I heard you're feeling restless. You need something more than just cutting stone."
