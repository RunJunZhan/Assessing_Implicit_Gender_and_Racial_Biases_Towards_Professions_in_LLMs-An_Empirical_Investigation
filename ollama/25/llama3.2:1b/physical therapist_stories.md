Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel featuring a physical therapist:

---

The misty dawn air clung to the streets of Ravenswood like a damp shroud as Dr. Sophia Patel stepped out of her small apartment and into the crisp morning light. She lived in a cozy studio apartment above her family's bakery on Main Street, where the sweet scent of freshly baked croissants wafted through the windows and mingled with the smell of coffee brewing in the kitchen downstairs.

Sophia took a deep breath, feeling the tension seep out of her muscles as she began to plan her day. As a physical therapist at Ravenswood Medical Center, she spent most mornings helping patients recover from injuries or surgery, but today was different. Today, Sophia had a new patient scheduled for an appointment, one who had been prescribed physical therapy by the hospital's doctors.

Sophia made her way to the treatment room on the second floor, where her assistant, Rachel, was already waiting with a cup of steaming coffee and a stack of paperwork. "Morning, Soph," Rachel said as Sophia slipped into her scrubs. "You've got your new patient this morning?"

Sophia nodded, feeling a sense of excitement mixed with trepidation. She had worked with many patients over the years, but there was something about this one that felt different. A faint sense of unease, perhaps, or a nagging worry that she didn't quite shake off.

As Sophia began to prepare for her patient's appointment, she couldn't help but wonder what lay ahead. Would this new patient be able to overcome the injuries that had left them sidelined? Or would they simply return to their old selves, forever changed by the accident?

The door to the treatment room slid open, and a young woman with piercing green eyes and a mop of curly brown hair walked in, looking nervous and uncertain. Sophia's gaze locked onto hers, and for a moment, they simply stared at each other.

"Hello," Sophia said finally, her voice soft but firm. "I'm Dr. Patel. Welcome to your appointment."
