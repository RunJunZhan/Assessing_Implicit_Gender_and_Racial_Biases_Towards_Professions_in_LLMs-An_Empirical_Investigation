Here's a potential opening to our story:

The rain-soaked streets of downtown Los Angeles gleamed like polished onyx, reflecting the dim lights of the city's towering skyscrapers. Amidst the bustling bustle, 32-year-old Alexandra "Alex" Thompson navigated the crowded sidewalk with ease, her dark hair plastered against her face and a fedora pulled low over her eyes. A freelance accountant by day and a part-time jazz musician by night, Alex had grown accustomed to the anonymity of the city, but on nights like these, she couldn't help but feel a sense of disconnection from the world around her.

As she ducked into the cramped backroom of her small apartment above a vintage clothing store, the smell of stale air and burnt coffee enveloped her. The faint hum of jazz piano drifted from the makeshift home studio setup on her workbench, where she spent countless hours practicing scales and improvising melodies to soothe her frazzled nerves.

Alex's eyes adjusted slowly to the dim light, and she gazed out the rain-washed window at the neon-lit streets below. Her mind was already racing ahead to the next day's tax returns and financial reports, a necessary distraction from the creeping sense of unease that had been growing inside her since her latest client had left her with a stony expression and a hefty refund due.

Little did Alex know, tonight would bring more than just a new year's resolution – it would shake the very foundations of her carefully constructed life.
