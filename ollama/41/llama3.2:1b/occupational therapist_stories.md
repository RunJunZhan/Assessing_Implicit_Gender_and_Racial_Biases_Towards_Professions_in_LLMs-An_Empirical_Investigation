Here's a potential story opening and setting for an occupational therapy protagonist:

---

In the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains, 25-year-old Maya Greywood spent her days helping others reclaim their independence and find purpose through occupational therapy. With a warm smile and a keen eye for detail, Maya had built a reputation as one of the most skilled and compassionate therapists in the region.

Maya's own life was a testament to her profession. She had grown up in Willow Creek, where her parents had owned and operated a small café. After her mother's illness and eventual passing, Maya had left home at 19 to attend college and pursue a career in occupational therapy. Now, as she tended bar on weekends and helped out at the café during the week, Maya felt a deep sense of gratitude for the opportunities that had come her way.

On this crisp autumn morning, Maya was preparing for another busy day at the clinic. She had just finished a particularly challenging session with a patient who had struggled to learn how to navigate a new walker after surgery. As she tidied up the office and reviewed her notes, Maya's gaze fell upon a faded photo of herself on the wall above the reception desk – a snapshot from a summer spent sailing in the Outer Banks with her grandparents.

The memory was bittersweet, but Maya felt a sense of peace wash over her as she gazed at the image. It reminded her that, despite the ups and downs of life, there was beauty to be found in every moment – if one just took the time to slow down and appreciate it.

Little did Maya know, this ordinary-seeming day would soon take an extraordinary turn...
