In the sleepy coastal town of Tidestone, where the sun dipped into the ocean like a paintbrush stroking the waves, 22-year-old Luna Nightingale worked as a graphic designer at the local publishing house. Her fingers flew across her desk, bringing the bustling streets of Tidestone to life on paper in a kaleidoscope of colors and typography. As she poured her heart and soul into each design, Luna felt a sense of contentment wash over her, a feeling that was hard to shake when the sound of seagulls crying overhead and the salty scent of the ocean filled her lungs.

Luna's small studio apartment above the publishing house was her sanctuary, a cozy retreat from the chaos of her daily routine. The walls were adorned with prints of her favorite artists, and the shelves were lined with stacks of dog-eared books on graphic design, art history, and the occasional novel she hoped to one day publish herself. Despite the comforts of her apartment, Luna's mind often wandered to her childhood dreams of becoming a published author, and the words that had once flowed so easily now seemed stuck in a tangled web of self-doubt and uncertainty.

As the first light of dawn crept into Tidestone, casting a warm glow over the sleepy town, Luna took a deep breath, feeling the familiar stirrings of creativity within her. She was ready to face another day as a graphic designer, and the promise of new projects and challenges lay ahead. But little did she know, today would bring more than just deadlines and design files – it would bring the arrival of a mysterious package with a single word that would change everything: "Echo."
