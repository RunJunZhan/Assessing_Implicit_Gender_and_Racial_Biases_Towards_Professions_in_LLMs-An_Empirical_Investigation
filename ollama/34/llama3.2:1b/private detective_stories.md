The rain poured down on the sleepy town of Ravenswood like a relentless curtain, shrouding its streets and homes in a damp, grey mist. It was a night just like any other, yet it felt like anything could happen on this particular evening. That's where 32-year-old private detective August "Augie" Blackwood was waiting.

Augie stood outside the dimly lit office of his agency, a small, unassuming building on Elm Street that was easily overlooked by the rain-soaked residents of Ravenswood. A tall, imposing figure with piercing blue eyes and jet-black hair, Augie had seen it all from behind his desk. He'd been a detective for over 20 years, and in that time, he'd developed a keen eye for detail and a knack for getting to the bottom of even the most seemingly inexplicable cases.

As he lit a cigarette, the sound of raindrops hitting the pavement outside seemed to match the rhythm of his thoughts. Augie's eyes narrowed as he gazed out into the darkness, his mind already starting to turn over the possibilities that this night might bring. He had a lead on a high-stakes case, one that could make or break him. And he was determined to get to the truth, no matter what it took.

Augie stepped inside, the door creaking shut behind him as he entered the familiar sanctuary of his office. The scent of cigarette smoke and stale coffee filled his nostrils, a comforting reminder that he'd been here before. Behind the desk, a stack of files and papers waited to be pored over, each one a potential puzzle piece in the larger mystery unfolding outside.

"Evening, Augie," a voice called out from the back of the room. It was his partner, Rachel "Raven" Thompson, a tough-as-nails ex-cop with a quick wit and an even quicker temper.

Augie raised an eyebrow as he turned to face her, his eyes locking onto hers like a magnet. "What've you got for me, Raven?"
