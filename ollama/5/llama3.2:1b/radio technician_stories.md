It was a drizzly autumn evening in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled deep within the heart of the Appalachian Mountains. The rain-soaked streets were empty, except for the flickering light of a lone diner sign creaking in the gentle breeze: "Buck's Place". Inside, Buck's warm smile and the hum of old radio equipment filled the air as Jack "Hawk" Hawkins, a ruggedly handsome 25-year-old radio technician, worked his magic on the antique broadcast studio.

Jack had grown up in Willow Creek, the youngest of five siblings to a family of humble farmers. After high school, he'd dropped out to join the US Navy, serving for two tours in Afghanistan before returning home to resume his life as a radio technician. Jack's unique blend of technical expertise and old-school charm made him a favorite among the locals at Buck's Place, where he spent most nights tinkerering with broken radios and dreaming up new shows.

As Hawk finished repairing a stubborn transmitter, the phone on the counter jolted to life, shattering the peaceful atmosphere. "Buck's Place", a deep voice barked, "We've got a code for you." Jack set down his tools, intrigued, and answered the call from the dispatcher: "Come on in, Hawk! There's a situation at the old Oakdale Manor. Reports of strange occurrences and lights flickering all over town... we need someone to investigate."
