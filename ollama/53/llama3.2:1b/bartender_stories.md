It was a warm summer evening in the small coastal town of Tidestone, where the sun had just set over the horizon and the smell of saltwater and coconut sunscreen wafted through the air. The sound of waves gently lapping against the shore created a soothing melody that seemed to match the rhythm of Emma Taylor's steady hand as she expertly mixed cocktails in her small bar, "The Cove."

Emma, a 28-year-old bartender with a warm smile and piercing green eyes, had grown up in Tidestone. She had always been drawn to the ocean, spending countless summers working at the local beachside restaurants and eventually opening her own spot, The Cove, where she could serve up her favorite drinks and watch the sunset over the water every evening.

As she polished a glass with a white towel, Emma's thoughts turned to the upcoming town festival, which was just around the corner. She had spent weeks planning the menu, decorations, and entertainment for the event, but now that it was finally near, she couldn't help but feel a mix of excitement and nerves. The festival was always a big deal in Tidestone, attracting tourists from all over and drawing in crowds that spilled onto the streets. Emma's bar would be bustling with people looking for a cool drink to enjoy while soaking up the atmosphere.

Just then, the door to The Cove swung open, and a tall, lanky figure walked in, his eyes scanning the room as if searching for something or someone. He was dressed in worn jeans and a faded band t-shirt, giving off a surfer-dude vibe that immediately put Emma on high alert. As he made his way towards her, a slow smile spread across his face, and he flashed her a quick wink before taking a seat at the bar.
