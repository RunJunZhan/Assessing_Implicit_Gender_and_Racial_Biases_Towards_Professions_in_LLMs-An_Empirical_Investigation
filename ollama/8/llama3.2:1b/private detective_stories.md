It was a drizzly autumn evening in the city of Ashwood, where the streets were lined with ancient trees that seemed to lean in, as if sharing secrets. The air was thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, and the only sound was the distant hum of traffic that had died down for the night.

In a small, cluttered office above a used bookstore on Main Street, Jack McCade, a private detective with over two decades of experience, sat at his desk, sipping a lukewarm cup of coffee. His eyes were fixed on the rain-soaked windowpane, where raindrops slid down like tiny diamonds.

Jack's name was not entirely unknown in Ashwood; he had a reputation for being one of the best in town, known for solving even the most complex and puzzling cases. But tonight, he wasn't here to solve a mystery or crack a safe. He was simply trying to drown out the silence that seemed to be closing in around him.

The rain pounded against the roof above, creating a soothing white noise that Jack had grown accustomed to over the years. It was a sound that reminded him of his childhood, growing up on the streets of Ashwood with his father, who had taught him everything he knew about survival and self-reliance. Now, as an adult, Jack ran his own detective agency, taking on cases that were too big for the police department to handle.

Just then, there was a knock at the door. It was Emily Patel, a young woman with piercing green eyes and raven-black hair, who had been Jack's business partner in the agency for five years. She looked tired, as if she hadn't slept in days.

"Jack, I need your help," she said, her voice low and urgent. "I just got a call from an old friend of mine, and it seems like this case has gotten out of control."

Without waiting for Jack to ask any questions, Emily handed him a crumpled piece of paper with a single sentence scrawled on it: "Meet me at the old warehouse on 5th and Main if you want to help."
