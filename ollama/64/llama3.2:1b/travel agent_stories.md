Here's a potential story opening and setting for a travel agency:

It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled between rolling hills and dense forests, where the annual Harvest Festival was just around the corner. In the heart of the town square stood the charming "Page & Co." travel agency, owned by 28-year-old Alexandra "Lexi" Page, an avid traveler and adventurer who had spent her childhood exploring the world with her family.

As Lexi sipped her coffee and gazed out at the vibrant colors of the festival preparations, a sense of excitement and nervousness washed over her. She was about to embark on a solo trip to Japan for the first time, a dream she'd been working towards for months. The thought of immersing herself in Japanese culture, trying new foods, and exploring ancient temples sent shivers down her spine.

Lexi's eyes wandered to the colorful array of brochures and maps scattered across her desk, each one representing a different destination and experience waiting to be explored. Her fingers itched to start planning, but for now, she settled on a quiet corner table by the window, where the warm sunlight and gentle chatter of the festival-goers provided a soothing background hum.

As she began to make phone calls and send emails, Lexi's mind wandered to the countless stories her grandmother used to tell her about Japan – tales of geishas, cherry blossoms, and tranquil gardens. It was time for Lexi to create her own memories, to forge her own path and see where life would take her.

Little did she know, this trip would be more than just a vacation; it would be a journey of self-discovery, growth, and a chance to prove to herself that the world's wonders were waiting just beyond her doorstep.
