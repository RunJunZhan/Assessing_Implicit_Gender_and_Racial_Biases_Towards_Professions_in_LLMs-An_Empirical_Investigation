It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakwood, nestled in the heart of rural Illinois. The sun was just starting to rise over the rolling hills and fields of golden wheat that stretched as far as the eye could see. In the midst of this peaceful landscape, 35-year-old Jeremiah "Jere" Thompson made his way across the dewy grass of Oakwood Gardens, the local residential neighborhood where he worked as a groundskeeper.

Jere had lived in Oakwood all his life, and he took great pride in keeping the town's beautiful properties looking their best. He knew every nook and cranny of the gardens, every curve and bend in the pathways, and every patch of overgrown vegetation that needed tending to. As he worked, he hummed a soft tune to himself, enjoying the quiet morning hours before the day got busy.

As he made his way up the path to the first house on his route, Jere noticed that something was off. The usually immaculate lawn was a bit trampled, and there were signs of recent foot traffic - a scuff mark on the front porch step and a faint splatter of mud on the sidewalk. Jere's instincts told him that something was amiss, but he wasn't sure what. He quickened his pace, his eyes scanning the neighborhood for any other signs of trouble.
