It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of rural Massachusetts. The sun was just starting to peek over the horizon, casting a warm glow over the quaint streets lined with ancient oak trees and weathered Victorian homes. The air was alive with the sweet scent of ripe apples from the local orchard, which had become a staple for the town's annual Apple Festival.

Amongst the bustle, Dr. Sophia Ellis stood in front of her office, a cozy haven filled with books, puzzles, and calming artwork that reflected her passion for helping others. As a school psychologist, Sophia had dedicated her career to supporting students, teachers, and families in Oakdale and beyond. With a warm smile and a gentle demeanor, she greeted the first client of the day, a nervous-looking boy named Ethan Thompson.

Ethan was a 12-year-old sophomore at Oakdale High School, struggling to cope with the recent changes in his life. His parents had announced their retirement, leaving him and his younger sister to navigate the challenges of independent living. The transition had been tough for Ethan, and he often found himself withdrawn and anxious, unsure of how to handle his emotions.

As Sophia began her session, she noticed a sense of unease emanating from Ethan's shoulders. He fidgeted in his seat, his eyes darting around the room as if searching for an escape route. Sophia took a deep breath, settling into her chair and launching into their conversation, "So, Ethan, tell me more about what's been going on with your parents' announcement. How are you feeling?"
