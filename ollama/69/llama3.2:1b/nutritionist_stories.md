Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel about a nutritionist:

The sun was just starting to peek over the horizon as Dr. Ava Moreno stepped out of her sleek black sedan and onto the cracked asphalt of the outskirts of town. She breathed in deeply, taking a moment to appreciate the sweet scent of blooming jasmine that wafted through the air, mingling with the faint tang of diesel fuel from the nearby truck stop. It was a place where people came to escape the hum of urban life, and Ava had grown accustomed to it over the years.

As she walked towards her apartment building, Ava couldn't help but think about how much she loved this small town in Southern California. She had come here after college, eager to pursue a career as a nutritionist at the local university. But now, with the stresses of running her own practice and the demands of mentoring new interns, she found herself feeling more disconnected from the world outside these four walls.

Ava lived in a cozy apartment on Elm Street, overlooking the lush greenery of the community garden that had sprung up around the building's courtyard. The garden was tended by a small group of volunteers, led by the charismatic and wise Elara Patel, who had become like a mentor to Ava herself when she first arrived in town.

As Ava approached her apartment door, she felt a sense of contentment wash over her. She unlocked the door and slipped inside, dropping her bag onto the floor with a soft thud. The apartment was tidy, as always, with every inch of surface cleared or organized. But despite its neatness, there was something about this space that made Ava feel at home – perhaps it was the way the sunlight streaming through the window illuminated the dust motes dancing in the air, or the sound of Elara's soft humming in the distance.

Little did Ava know, her life was about to take a dramatic turn, one that would challenge everything she thought she knew about herself and her place in the world.
