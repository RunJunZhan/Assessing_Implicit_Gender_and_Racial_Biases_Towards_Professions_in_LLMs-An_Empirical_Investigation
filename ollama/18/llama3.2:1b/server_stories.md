It was a chilly autumn evening in the quaint town of Willow Creek, nestled between two great rivers. The sun had long since set, casting a warm orange glow over the small streets and homes, but a faint mist rose from the water's edge, shrouding the scene in an ethereal mystery.

Ava Moreno wiped down the counter of the cozy restaurant, "The Cozy Cup," with a dirty rag, her mind drifting to the events that had led her here. As a server at this quaint eatery, Ava had grown accustomed to the rhythms of small-town life: the early morning wake-up calls from the sleepy townsfolk, the afternoon rush when the sun beat down on the patio, and the late-night lull when the regulars retired to their rooms.

Ava's thoughts were interrupted by a cheerful bell above the door, signaling the arrival of her next customer. She straightened her apron and smiled at the regular who always sat in the corner, sipping coffee and reading the newspaper. It was Jake Thompson, the owner of "The Cozy Cup," and Ava had grown fond of his dry wit and easy laugh over the past few years.

As she expertly juggled taking orders and refilling drinks, Ava's gaze drifted out into the night, her eyes scanning the deserted streets. The mist seemed to grow thicker, like a living entity that pulsed with secrets and stories. She felt a shiver run down her spine, a sense of foreboding that hinted at something more than just another ordinary evening in Willow Creek.

"Welcome to The Cozy Cup, Ava," Jake said, his voice warm as he slid onto the stool beside her. "What can I get you tonight?"
