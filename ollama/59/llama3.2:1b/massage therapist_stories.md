It was a crisp autumn morning in the quaint coastal town of Ravenswood, where the air was alive with the sweet scent of blooming cherry blossoms and the sound of seagulls crying overhead. The sun was just starting to rise over the horizon, casting a warm golden light over the sleepy streets and homes. In a small studio apartment above her family's bakery in the heart of town, Aurora "Rory" Wellesley prepared for another day of massages at her own unique wellness center, "Serenity Points".

Aurora, a 28-year-old massage therapist with long, curly brown hair and warm, hazel eyes, had always been drawn to the healing art of touch. Growing up in Ravenswood, she had spent countless hours exploring the coastline, collecting seashells and watching the sunset over the ocean. After completing her degree in massage therapy from the prestigious Coastal School of Massage, Rory had returned to her hometown to open Serenity Points, where she could share her gift with others and create a space that felt like a warm hug.

As she carefully arranged the candles and essential oils on the massage table, Rory took a deep breath, feeling a sense of calm wash over her. She loved being a massage therapist, not just for the physical release it brought to her clients, but also for the emotional connection she made with each person who walked through her door. It was moments like these that reminded her why she had become a therapist in the first place – to help people find peace and serenity in their lives.

Just as Rory was about to begin her first appointment of the day, there was a knock at the door. It was her best friend and business partner, Lila, who had stopped by to lend a hand with the morning rush. "Hey, girl!" Lila exclaimed, giving Rory a warm hug. "I brought you a fresh batch of pastries from the bakery. You need some fuel for this busy day ahead."
