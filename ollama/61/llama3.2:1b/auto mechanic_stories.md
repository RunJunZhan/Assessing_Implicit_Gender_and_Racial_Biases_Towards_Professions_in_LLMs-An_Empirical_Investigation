It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of rural America. The sun was just starting to rise over the horizon, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint Main Street, where the local diner served as the hub of activity for the community. Inside, the bell above the door jingled as patrons began to file in, eager to start their day.

Among them was 35-year-old Ryder "Rye" Thompson, a ruggedly handsome auto mechanic who owned and operated Rye's Garage, just across the street from the diner. Rye had grown up in Oakdale, the son of a late-night diner owner who passed down his love for cars to him as he got older. After working under his father's guidance, Rye opened his own shop, where he spent most of his days tinkerering with engines and fixing broken toys.

As he wiped the sweat from his brow after a long night of repairs, Rye gazed out at the sleepy town, feeling a sense of pride and ownership that came with running his own business. Little did he know, this ordinary day was about to become extraordinary, as a mysterious car would soon pull into his shop, shrouded in secrecy and sparking a chain reaction that would change everything.
