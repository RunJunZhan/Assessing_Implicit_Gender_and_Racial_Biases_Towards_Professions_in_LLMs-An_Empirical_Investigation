Here's a potential story opening and setting for a mystery novel featuring a stone cutter as the main character:

**Story Title:** The Quarry of Ravenswood

**Main Character:**

Full Name: Emmet "Mac" McCarthy
Age: 42
Occupation: Stone Cutter at Ravenswood Quarry, a small, rural quarry in the heart of Ireland's Wicklow Mountains

**Setting:**

The story takes place in late autumn, on a crisp, gray morning in October. The mist hangs low over the rolling hills and rugged mountainsides, casting a mystical gloom over the landscape. Mac McCarthy wakes up before dawn in his small cottage on the edge of Ravenswood Quarry, where he's lived for the past 20 years. He lives just a stone's throw from the quarry, which has been in his family for generations.

As he steps out into the chill air, the sound of hammers ringing out from the nearby quarry fills the morning silence. Mac heads to the kitchen to brew some coffee and check on the day's work. The fog is starting to clear, revealing a sea of rough-hewn stone walls stretching out as far as the eye can see.

Mac lives by his wits, relying on his knowledge of the land, the quarry, and the people who live in these hills. He knows every nook and cranny, every hidden corner and secret passage. But he's also aware that there are those who would seek to exploit this place, to mine the stone for their own gain.

As Mac begins his day's work at the quarry, he notices a strange piece of paper caught in a broken fence post near the entrance. He picks it up, smoothing out the creases and examining it. It appears to be a map, hand-drawn on a piece of rough-hewn parchment. The symbol for Ravenswood Quarry is emblazoned at the top, followed by a crude diagram of the quarry's layout.

Mac's curiosity is piqued. Who could have left this map? And what does it say about their intentions?
