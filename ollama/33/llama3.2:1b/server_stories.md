It was a warm summer evening in the small coastal town of Tidal Cove, where the sun dipped into the ocean, casting a golden glow over the bustling streets. The sound of seagulls filled the air as patrons spilled out onto the sidewalk, eager to grab a last-minute dinner before heading home. Amidst the chaos, one figure stood out - 22-year-old waitress, Avery Wellesley.

Avery had grown up in Tidal Cove her whole life, but after high school, she left to chase her dreams in the city. She worked as a server at the local favorite restaurant, The Crab Shack, to make ends meet while pursuing a degree in culinary arts. Her bright smile and infectious laugh had made many of her customers feel at ease, but Avery's own confidence was still a work in progress. Despite her natural charisma, she often found herself feeling overshadowed by her more outgoing colleagues.

As Avery polished a glass with a white towel, her gaze drifted out to the ocean, where a small sailboat bobbed gently in the distance. She took a deep breath, letting the salty air fill her lungs, and a sense of peace washed over her. This was what she had been missing - a taste of freedom, of adventure, and of something more than just a 9-to-5 job.

Just then, Avery's manager, Rachel, approached her with a concerned look on her face. "Avery, we've got a problem," Rachel said quietly. "One of our regulars is coming in right now, and I think he might be in trouble. He's been acting really strange all day...and I'm not sure what to do." Avery's heart skipped a beat as she exchanged a curious glance with the other servers, but before they could say anything, the door swung open, and a tall, handsome stranger strode in, his piercing blue eyes scanning the room.
