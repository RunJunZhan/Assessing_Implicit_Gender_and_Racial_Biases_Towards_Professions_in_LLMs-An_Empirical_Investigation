It was a crisp autumn evening in the bustling city of New Haven, where the smell of roasting chestnuts and freshly brewed coffee wafted through the air from the local cafes. The sun had just set, casting a warm orange glow over the cobblestone streets and historic buildings that lined the avenue. Amidst the hustle and bustle, 22-year-old server, Emilia "Mia" Thompson, worked at the trendy rooftop bar, aptly named "The Starlight", where she poured glasses of craft cocktails to a crowd of fashionable folks sipping on wine and champagne.

As Mia expertly juggled taking orders, refilling glasses, and delivering plates of appetizers, her mind wandered to the events of the day. She had been working at The Starlight for three years now, and it never got old – not even when she had to deal with demanding patrons or spilled drinks. Her coworkers had grown accustomed to her quick wit and infectious smile, which could turn a tense situation into a laugh-out-loud moment.

Mia's thoughts were interrupted by the arrival of her manager, Rachel, who was frantically waving Mia over from behind the bar. "We need you, Mia! Our VIP table is ready for 7:45 PM, but they're being super late. We've got a waiting list, and I don't know how much longer we can hold their coats!"
