It was a drizzly autumn evening in the small town of Oakdale, nestled deep within the English countryside. The streets were empty, save for the occasional stray dog wandering down Main Street, its tail wagging listlessly in the wind. In a world where time seemed to slow down on these quiet evenings, a lone figure emerged from the shadows of the local library.

Alexander "Alex" Thompson, a 28-year-old janitor with a passion for literature and a penchant for getting lost in the pages of his favorite novels, made his way through the musty halls, his worn coat clinging to him like a damp shroud. His dark hair was mussed, and his eyes seemed to hold a world of their own, as if the stories he loved so much were whispering secrets only he could hear.

As he pushed open the creaky door of the library, a warm golden light spilled out onto the sidewalk, illuminating the rain-soaked pavement. The sound of dripping water echoed through the empty streets, a soothing melody that accompanied Alex as he made his way to his small apartment above the library, a cozy refuge from the chill of the evening air.

The old building loomed above him, its stone walls bearing witness to countless nights like this one, when Alex would retreat into the world of words and let the stresses of everyday life fade away. But tonight, as he stood outside, lost in thought, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off – a sense of foreboding that had been building for weeks, ever since rumors had begun to circulate about a mysterious stranger who was said to be haunting the streets of Oakdale.
