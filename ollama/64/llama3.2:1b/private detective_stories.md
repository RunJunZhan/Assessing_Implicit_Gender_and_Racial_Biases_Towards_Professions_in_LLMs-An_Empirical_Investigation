It was a crisp autumn morning in the rain-soaked streets of New Haven, Connecticut, where the misty veil of dawn lifted to reveal the imposing facade of 14 East Main Street. This humble abode had been home to the enigmatic and reclusive private investigator, Julian Blackwood, for over two decades. The worn wooden sign above the door creaked in the gentle breeze, bearing the name "Blackwood Investigations" in elegant, golden lettering.

As Julian stepped out into the chilly air, he wrapped his fedora around his head, casting a shadow over his eyes as he gazed up at the gray sky. A thin layer of fog clung to the streets, casting long, sinuous shadows across the pavement. The city's sounds – the distant hum of traffic, the wail of sirens in the distance – blended together in a soothing melody that Julian had grown accustomed to over the years.

He strode down East Main Street, his eyes scanning the crowded sidewalks for any sign of his next case. A discreet glance at his watch revealed that he still had a few hours before he needed to meet with Mrs. Vivian Wellesley, the owner of the prestigious Rosewood Manor, about a matter of some urgency. Julian's phone buzzed in his pocket, breaking the silence. He pulled it out and answered, his voice low and smooth as he spoke into the receiver.
