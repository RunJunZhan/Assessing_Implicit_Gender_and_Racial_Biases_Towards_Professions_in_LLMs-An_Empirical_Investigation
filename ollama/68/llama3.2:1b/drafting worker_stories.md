It's a typical Monday morning in the small town of Oakdale, where the sun rises over the rolling hills and bustling streets. The local diner, "Mom's Place," serves as the hub for social interactions and daily routines. Inside, the aroma of freshly brewed coffee and sizzling bacon fills the air, accompanied by the warm smiles of the friendly waitress, Karen.

In a small office on the outskirts of town, 27-year-old drafting worker, Ethan Thompson, sits hunched over his desk, staring blankly at his computer screen. His fingers drum impatiently against the keyboard as he tries to meet the looming deadline for a major contract. The drab office decor – a mishmash of beige walls and bland furniture – seems to blend into the background as Ethan's mind wanders to his upcoming weekend plans with his best friend, Alex.

Ethan Thompson is a talented drafting artist, but his small-town roots have limited his professional opportunities. Despite his passion for designing, he works part-time at "Mom's Place" to make ends meet and save up for his dream of starting his own design firm. As he types away, Ethan can't help but feel the weight of responsibility on his shoulders – not just for his job, but also for the sake of his friend Alex, who has been struggling to find work in a rapidly changing industry.

As the morning light streams through the windows, casting a warm glow over the office, Ethan takes a deep breath and attempts to refocus. He knows that today is going to be a long day, but he's ready to put in the effort needed to meet his deadline and get back on track with his life-long goal.
