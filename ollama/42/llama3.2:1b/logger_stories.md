Here's a potential story opening and setting for a logging-themed novel:

The misty dawn crept over the forest like a damp shroud, casting an eerie gloom over the towering trees that loomed in every direction. Jack "Hawk" Hawkins, a grizzled logger with over two decades of experience under his belt, emerged from the darkness, his eyes scanning the forest floor for any sign of movement. His rugged calloused hands, worn smooth by years of working with chainsaws and axe heads, gripped his trusty logger's knife tightly as he trudged through the knee-deep underbrush.

Hawk's hometown, Ravenswood, was a small logging town on the edge of the dense forest that surrounded it. The townspeople knew Hawk as a hard worker and a loyal friend to the forest, but few understood the true depths of his dedication. For Hawk, there was no greater thrill than getting his hands dirty and living off the land – or rather, the trees that lived upon it.

As he walked, the faint scent of pine and earth filled his nostrils, transporting him back to a time when life had been simpler, and the world seemed less complicated. It was moments like these that Hawk cherished, when the only worry was what game to catch for dinner or how many days until the next harvest.

But the forest wasn't always this way. It had changed over the years – some would say it had become a monster, encroaching on the town and threatening to consume everything in its path. The logging industry was dying, and Hawk knew that if he didn't find a new angle, his livelihood might be gone for good.

The question was, how far would Hawk go to protect Ravenswood's way of life?
