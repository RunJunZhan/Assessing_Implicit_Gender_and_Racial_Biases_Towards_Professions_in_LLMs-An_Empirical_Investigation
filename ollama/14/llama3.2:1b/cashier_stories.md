It was a typical Friday afternoon at the Silver Oak Mall, with shoppers milling about outside the food court and store windows displaying last night's sales. Inside the bustling mall, 22-year-old Jamie Thompson scanned a stack of dollar bills with precision, her dark hair pulled back into a ponytail as she worked. She had just finished a long shift at her job as a cashier at the cosmetics counter, and was looking forward to unwinding with a cup of coffee from the mall's café.

As the lunch rush subsided, Jamie took a moment to tidy up the register, straightening up the displays of makeup and hair accessories before moving on to the next customer. She had worked at the Silver Oak Mall for about two years now, starting out as a part-time cashiers during her summer breaks from college. Over time, she had graduated to full-time positions in various stores within the mall.

As Jamie finished her task, she caught a glimpse of herself in the mirror behind the counter and smiled wryly. She looked like a typical 22-year-old cashier - tired eyes, sunken cheeks, and a hint of a smudge on her left cheekbone from staring at computers all day. But for now, that was okay. She just wanted to relax and enjoy her evening free from the stresses of work.
