Here's a potential story opening and setting for a counseling psychology novel:

---

Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson had always been drawn to the complexities of the human mind. As a child, she spent hours poring over her mother's old self-help books, fascinated by the secrets they whispered about the art of therapy. Now, at 35, Lexi worked as a licensed professional counselor in a small town on the coast, where the weather was always grey and the residents were always struggling.

Lexi's own life had been marked by struggles of its own – her father's death from cancer when she was just a teenager, and the subsequent emotional turmoil that followed. She had always found solace in helping others navigate their own inner worlds, but it wasn't until she turned 30, after years of working in corporate counseling, that she finally decided to take the leap and pursue her true passion: private practice.

With her small office nestled behind a quaint bookstore in the town's main street, Lexi was surrounded by bookshelves stacked with everything from Jungian theory to self-help bestsellers. It was here, amidst the musty scent of old books and the soft hum of espresso machines, that she would meet the next chapter of her own story – one that would take her on a journey of discovery, healing, and ultimately, transformation.

As Lexi began her day, she gazed out at the rain-soaked streets, feeling the familiar tug of her own heartstrings. It was going to be another long, quiet morning, filled with the promise of new beginnings – but also the weight of old fears and doubts waiting in the shadows...
