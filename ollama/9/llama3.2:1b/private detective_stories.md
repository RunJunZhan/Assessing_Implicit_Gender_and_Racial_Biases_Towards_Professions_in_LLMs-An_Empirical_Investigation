It was a drizzly autumn evening in the city of Ashwood, a metropolis shrouded in mist and mystery. The rain-soaked streets were lined with towering skyscrapers that seemed to lean in, as if listening to the whispers of the past. Amidst this eerie backdrop, a lone figure emerged from the shadows.

Roderick "Rod" Thompson was a private detective with a reputation for being one step ahead of the game. His tailored suit and fedora hat made him look like a million bucks, but Rod knew the truth - he was just as much a product of the city's darkness as anyone else.

Rod's office, a nondescript storefront on 5th Street, was a testament to his no-nonsense approach to solving crimes. The air inside reeked of stale cigarettes and stale coffee, a far cry from the fancy suits and high-end amenities that usually greeted clients in more affluent areas. But Rod didn't care - this was where he came to face the demons of Ashwood.

As he sat at his desk, a large window overlooking the city's waterway offered a view of the mist-shrouded river. The sound of raindrops pattering on the roof above and the hum of traffic from the nearby highway created a symphony that seemed to lull the city into a uneasy sleep. It was moments like these that Rod felt most at home - in the midst of it all, where the only rule was survival.

Just as he was about to take another sip of his lukewarm coffee, the phone on his desk jolted him out of his reverie. "Rod," he said, picking up the receiver and listening intently for a moment before responding in a low tone. "Tell her...".
