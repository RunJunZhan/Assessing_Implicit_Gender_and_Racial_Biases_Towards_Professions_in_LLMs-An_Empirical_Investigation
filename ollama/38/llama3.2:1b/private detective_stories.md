It was a chilly autumn evening in the city of New Haven, where the streets were lined with old brick buildings and the sound of crunching leaves beneath foot echoed through the crisp air. The sun had just set, casting a warm orange glow over the bustling metropolis, but it seemed to be quickly fading away as the darkness gathered.

Private detective Evelyn Wellesley stood outside her office, a modest two-story building nestled between a vintage bookstore and a small antique shop. The sign above the door read "Wellesley Investigations" in elegant script, and the windows were lined with a collection of dusty old books that seemed to be calling out to her. A black sedan pulled up outside, its tinted windows reflecting the dim light of the street lamps.

Evelyn Wellesley, a woman in her mid-40s with short, dark hair and piercing green eyes, stepped out onto the sidewalk, her eyes scanning the area before focusing on the sedan's driver, a tall, imposing figure with chiseled features and a sharp jawline. He was dressed in a black leather jacket and jeans, his hair pulled back into a tight ponytail, giving him an air of quiet confidence.

"Mr...?" Evelyn asked, her voice low and even, as she approached the driver.

The driver hesitated for a moment before responding, "James. James Reed."

Evelyn nodded, her eyes never leaving his face. "Welcome to Wellesley Investigations, Mr. Reed. Can I help you with something?"

As they stepped into the office building, Evelyn felt a sense of unease settle in the pit of her stomach. Something wasn't right about this case yet...
