Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel:

The city of New Haven lay shrouded in an eternal twilight, its streets and buildings seeming to absorb the last rays of sunlight like a parched soul. The year was 1850, and the city was still reeling from the aftermath of the Great Devastation, a catastrophic event that had left widespread destruction and famine in its wake.

As the sun dipped below the horizon, casting the city in a deep crimson hue, Guard Thaddeus Wychwood stood watch on the outskirts of town. His eyes, a piercing yellow, scanned the rooftops and alleys with a mixture of habit and curiosity, his rugged features etched by years of service to the city's government. A grizzled veteran of countless midnight patrols, Thaddeus had grown accustomed to the silence that fell between the stars, but he never lost his sense of unease.

Thaddeus's days began before dawn, when the city's inhabitants retreated into their homes and businesses, leaving only the hard light of the gas lamps to illuminate the streets. As a guard, he was tasked with keeping order and maintaining the fragile peace that existed between the rival factions of New Haven: the wealthy elite who controlled the city's infrastructure; and the working class, who toiled in the factories and fields.

Tonight, Thaddeus stood at attention on the city gate, his torch casting flickering shadows on the walls as he listened for any sign of trouble. The air was heavy with tension, and the guard's instincts told him that something was brewing.
