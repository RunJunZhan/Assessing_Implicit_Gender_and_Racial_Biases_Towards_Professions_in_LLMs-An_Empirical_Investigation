It was a drizzly autumn evening in the rain-soaked streets of New Haven, Connecticut. The kind of night that made you want to stay indoors with a good book and a cup of hot coffee. But for private investigator Jameson "Jim" Mitchell, there was no time for such comforts. His eyes, a piercing blue, scanned the crowded streets as he navigated through the dimly lit alleys, his earpiece crackling to life in front of him.

"Jim, we've got a hit," his partner, Rachel Kim, said, her voice low and urgent. "A wealthy businessman's mansion on Elm Street. He's been getting threatening phone calls all day."

Mitchell's gaze narrowed as he quickened his pace, the wet pavement squelching beneath his feet. He had a reputation for being one of the best in the business, and he always got his man...or woman. Or, in this case, whatever it was they were dealing with.

As he turned onto Elm Street, Mitchell's eyes landed on the grand mansion, its windows like empty eyes staring back at him. He spotted a black sedan parked outside, a nondescript car that looked more like a rental than a luxury vehicle. Something about it didn't sit right with him.

Mitchell pulled up to the mansion and stepped out of his own car, the wet air clinging to his skin like a damp shroud. He surveyed the area, taking in the manicured lawns and overgrown bushes. This was going to be an interesting case.
