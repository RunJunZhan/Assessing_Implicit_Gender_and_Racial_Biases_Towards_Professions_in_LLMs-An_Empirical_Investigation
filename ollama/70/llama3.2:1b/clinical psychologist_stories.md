The fog crept in off the dimly lit streets of Ravenswood, shrouding the city's oldest district in a damp, grey mist. Dr. Astrid Vega stood at her window, gazing out at the deserted sidewalks as she sipped her lukewarm coffee. It was a chilly autumn morning, and the usual bustle of Ravenswood's residents had given way to an eerie quietness.

As a clinical psychologist specializing in the mental health of the city's working-class population, Astrid's practice office was a cozy haven amidst the foggy streets. Her worn leather armchair sat at the end of her small, cluttered study, surrounded by stacks of dusty psychology textbooks and scattered notes on stress management techniques.

Astrid's mind, however, was elsewhere - preoccupied with the latest case files from the past week. A young mother, Sarah Jenkins, had been admitted to the hospital with symptoms of postpartum depression. As Astrid delved into Sarah's story, she couldn't shake the feeling that there was more to the case than met the eye.

The fog outside seemed to be seeping into her office, casting a gloomy shadow over her thoughts as she began to unravel the tangled threads of Sarah's narrative...
