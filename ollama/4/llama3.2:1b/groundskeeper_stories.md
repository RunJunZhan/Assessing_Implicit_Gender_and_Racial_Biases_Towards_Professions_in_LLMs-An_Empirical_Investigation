It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains. The sun rises over the rolling hills and lush green pastures, casting a warm golden light over the sleepy town. In the midst of this serene landscape, 25-year-old Jaxon "Jax" Thompson stands tall among the rows of verdant lawns, his worn denim jacket flapping in the gentle breeze as he surveys his domain.

As the groundskeeper for Willow Creek's prestigious Oakwood Manor, Jax has spent countless hours tending to the estate's meticulously manicured gardens and immaculately maintained courtyards. His rugged good looks and rough-hewn hands have earned him a reputation among the townspeople as a man who gets the job done, no matter what the task may be.

Jax's eyes narrow slightly as he gazes out at the manor's sprawling grounds, his mind already turning to the day's tasks ahead. The annual Harvest Festival is just around the corner, and Oakwood Manor is in desperate need of a thorough spring cleaning to ensure it shines like new for the big event. With a deep breath, Jax sets off towards the manor's entrance, his boots crunching on the gravel path as he prepares to tackle another day in paradise.
