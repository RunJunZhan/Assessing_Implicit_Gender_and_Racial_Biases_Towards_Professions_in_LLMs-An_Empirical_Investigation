Here's a potential story opening:
In the sweltering summer of 1985, in the heart of New York City's Theater District, a brilliant and fiery young woman named Augusta "Gus" Everett prepared for the most anticipated production of her career: a new musical adaptation of Shakespeare's "A Midsummer Night's Dream". As the director of the prestigious Mercury Theatre Company, Gus was determined to bring this beloved classic to life in a way that would surpass all expectations. With her sharp mind, quick wit, and infectious enthusiasm, Gus had a gift for coaxing even the most reluctant actors into their characters' depths.

Gus lived in a modest but stylish brownstone apartment overlooking the bright lights of Broadway, surrounded by the works of the great playwrights and the endless stream of scripts she'd devoured over the years. Her own theatrical ambitions were fueled by a fierce determination to prove herself as one of the city's top directors, and to leave her mark on the world of theater.

As Gus stood in front of her sprawling living room, surrounded by sketches for "A Midsummer Night's Dream" and scraps of torn tissue from late-night script revisions, she felt a sense of excitement and trepidation wash over her. She knew that this production would be a turning point in her career – a chance to showcase her talents to the world, and to challenge herself in ways she never thought possible. Little did she know, however, that this summer's most magical - and unpredictable - project was just around the corner, threatening to upend everything she thought she knew about theater, love, and the power of imagination...
