Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel featuring a rugged but kind-hearted roofer:

The small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains, was never more than a few miles from the unforgiving wilderness that surrounded it. The air was thick with the scent of pine and damp earth, and the only sounds were the creaks and groans of old wooden buildings and the distant howl of wolves.

At the local roofing supply store, "Ridgeview Hardware," Jack "Hawk" Hawkins was busy setting up for another day of work. With his calloused hands and thick brown hair, Hawk had built a reputation in Oakdale as one of the toughest, most reliable roofers in the business. He'd spent years honing his craft on his own family's farm before striking out on his own, and he took pride in every shingle he laid.

As Hawk worked to secure a stubbornly stubborn roof, the phone rang, shrill in the stillness of the morning. He answered it, his deep voice growling with a mixture of annoyance and familiarity as he listened to the caller's words. It was Sheriff Jenkins again, asking him to come out to investigate a strange noise coming from the old windmill on the outskirts of town.

Hawk snorted, rubbing his temples in frustration. Another mystery for another day. But something about the sheriff's tone told him that this might be more than just a simple windmill problem...
