It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the leaves on the trees rustled softly in the gentle breeze like a chorus of whispers. The sun was just starting to peek over the horizon, casting a warm golden light over the quaint streets and homes. In the heart of this charming town, 35-year-old landscaper, Ethan Thompson, stirred outside his family's old ranch-style home, nestled among the rows of neatly manicured lawns.

Ethan lived with his parents, Rachel and Mark, in the house that had been passed down to him from his grandfather. His father was a retired carpenter, and Ethan learned the trade from him as a child. He took pride in making sure every lawn in Willow Creek looked its best, often going above and beyond to ensure his clients were satisfied with his work.

As Ethan stepped outside into the cool morning air, he breathed in deeply, feeling the scent of damp earth and fresh cut grass fill his lungs. He stretched his arms and yawned, feeling a sense of contentment wash over him. Today was supposed to be a busy day – a wedding at the town hall, followed by a private garden party for one of the clients' families. But before he could even start prepping for the events, Ethan's phone buzzed with an urgent text from his boss: "Ethan, we need you to take care of something in one of our commercial properties. Can you make it your priority?"
