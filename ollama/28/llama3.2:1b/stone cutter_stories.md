It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Raven's Peak, nestled deep within the rugged mountains. The air was alive with the sweet scent of ripe apples and the gentle chirping of birds, as if the very forest itself was awaking from its summer slumber. In a small stone cottage on the outskirts of town, nestled between two great boulders that had weathered countless storms, lived Alistair "Blackjack" McTavish, a renowned stone cutter with hands as rough as the granite he worked with.

Alistair's fingers were stained crimson from years of working with iron pyrite, his go-to stone for cutting and shaping into magnificent works of art. He lived and breathed his craft, having learned the trade from his father and grandfather before him. The stories they told around the fireplace, of ancient battles won and lost, of mighty mountains shaped by human hands – these were the tales that fueled Alistair's passion for stone.

As he emerged from the cottage, a wispy cloud of dust swirled around his feet, carried by the gentle breeze that rustled through the trees. The sunlight danced across his rugged features, accentuating the sharp planes of his face and the deep creases etched into his skin from years of working in the unforgiving mountains. Alistair's bright blue eyes sparkled with a hint of mischief, as if he knew secrets only the stones themselves understood.

With a grunt, he shouldered his leather satchel, slung over his shoulder, and set out to face another day of shaping the very rocks that would become his masterpieces. Little did he know, today would bring a new challenge – one that would test his skills, push him to his limits, and perhaps, reveal secrets hidden deep within the stones themselves.
