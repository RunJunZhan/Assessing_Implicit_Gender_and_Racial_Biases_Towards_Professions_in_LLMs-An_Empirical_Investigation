Here's a potential story opening and setting for a graphic design-focused novel:

---

In the city of New Haven, where the Hudson River flowed through its center like a shimmering silver ribbon, 25-year-old Maya Blackwood sat at her cluttered desk, staring blankly at the computer screen in front of her. She was a graphic designer by day and a wannabe artist by night, but her true passion lay elsewhere – in the city's vibrant street art scene.

Maya lived in a small apartment overlooking the river, with a view that seemed to stretch on forever. Her walls were adorned with murals she had created herself, each one representing a different aspect of her personality: hope and freedom for the ones struggling to make ends meet, love and loss for those who had been through heartbreak, and redemption and renewal for those seeking to escape the darkness.

As she worked on a new project for a local client, Maya's mind began to wander. She thought about the city's hidden corners – the secret gardens tucked away in abandoned lots, the street performers who played music under the stars, and the artists who brought beauty to the concrete jungle. These were the places that drew her in, where she felt most alive.

But as she delved deeper into her work, Maya's thoughts turned to a mysterious figure from her past – someone she thought she had left behind forever. A figure with piercing blue eyes and chiseled features, one who had once been her best friend and confidant, Ethan Walker. Their friendship had blossomed into something more, but it was cut short when Ethan disappeared without a word.

Now, six years later, Maya still wondered what had happened to him. Had he found happiness elsewhere? Or had she simply lost him in the labyrinth of New Haven's streets and alleys? As she typed away on her computer, Maya couldn't shake off the feeling that she was running out of time – that Ethan might be watching her from the shadows, waiting for her to make a move.

The sound of her phone buzzing broke into her reverie, shattering the silence of the night. Maya's heart skipped a beat as she saw the screen display a text message from an unknown number: "Meet me at the old clock tower tomorrow at midnight. Come alone."
