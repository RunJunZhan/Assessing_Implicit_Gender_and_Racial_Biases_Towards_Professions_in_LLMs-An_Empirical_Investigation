Professor Emilia Aurelia Grey stood at the edge of the mist-shrouded moors, her eyes fixed on the worn stone walls of Ravenswood Manor that loomed before her. The autumn leaves crunched beneath her feet as she breathed in the crisp air, the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves carrying her back to a place she thought she'd left behind.

As a professor of English literature at the local university, Emilia had spent years studying the works of the British masters – Austen, Eliot, and Brontë, among others. But it was on these very moors that she'd spent countless hours as a student, pouring over texts and essays in her small dormitory room above the manor's kitchen.

Now, at the ripe old age of 55, Emilia felt the call of the moors – the wind, the wildflowers, the silence. She'd left Ravenswood Manor behind years ago to pursue her career, but a part of her had always remained, tied to this place and its people.

As she gazed out at the manor's windows, Emilia noticed that they were darkening – the curtains were being drawn, and the fire was burning low. She felt a sense of unease creeping over her; was it just her imagination, or did someone – or something – else move in the shadows?
