Here's a potential start to a story:

In the sweltering summer of 2035, in the bustling metropolis of New Haven, 32-year-old Maya Jensen sat at her desk, surrounded by the hum of computers and the glow of screens that illuminated the late afternoon sun streaming through the office windows. A systems analyst for a major tech firm, Maya had spent countless hours poring over lines of code, troubleshooting network issues, and designing database schema. But as she delved into her work, her mind began to wander to the strange occurrences that had been plaguing the city's infrastructure just days prior.

It started with minor glitches – a flicker in the water treatment plant, a brief loss of internet connectivity in a particularly busy shopping district. But as the incidents piled up, Maya realized that something was amiss. The systems analysts at New Haven were being called upon to investigate, and Maya had been chosen for her expertise.

With a sense of trepidation, Maya stood up from her chair, stretched her cramped muscles, and began to prepare for what promised to be a long night's work. As she walked to the elevator, she couldn't shake the feeling that something more sinister was at play – something that only came alive in the shadows, waiting to strike.

The city outside seemed to grow quieter as Maya rode the elevator up to her floor. She felt a shiver run down her spine as the doors slid open and she stepped into her small apartment. The air conditioning unit hummed softly in the corner, its gentle tick-tock a welcome respite from the heat that seemed to cling to everything it touched.

Maya took a deep breath, feeling a sense of determination wash over her. She had always prided herself on being a problem-solver, and she was ready to face whatever lay ahead – no matter how strange or unsettling.
