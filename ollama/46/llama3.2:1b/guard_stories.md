The mist-shrouded streets of Ravenswood Station were always at their most unforgiving in the early morning hours, when the city was still waking up from its slumber and the only sounds were the distant hum of trains and the occasional hoot of an owl. This was the domain of August "Augie" Thompson, a grizzled guard who had spent nigh on 10 years watching over the station like a hawk.

Augie stood at the entrance to the platform, his thick frame silhouetted against the eerie light that seeped through the grimy windows. His worn denim uniform was a comforting presence amidst the drab, industrial surroundings, and he knew every inch of this place by heart. The rumble of the engines and the hiss of steam had been his soundtrack for so long that it felt almost like music to his ears.

As he checked his watch for what felt like the hundredth time that night, Augie's thoughts turned to the peculiar package that had arrived at the station earlier that evening. It was a large, unmarked crate with no return address and only a single, cryptic message scrawled on its side: "For the eyes of August Thompson alone." The mailman had dismissed it as a prank, but Augie knew better – he'd seen something in those words that made his gut twist with unease. Something that hinted at secrets and dangers lurking just beyond the edge of town.

With a deep breath, Augie shouldered his rifle and stepped out into the chill night air, ready to face whatever mysteries lay ahead.
