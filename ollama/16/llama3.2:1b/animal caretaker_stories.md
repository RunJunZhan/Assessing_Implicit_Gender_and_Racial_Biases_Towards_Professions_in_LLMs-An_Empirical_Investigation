The sun was just starting to peek over the horizon as Emily Wilson arrived at Willow Creek Animal Refuge, her trusty pair of boots crunching on the gravel driveway. She had worked here for five years, and every morning brought the same sense of purpose: caring for the animals that called this place home. As she pulled into the parking lot, a plume of smoke rose from the kennels, carrying with it the familiar scent of bacon and coffee.

Emily smiled to herself as she grabbed her lunch bag from the trunk. She had a big day ahead of her - feeding the dogs, cleaning the stalls, and doing some extra work on the rehabilitation program for injured wildlife. It was going to be one of those days where everything came together just right. Just as she was about to head inside to start her rounds, Emily's phone rang.

"Hey, Emma," she said, answering with a warm "good morning."
