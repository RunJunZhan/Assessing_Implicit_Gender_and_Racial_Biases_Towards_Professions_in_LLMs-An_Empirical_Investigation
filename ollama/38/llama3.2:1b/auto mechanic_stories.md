It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of rural America. The streets are empty, save for the occasional truck rumbling down Main Street as it makes its way to the local garage where Jack "Havoc" Harris fixes cars and tells tales by the light of a flickering diner sign.

The sun rises over the rolling hills, casting a warm glow over the worn asphalt and rusty metal that lines the streets. A haze of exhaust fumes and greasy oil hangs heavy in the air, carrying with it the smell of gasoline and the promise of another day's work on the family business.

In a small, cluttered office above the garage, Jack Harris sits at his desk, sipping coffee and staring out the window at the chaos that is Oakdale. The town is always bustling, but on days like this, the rhythms of its residents seem to come alive. Children chatter excitedly as they ride their bikes down Main Street, their parents hurrying them home with bags of fresh-baked bread or a thermos of coffee. The local diner, where Jack works, is already starting to fill with the morning crowd, and the aroma of sizzling bacon and hash browns wafts through the air, making his stomach growl in anticipation.

As he stares out the window, Jack's mind begins to wander back to last night's strange call from the sheriff. A local farmer had reported a suspicious vehicle seen speeding down Main Street, weaving in and out of lanes and nearly crashing into several parked cars. The sheriff thought it might be a joyride gone wrong, but something about the story didn't add up. Jack had always suspected that Oakdale was hiding secrets, and now he couldn't shake the feeling that this night's events were more than just a wild goose chase.
