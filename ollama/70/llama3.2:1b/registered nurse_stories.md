In the heart of the bustling city of New Haven, where the sounds of car horns and chatter filled the air, Registered Nurse Ava Moreno stood at the front desk of her family's small medical practice, "Merends' Medical," on the second floor of a quaint apartment building. The sun was setting over the towering skyscrapers, casting a warm orange glow through the large windows that overlooked the city streets below.

Ava, a 28-year-old Mexican-American woman with long, dark hair and expressive brown eyes, had spent years honing her skills as a nurse, working in various hospitals and clinics across the country. But she never thought she'd end up running her own practice, surrounded by the people and the city that had become her home.

As she typed away on her computer, Ava's mind began to wander back to the day she had saved the life of a young patient, a 25-year-old woman who had suffered a severe allergic reaction. The memory still brought a mix of emotions – relief, pride, and a sense of purpose that Ava couldn't quite put into words.

Just as she was about to take a break and head home to her family's apartment across town, the doorbell above the entrance chime let out a jarring ring, signaling the arrival of their first patient for the evening. Ava took a deep breath, smoothing out her crisp white lab coat and adjusting her glasses before getting up from her chair to answer the door.

"Merends' Medical," she said warmly, flashing a bright smile at the young woman who stood on the other side of the door, clutching a crumpled medical record in one hand and fumbling with a pen in the other.
