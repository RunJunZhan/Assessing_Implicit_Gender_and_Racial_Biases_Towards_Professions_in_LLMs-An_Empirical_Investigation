Here's a potential story opening and setting for a mystery novel:

The rain-soaked streets of Ashwood City glistened like polished onyx as Jack McCade, a grizzled and seasoned private detective with over two decades of experience, stepped out of his worn sedan and onto the cracked sidewalk in front of the rundown office building. The once-thriving hub of commerce had long since faded into decline, its heyday long behind it, but McCade had always been drawn to the shadows that lingered within its crumbling walls.

As he lit a cigarette, the acrid smell wafting up mingled with the stench of damp earth and decay. The air was heavy with the weight of secrets, and McCade knew better than to let his intuition mislead him on this particular day. He was on a mission to track down the source of a cryptic message that had been received by one of his clients, a reclusive millionaire named Julian Blackwood.

The message itself had been delivered via an encrypted email, and Blackwood's frantic phone call had been the first sign of trouble in what promised to be a long and complicated investigation. McCade's gut told him that something was amiss, but he needed more information before he could take on the challenge.

With a sharp gaze scanning the deserted streets, McCade pulled out his wallet and slotted a few bills into his pocket, readying himself for the journey ahead. The rain showed no signs of letting up, and he knew that Ashwood City was about to get a lot wetter – and a whole lot darker.
