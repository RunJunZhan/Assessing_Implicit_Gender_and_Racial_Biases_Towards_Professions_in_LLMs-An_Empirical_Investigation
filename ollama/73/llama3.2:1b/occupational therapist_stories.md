It was a crisp autumn morning in the quaint town of Oakwood, nestled in the heart of rural Pennsylvania. The leaves on the oak trees outside Dr. Sophia Ellis's office window were a vibrant amber hue, and the air was alive with the sweet scent of ripe fruit from the orchards that lined the street.

Dr. Sophia Ellis, a warm and compassionate occupational therapist in her mid-30s, sat at her desk, sipping her coffee as she reviewed her schedule for the day. She had been working with patients at Oakwood Children's Hospital for over five years, and she knew every nook and cranny of this small town. The hospital was a hub of activity, with young minds full of wonder and potential waiting to be unleashed.

As she prepared for another busy day of assessment, therapy sessions, and consultation with the medical team, Sophia felt a sense of contentment wash over her. This was what she loved about her job – the opportunity to make a real difference in people's lives, one hand move at a time. And yet, despite the joy that came with her work, Sophia couldn't shake off the feeling that something was amiss. Little did she know, today would bring more than just another typical day of patient care...
