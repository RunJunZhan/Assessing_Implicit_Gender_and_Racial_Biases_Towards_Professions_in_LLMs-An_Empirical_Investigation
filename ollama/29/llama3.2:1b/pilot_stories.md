Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel about a pilot:

**Title:** "The Sky's Edge"

**Main Character:** Captain Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson

**Story Opening:**

April 15th, 2023 - 08:00 AM EST

Lexi Thompson stood at the controls of her Cessna Citation X, her eyes scanning the horizon as she leveled out at 30,000 feet. The sun was just starting to rise over the rolling hills of rural Colorado, casting a warm glow over the landscape. Lexi felt a sense of peace wash over her, the sound of the engines humming in perfect sync with the gentle stirring of the clouds.

It had been a long journey, from the cramped cockpits of military transport planes to the comfortable confines of her own home in the small town of Crested Butte. Lexi had always loved flying, but after leaving the Air Force, she'd taken on a part-time gig as a charter pilot to supplement her income and travel whenever possible.

As she gazed out at the endless expanse of sky, Lexi felt a sense of excitement building in her chest. She was scheduled to make a pickup for a group of adventurers from Aspen, bound for a remote mountain lodge deep in the San Juan Mountains. The weather forecast was looking good, with plenty of clear skies and light winds expected throughout the day.

Lexi's mind began to wander as she pondered the evening ahead - a champagne reception at the lodge, followed by a night spent swapping stories and sharing laughs with her clients. It was moments like these that reminded her why she loved being a pilot so much: the freedom of flight, the thrill of adventure, and the chance to connect with people from all walks of life.

Just as Lexi was about to start her descent into the lodge, her radio crackled to life over the intercom. "Lexi, this is Aspen - we have a situation developing in Sector 7. Reports of a rogue snowboarder causing chaos on the mountain roads. Requesting your presence at the Aspen Airport."

Lexi's eyes snapped back to attention as she banked the plane hard to the left, heading straight for the Aspen Airport radar site...
