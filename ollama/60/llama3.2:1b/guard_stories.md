The misty veil of morning shrouded the abandoned asylum on Ravenwood Island, casting an eerie gloom over the crumbling structure that had stood for decades as a testament to human suffering and neglect. The year was 1955, and the whispers of its dark past still lingered in the air like the faint scent of decay.

Against this bleak backdrop, Captain Elijah "Hawk" Hawkins stood at the entrance of the asylum's main hall, his eyes scanning the shadows for any sign of movement. A veteran guard with years of experience, Hawk had been tasked with escorting a group of patients and their attendants through the decaying halls to a scheduled transfer to the mainland's mental institution.

Hawk was a man in his late forties, ruggedly handsome with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes that seemed to bore into those he met. His dark hair was cropped short, revealing a prominent scar above his left eyebrow, which he had obtained during his early days as a soldier. He wore his worn uniform with pride, adorned with the emblem of Ravenwood Island's former medical authorities.

As he stood at the entrance, Hawk felt a shiver run down his spine. Something didn't feel right about this transfer.
