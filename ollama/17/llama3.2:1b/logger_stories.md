It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled deep within the heart of the Pacific Northwest forest. The year is 1922, and the logging industry is thriving, with towering trees and bustling campsites dotting the landscape. Among the many loggers working in these mills is Jack Harris, a rugged and seasoned logger in his late 30s, with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes that seem to see right through you.

Jack lives in a small cabin on the edge of town, surrounded by nothing but trees as far as the eye can see. His days are filled with the constant din of chainsaws and the smell of freshly cut wood, but he's grown accustomed to the routine. He's been logging for over 20 years, and has earned a reputation among his peers as one of the most skilled and reliable loggers in the business.

As Jack heads out to start his shift at the nearby mill, the sun rises over the forest, casting a golden glow over the landscape. The air is cool and fresh, filled with the scent of pine and earth, and Jack takes a deep breath, feeling invigorated by the morning's promise. He's got a long day ahead of him, and he knows that the sawmill will be running on 10-hour shifts to meet the demand for timber.

The mill is located just outside town, in a small clearing surrounded by towering spruce trees. The sound of chainsaws and the murmur of hammers echoing off the metal beams creates a constant background hum, punctuated by the occasional shout or laughter from the other loggers. It's a familiar scene, one that Jack has grown to love over the years, but it also brings with it a sense of responsibility - he knows that his work is crucial to keeping Oakdale thriving.

As Jack walks into the mill, the sound of machinery greets him like an old friend. He exchanges nods and greetings with the other loggers, who are all chatting and laughing as they prepare for another long day of sawing and hauling timber. The tension in the air is palpable, but it's a good kind - a sense of camaraderie that comes from working together towards a common goal.

The mill is a labyrinthine operation, with rows of wooden machines humming along the walls and the scent of freshly cut wood wafting through the air. Jack knows every inch of this place, every machine and every log that's ever come through these doors. He's spent his entire life working in the forest, and he knows that he's exactly where he belongs - among the trees, with the wind in his hair and the sun on his face.
