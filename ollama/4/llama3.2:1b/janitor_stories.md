The sun had barely risen over the crumbling high school on the outskirts of town, casting a pale light over the faded marquee that still read "Oakwood High" in bold, peeling letters. The streets were empty, save for the occasional delivery truck or late-night diner patron, and the only sound was the distant hum of a city bus making its way down Main Street.

But amidst the silence, one figure moved with purpose. Emily Mayfield wiped down the polished wood counter of the school's cafeteria, her movements economical and practiced. She had been a janitor at Oakwood High for five years now, and she knew every nook and cranny by heart. But today was different. Today, she had a job to do.

As she worked, Emily's mind wandered back to the strange letter she had received just that morning. It was from an unknown sender, with no return address and only one sentence: "They're watching you." The words had sent a shiver down her spine, but Emily had tried to brush them off as mere paranoia. After all, she was just a janitor. What could possibly be on the other end of that letter?

Despite her skepticism, Emily couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. And as she finished up her duties and headed out into the cool morning air, she felt a sense of unease settle in the pit of her stomach.
