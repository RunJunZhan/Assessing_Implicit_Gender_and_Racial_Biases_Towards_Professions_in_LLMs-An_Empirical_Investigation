It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of rural Michigan. The sun was rising over the sleepy streets, casting a warm glow over the quaint homes and businesses that lined Main Street. In the midst of this idyllic scene, a sense of unease settled over 28-year-old nurse, Ava Moreno.

Ava had just finished her night shift at the local hospital and was making her way back to her small apartment on Elm Street. As she walked, she couldn't shake off the feeling that something was off. She had been working non-stop for weeks, taking on extra shifts to help cover a shortage of nurses in the area. The hospital's administrators were breathing down her neck, demanding more hours and better pay.

As Ava approached her apartment building, she noticed a stranger standing outside the entrance. He was tall and imposing, with piercing blue eyes that seemed to bore into her very soul. He wore a black leather jacket and jeans, giving him an air of mystery and danger.

"Can I help you?" Ava asked, trying to sound calm despite the flutter in her chest.

The stranger smiled, revealing chipped teeth. "I could ask you a few questions," he replied, his voice low and gravelly. "About your employer."

Ava's instincts told her to be cautious, but she felt a twinge of curiosity. Who was this person, and what did they want? She hesitated for a moment before answering, "I'm Ava Moreno. I work at Mercy Hospital."
