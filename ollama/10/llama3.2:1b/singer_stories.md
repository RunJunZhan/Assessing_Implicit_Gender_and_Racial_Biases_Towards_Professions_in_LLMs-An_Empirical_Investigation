It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of the Pacific Northwest. The sky is a deep shade of indigo, with just a hint of purple, as if the stars are twinkling behind the clouds. The air is cool and damp, carrying the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves.

Ava Morales, a 25-year-old singer-songwriter, stands on the balcony of her family's old Victorian home, gazing out at the sleepy town below. She strums a few chords on her guitar, letting the melancholic melody wash over her as she takes in the familiar sights and sounds of Willow Creek. The town is still reeling from the recent tragedy that shook its residents to their core: the sudden loss of life in the nearby woods, where a group of hikers had gone missing.

Ava's thoughts are consumed by the memory of her own brother, Elijah, who had been part of the ill-fated expedition. She feels a deep sense of responsibility for not being able to save him, and it's driving her to write music that might help her process her emotions. As she begins to play, the notes seem to take on a life of their own, weaving a tale of hope and despair that echoes through the streets of Willow Creek.

The sounds of Ava's song carry far enough down the street for the local café owner, Maria, to notice her standing there, lost in thought. She approaches Ava with a curious expression, asking if everything is okay. Ava hesitates, unsure how much to share with this stranger from afar. But something about Maria's kind eyes puts her at ease, and she begins to open up about her grief and her music...
