It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of the Pacific Northwest. The streets were lined with towering evergreen trees, their leaves rustling softly in the gentle breeze as Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stepped out of her cozy office and into the bright sunlight.

Lexi, a 35-year-old clinical psychologist, had spent the past year working at the local psychiatric hospital, helping patients overcome the trauma and struggles that had shaped their lives. Her own experiences had informed her approach to therapy, which focused on empathy, understanding, and gentle guidance. As she walked down the main street of Willow Creek, Lexi felt a sense of pride and purpose, knowing that she was making a difference in the lives of those around her.

The hospital, named Oakwood Manor, was a quaint building with a warm, welcoming atmosphere, adorned with vibrant artwork and lush greenery. It had been a staple of Willow Creek for over 20 years, providing a safe haven for individuals struggling with mental health issues, addiction, and trauma. Lexi's office, located on the second floor, overlooked the hospital's courtyard, where patients could take a stroll or enjoy the beautiful gardens during the warmer months.

As she walked to her car, parked on the street corner near the hospital entrance, Lexi noticed a sense of unease emanating from the crowded parking lot. She had a feeling that something was amiss, but she couldn't quite put her finger on it. A nagging question echoed in her mind: what news could possibly cause so much commotion among the patients and staff?
