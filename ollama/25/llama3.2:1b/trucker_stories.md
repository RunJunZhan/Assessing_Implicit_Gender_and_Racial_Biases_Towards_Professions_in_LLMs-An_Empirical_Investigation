It was a crisp autumn evening in rural Nebraska, the kind of night that made you want to curl up with a cup of coffee and a good book on a cozy truck stop diner counter. Jack "Hawk" Hawkins, a grizzled 45-year-old trucker, had just pulled into the small station, his Peterbilt 389 rumbling down the main street like thunder as he filled up on diesel fuel. The neon sign above the diner creaked in the gentle breeze, reading "Mae's Eats" in faded letters.

As Hawk waited for his driver's license and registration to be processed, he gazed out at the rolling hills and cornfields that seemed to stretch on forever. He had been driving for Hertz for the past three months, hauling cargo from Chicago to Los Angeles, but a few weeks ago, he'd decided to take a break and ride the open roads of Nebraska with his old friend, Sheriff Jake "Bull" Jenkins.

Hawk's thoughts were interrupted by the friendly smile of Mae herself, who was polishing a glass case with a dirty rag while chatting with a regular customer. She handed Hawk his new driver's license and registration, her eyes twinkling with a hint of mischief.

"Mornin', sweetheart," she said, as if she knew better than to correct him. "You're lookin' a little worse for wear after that long road trip."

Hawk chuckled, ruffling his dark hair as he took the documents from Mae. "Just a few scratches on my ol' girl," he replied, patting her friendly hand before walking out of the diner, into the cool autumn air.
