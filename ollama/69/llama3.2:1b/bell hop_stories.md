It was a crisp autumn morning in New Haven, the kind of day where the leaves on the old oak trees seemed to rustle with an otherworldly language, and the air was alive with the sweet scent of pumpkin spice wafting from the local coffee shops. The city was still waking up, its residents slowly emerging from their homes like a giant, sleepy beast stirring from a long slumber.

In this sleepy New Haven, nestled between the winding streets of Beacon Hill and the bustling waterfront district, lay the luxurious Bellvue Hotel. Its grand entrance, adorned with a majestic stone façade and a brass sign that creaked in the morning breeze, was the kind of place where one could afford to let their worries fade away.

At the Bellvue Hotel, nothing came between you and your dream – or so they said. Behind the elegant lobby and its polished marble floors, the reality for bellhops like Emilia Everett was that they were the ones keeping the hotel running smoothly. Her name was Emilia Everett, but her friends called her Mia, and she had learned to take pride in being more than just a bellhop – she was a guardian of elegance.

As Emilia began her shift at the Bellvue Hotel, the first guests of the day started to arrive. They would come and go, each with their own story, their own secrets, and their own dreams. Some were business travelers seeking refuge in the city's luxury, while others were families on vacation, dreaming of warm beaches and sun-kissed days.

Little did Emilia know, today was going to be a day unlike any other at the Bellvue Hotel.
