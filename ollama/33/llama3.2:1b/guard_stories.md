It was a chilly autumn evening in the sleepy town of Ravenshire, nestled deep within the heart of the Whispering Woods. The trees, adorned with leaves that shimmered like emeralds in the fading light, cast long shadows across the narrow streets and quaint shops. The air was alive with the sweet scent of ripe fruit and the soft chirping of crickets, a lullaby to the residents as they prepared for the night ahead.

At the town's only guard station, a sturdy stone building stood like a sentinel, its windows gleaming like polished onyx. Here, stationed by duty was Alexander Thorne, a man in his late forties with a rugged jawline and piercing blue eyes that seemed to see right through the darkness. His weathered face was etched with lines of experience, earned from years of protecting this mystical town from those who would seek to harm it.

As Alexander descended into the guard station, the soft creak of the wooden floorboards beneath his feet echoed through the empty halls. He lit a cigarette, taking a long drag before extinguishing it in an ashtray, the ember dying out like a small flame in the darkness. With a quiet satisfaction, he adjusted his worn leather gloves and stepped out into the autumn night, his eyes scanning the shadows for any sign of trouble.
