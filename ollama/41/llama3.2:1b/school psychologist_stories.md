In the quaint, coastal town of Ravenswood, nestled between the rocky shores and the endless blue horizon, Dr. Adelaide "Addie" Everett had spent her entire career working with children. As a school psychologist at Ravenswood Elementary School, she had witnessed firsthand the joys and challenges that came with growing up in this tight-knit community.

The sun was rising over the town, casting a warm glow over the sleepy streets as Addie stepped out of her small, weathered car and onto the cracked asphalt of the school's parking lot. She stretched her arms overhead, feeling the stiffness in her back from a long night of sessions with a rowdy sixth-grade class. Her dark hair was pulled back into a loose ponytail, revealing a smattering of freckles across the bridge of her nose.

As she walked towards the school building, Addie felt a sense of comfort and familiarity wash over her. She had lived in Ravenswood all her life, and this town knew her better than anyone else in the world. The smell of freshly brewed coffee wafted from the cafeteria, mingling with the sound of murmured conversations and the faint clang of pots and pans from the kitchen.

Addie pushed open the school door, revealing a warm, golden light that spilled out onto the sidewalk. She took a deep breath, savoring the familiar scent of books and learning, before making her way to her office, which was tucked away in a cozy corner of the administration building.
