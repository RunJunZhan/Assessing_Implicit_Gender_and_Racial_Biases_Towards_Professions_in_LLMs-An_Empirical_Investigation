Here's a potential story opening and setting for a psychological thriller:

Dr. Amelia Ellis stepped out of the rainy night air and into her office building, shaking the water from her long, curly brown hair. She had just finished a grueling 12-hour session with a young woman named Sarah, who was struggling to cope with the aftermath of a painful breakup. As she locked up the office and headed towards the elevator, Amelia couldn't help but feel a sense of satisfaction at having helped another individual in her work.

The building itself was a nondescript modern high-rise, surrounded by towering skyscrapers and the hum of city traffic. But as Amelia stepped into the elevator, she felt a shiver run down her spine – it was always a little too quiet up here. And then there was the small voice that echoed in the back of her mind, warning her that tonight seemed different.

She reached into her pocket and retrieved her phone, which buzzed with an incoming text from her colleague, Dr. Liam Chen: "Be careful tonight, Amelia. Something feels off."

Amelia had worked with Liam for years – they shared a deep respect for each other's expertise, but their differing personalities often led to tense moments in the office. Still, she trusted his instincts.

As the elevator lurched to a stop on the 23rd floor, Amelia took a deep breath and stepped out into the rain-soaked night, her eyes scanning the deserted streets below for any sign of trouble.
