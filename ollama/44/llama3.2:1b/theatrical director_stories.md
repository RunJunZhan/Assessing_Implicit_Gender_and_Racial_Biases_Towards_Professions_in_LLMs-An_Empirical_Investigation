Here's a potential opening and setting for a story:

In the sweltering summer of 1995, Julianne "Jules" LaFleur stood at the helm of her family's beloved playhouse on the edge of town, its worn wooden sign creaking in the gentle breeze like a chorus of whispers. The LaFleurs had been producing theatre for generations, and Jules was the latest in a long line of artistic directors to keep the tradition alive.

As she gazed out at the rows of crumbling brick buildings and faded storefronts that lined her family's property, Jules felt a sense of pride and responsibility wash over her. This was more than just a playhouse – it was a laboratory for new ideas, a incubator for dreams, and a sanctuary for creatives seeking refuge from the world beyond.

On this particular summer evening, however, Jules couldn't shake off the feeling that something was amiss. A chill ran down her spine as she watched a group of rowdy teenagers swagger onto her property, their flashlights casting eerie shadows on the ground. They seemed to be searching for something – or someone.

Jules's thoughts were interrupted by the sound of her own name being called from inside the playhouse. "Jules!" Her assistant, a quiet and reserved young woman named Sophia, flung open the door and ushered in a tall, lanky figure with a messy mop of hair and an air of disheveled elegance.

"Hey, boss," Sophia said, eyes wide with excitement. "We've got a problem."

Jules's gaze snapped back to the teenagers, who were now eyeing her playhouse with a mixture of curiosity and hostility. She felt a surge of determination ignite within her – it was time to take control of this situation and turn her world upside down...
