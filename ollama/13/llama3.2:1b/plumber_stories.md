Here's a potential story opening and setting for a coming-of-age mystery novel:

The fog rolled in off the Chesapeake Bay, shrouding the small town of Ravenswood in a damp, grey mist that clung to everything like a damp blanket. It was the kind of morning where the air felt heavy with possibility, but also seemed to be holding its breath, waiting for something to happen.

For 17-year-old Jack "Mac" McAllister, this was just another typical day before school. He lived in a small, weathered bungalow on Elm Street with his parents and younger sister, Emily. Mac's father had passed away three years ago, leaving him to take care of the household business – plumbing being his bread and butter.

As he trudged through the door, shedding his worn jeans and faded T-shirt, Jack couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. It wasn't just the fog; it was a sense of unease that had been growing inside him for weeks, ever since his best friend, Alex, had gone missing in the small town.

Alex's disappearance had left Mac with more questions than answers. The police had ruled it an accident, but Jack knew better – there were things he couldn't unsee, and some wounds just wouldn't heal. He'd always been close to Alex; they'd grown up together, exploring the woods and sharing secrets in the dead of night.

As he made his way downstairs, Mac could smell the familiar scent of freshly brewed coffee wafting from the kitchen. His mother, Rachel, was already up, stirring a pot on the stovetop with a look of concentration that seemed to be getting more intense by the minute.

"Morning, buddy," she said, not looking up as he entered. "You're late."

Mac shrugged, feeling a familiar knot form in his stomach. "Sorry, Mom. Traffic."

But as he approached her, he caught sight of a piece of paper on the counter, scribbled with a hasty scrawl: "Meet me at the old mill at midnight. -A"
