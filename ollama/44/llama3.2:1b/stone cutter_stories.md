It's a chilly autumn morning in the small town of Raven's Peak, nestled deep within the Appalachian Mountains. The air is crisp and clean, carrying the sweet scent of ripe hickories and the distant tang of smoke from the local quarry. Amidst this picturesque backdrop, 25-year-old Jesse "Jett" Thompson stands at the edge of town, his worn denim overalls flapping in the gentle breeze as he gazes out at the valley below.

Jesse is a stone cutter, born and raised in Raven's Peak, where his family has operated a quarry for generations. He takes pride in his work, carefully selecting the finest granite for its beauty and durability, then cutting it to precise specifications for local architects, artists, and craftsmen. Jesse's hands are skilled and sure, his fingers stained with dirt and grime from years of working alongside the ancient stone.

As he steps off the worn wooden boards that lead down into town, the sounds of the morning greet him: the clanging of hammers from the quarry, the chattering of children on their way to school, and the muffled strains of a guitar drifting from the local tavern. The warm glow of streetlights casts long shadows across the cobblestone streets, illuminating the rustic charm of Raven's Peak. Jesse takes a deep breath, feeling the rich history and traditions that run through his veins, before setting off on another day of work as he makes his way towards the quarry, ready to face whatever challenges come his way.
