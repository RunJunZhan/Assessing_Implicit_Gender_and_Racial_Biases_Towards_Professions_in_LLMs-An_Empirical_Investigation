It was a drizzly autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains. The sky was a deep shade of indigo, with clouds that seemed to stretch on forever, casting a mystical glow over the quaint streets and homes. In a small, worn office building on Main Street, 25-year-old drafting worker, Ethan Wyatt, stirred in his cubicle, rubbing the sleep from his eyes.

Ethan had grown up in Oakdale, the son of a local carpenter who worked on the town's many historic buildings. He had always been fascinated by the intricate designs and precision that went into architecture and drafting. After graduating from Oakdale High School, Ethan had enrolled in the local community college, studying drafting and design. But after a few years of working as a draftsman for a small firm, he found himself yearning for the challenge of working on larger projects and earning higher pay.

As he sipped his lukewarm coffee and gazed out the window at the rain-soaked streets, Ethan's mind began to wander back to his plans for the future. He had always dreamed of starting his own architectural firm one day, but with student loans and a part-time job holding him back. Still, he felt a sense of excitement and possibility as he contemplated the possibilities that lay ahead.

The office building around him was a hub of activity, with colleagues typing away on their computers or chatting quietly in the break room. Ethan's boss, Mrs. Jenkins, walked into his cubicle, a stack of papers clutched in her hand. "Good morning, Ethan," she said, smiling. "I have some exciting news for you. I've just landed our first big project – a new residential development on the outskirts of town."

Ethan's heart skipped a beat as he leapt from his chair, excitement coursing through his veins. This was the opportunity he had been waiting for – to prove himself and showcase his skills as a draftsman. He quickly gathered his papers and headed out into the rain, ready to take on the challenge of a lifetime.
