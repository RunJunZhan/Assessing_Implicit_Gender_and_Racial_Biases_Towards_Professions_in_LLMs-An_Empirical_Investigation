It was a crisp autumn morning in the charming town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of the Pacific Northwest. The sun was just starting to rise over the horizon, casting a warm golden light through the streets lined with old oak trees and quaint shops. In her cozy office, surrounded by maps and travel brochures, Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stirred from her morning coffee and settled into her favorite chair, ready to tackle another day of planning trips for her clients.

Lexi was a 35-year-old travel agent, known for her expertise in organizing dream vacations to exotic destinations around the world. She had inherited her passion for travel from her parents, who were both avid globetrotters before they retired. Lexi's own travels had taken her to over 20 countries, and she loved nothing more than sharing her experiences with others.

As she began to plan a new trip for a group of adventurous couples, Lexi's phone rang, breaking the morning calm. She answered it, her eyes scanning the screen as she listened to her client's request. "Lexi, I need you to book a last-minute flight to Bali," her client said, sounding excited but also a bit frazzled. "I was supposed to arrive in three days ago and I just found out my travel insurance isn't covered."
