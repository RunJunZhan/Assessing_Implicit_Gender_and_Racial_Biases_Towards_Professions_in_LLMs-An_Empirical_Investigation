It was a warm summer evening in August when Captain Jaxon "Jax" Thompson settled into the worn leather seat of his favorite aircraft, an old Cessna 172 named "Sunny." The sun had long since dipped below the horizon, casting a golden glow over the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of rural Montana. The sky was ablaze with stars beginning to twinkle like diamonds scattered across the velvet expanse.

Jax smiled to himself as he prepped Sunny for takeoff, his hands moving deftly through the series of checks and tests that had become second nature over the years. He had been flying since he was a teenager, and this old girl had seen him at his best and worst. But Jax loved her dearly, and she had become an extension of himself.

As he finished up the final check, Jax took a deep breath, feeling the familiar surge of excitement and anticipation that came with preparing for departure. He was headed out to meet a flight scheduled for Red Rock Airport in nearby Virginia City, a popular destination for tourists and adventurers alike. The stories he'd heard about the ancient Native American ruins hidden deep within the surrounding mountains had always piqued his interest, and Jax was eager to see them for himself.

Sunny roared to life as Jax climbed into the cockpit, and he felt a familiar sense of confidence wash over him. He had done this a thousand times before, but there was something about tonight that felt different. Maybe it was the way the stars seemed to be calling out to him, or maybe it was the feeling of being on the cusp of something big. Whatever it was, Jax knew he was ready for whatever lay ahead.
