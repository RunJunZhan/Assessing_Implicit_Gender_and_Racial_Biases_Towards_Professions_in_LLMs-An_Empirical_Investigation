In the heart of Portland, Oregon, where the lush greenery and vibrant arts scene met the city's rich history, 28-year-old occupational therapist Maya Greenwood settled into her small, cluttered apartment on Pearl Street. The sound of rain pattering against the roof and the scent of freshly brewed coffee wafting from her tiny kitchen were her constant companions, a soothing reminder that even in the midst of chaos, she found solace.

Maya's job at the local hospital had taken her to countless homes, nursing homes, and rehabilitation centers throughout the city. She had seen it all - the tears, the frustration, the resilience. But amidst all the challenges, Maya found joy in helping others rediscover their sense of purpose and independence. As she poured herself a cup of coffee, she gazed out the window at the rain-soaked streets, her mind already turning to the day's schedule and the various patients awaiting her attention.

The city outside was alive and pulsing, but within Maya's little world, time seemed to stand still. She had always been drawn to the intricacies of human movement, the way that even the smallest adjustments could have a profound impact on someone's life. As an occupational therapist, she spent most of her days helping clients relearn how to live, not just survive.

Little did Maya know, today was going to be one of those days that would challenge everything she thought she knew about herself and her role as an OT...
