The sun was just starting to peek over the sleepy suburban streets of Oakdale, casting a warm glow over the quaint homes and manicured lawns. In one of those homes, 22-year-old hairdresser Emilia "Mia" Thompson sat perched on the edge of her chair, her long, curly brown hair tied back in a ponytail as she carefully combed through a tangled mess of strands. She was at Thompson's Tresses, the local salon where she spent most of her days cutting, coloring, and styling the hair of Oakdale's fashionable residents.

As she worked, the sounds of sizzling pans, chattering clients, and the soft hum of the CD player playing in the background created a soothing background noise that seemed to lull the world into a peaceful slumber. It was just another typical Wednesday morning at Thompson's Tresses, but Mia was well aware that today would be different.

Today was her birthday, and she had big plans. She had been looking forward to this day for weeks, ever since her best friend Emma had convinced her to join the Oakdale Hairdressers' Association's annual party. The theme this year was "Glamour and Glitz," and Mia had been tasked with creating the evening's centerpiece: a dazzling hair-do inspired by the likes of Beyoncé and Lady Gaga.

As she finished up her last client and gazed out at the sea of expectant faces, Mia felt a thrill of excitement. This was going to be the best birthday ever – or so she hoped.
