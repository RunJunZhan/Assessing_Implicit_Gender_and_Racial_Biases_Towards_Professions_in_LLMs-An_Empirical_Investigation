It was a crisp autumn morning in the small coastal town of Tidestone, where the salty air and sound of seagulls filled the air as Dr. Sophia Ellis prepared for another day at the local rehabilitation center, "Surgeons Without Borders." As a licensed occupational therapist with a passion for helping others overcome injuries and disabilities, Sophia had grown up in Tidestone, having spent most of her childhood playing on the beach and exploring the rugged coastline.

The rehabilitation center was a nondescript building located just off the main street, but its reputation as a premier facility for helping patients recover from various injuries and conditions had earned it a loyal following. Patients came from all over the region to receive treatment under Sophia's expertise, ranging from post-operative care for surgery patients to working with individuals who had lost their independence due to neurological or muscular disorders.

As Sophia finished her morning routine of checking on patients' progress and reviewing charts, she gazed out the window at the ocean, watching as a small sailboat bobbed gently in the distance. It was a peaceful scene that always seemed to clear her mind before another busy day began. Little did Sophia know, today would be one of those days that would challenge everything she thought she knew about helping others.
