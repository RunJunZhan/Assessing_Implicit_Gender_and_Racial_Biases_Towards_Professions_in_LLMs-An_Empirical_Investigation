It was a crisp autumn morning in the small coastal town of Tidewater, Virginia, where the salty air and sound of seagulls filled the air as Dr. Alexandra "Alex" Thompson stood on the weathered dock, gazing out at the calm waters of the Chesapeake Bay. She took a deep breath, feeling the weight of her physical therapy practice settle in around her - the countless sessions with patients struggling to walk again after a hip replacement surgery, the endless hours spent reviewing patient charts and analyzing X-rays.

Alex's own life had taken a dramatic turn after the accident that left her right leg weakened and unstable. A grueling rehabilitation program at a top-rated hospital had been her only hope for a return to normalcy, but even now, six months in, she still felt like she was just getting started on the long journey towards full recovery.

As she stood there, lost in thought, a gentle tug on her arm broke the spell. It was her best friend and fellow physical therapist, Rachel Kim, who had come to help Alex with some paperwork before heading out to meet their patients for another day of therapy sessions. Together, they surveyed the dock, chatting about everything from their weekend plans to the latest sports news on the TV screens that lined the pier.

But as they talked, Alex's gaze wandered back to the sea, her mind drifting towards the upcoming session with a patient named Sarah, who was struggling to balance again after a stroke. It was one of the toughest cases of their time - not just because of the complex nature of the injury, but also because Sarah had lost so much mobility and independence in the years leading up to her accident...
