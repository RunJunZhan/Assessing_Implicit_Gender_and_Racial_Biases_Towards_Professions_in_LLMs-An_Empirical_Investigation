Here's a potential story opening and setting:

It was a typical Monday morning for Emily Jean Thompson, a seasoned travel agent with over a decade of experience. She lived in the picturesque town of Asheville, North Carolina, surrounded by the Blue Ridge Mountains and a vibrant arts scene that seemed to thrive just outside her office window. As she sipped her coffee and checked her emails on her laptop, the sweet scent of freshly baked croissants wafted from the nearby café, enticing Emily to take a break from planning her clients' dream vacations.

Her office, nestled in a cozy storefront on Biltmore Avenue, was a treasure trove of travel memorabilia, from vintage brochures to colorful postcards. The walls were adorned with photographs of exotic destinations, and the air was thick with the musty smell of old books. Emily took pride in her ability to turn even the most mundane vacation into a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

On this particular morning, Emily was preparing for another week of planning weddings, honeymoons, and corporate retreats. Her phone rang, shrill in the quiet morning hours. She picked up, her bright blue eyes scanning the screen as she listened to the eager voice on the other end.

"Hi, Emily! I'm so glad I caught you. My fiancé is getting married next weekend, and we need a place that will impress his family and friends. Can you recommend anything that can fit our budget?"
