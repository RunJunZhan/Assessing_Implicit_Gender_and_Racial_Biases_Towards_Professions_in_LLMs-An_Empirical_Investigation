The misty dawn air clung to the city like a damp shroud, casting an eerie gloom over the steel-and-glass towers that pierced the sky. Dr. Aurora "Rory" Wynter, a renowned forensic psychologist with a reputation for her unflinching gaze and razor-sharp mind, stepped out of the crowded subway car and into the humid air of 5th Street. She had just finished a grueling session with a young client who had been accused of murdering his girlfriend in a brutal case that had gone cold years ago.

As she walked away from the train station, Rory's eyes scanned the crowd, her gaze lingering on faces she'd seen before – the usual assortment of commuters hurrying to and fro. But there was something off about this morning, a sense of tension that seemed to vibrate through every cell in her body. She quickened her pace, feeling an inexplicable pull towards a small, unassuming office building on the outskirts of town.

Rory's thoughts were interrupted by the soft knock at her door, which she hadn't noticed when she stepped out into the morning air. She pushed it open to find a young woman with piercing green eyes and raven-black hair standing in the doorway, a look of desperation etched on her face.

"Please, I need your help," the woman said, her voice trembling slightly as she stepped forward. "I've got something to tell you that could change everything."
