It was a crisp autumn evening in the historic district of Paris, the Eiffel Tower twinkling like a diamond against the dark blue sky. The concierge office of Le Coeur de la Ville Hotel, run by 25-year-old Aurélie Dumont, bustled with last-minute preparations for the night's guests. Aurélie, with her short, spiky blonde hair and warm smile, was already busy checking the final details on the evening's schedule.

As she worked, the soft hum of jazz music floated through the lobby, mingling with the aroma of freshly brewed coffee and baked pastries. The hotel's staff had spent all day perfecting their service, from polishing silverware to arranging delicate centerpieces for each room. Aurélie took pride in making every guest feel welcome and at home.

Just as she was about to lock up the office, a sleek black limousine pulled up outside, its tinted windows glinting like black diamonds. A dashing gentleman stepped out, his tailored tuxedo gleaming in the fading light. Aurélie's eyes met his for a brief moment before returning to her work, but she knew that this guest was special – one who would require extra attention and discretion.

The limousine door slid open, and the gentleman strode into the lobby, his eyes scanning the room until they landed on Aurélie. She smiled, her cheeks flushing slightly as he approached. "Bonjour, Monsieur...?" she began, but before he could respond, a low voice interrupted her from behind him.

"Ah, Aurélie, darling. I hope you're ready for tonight."
