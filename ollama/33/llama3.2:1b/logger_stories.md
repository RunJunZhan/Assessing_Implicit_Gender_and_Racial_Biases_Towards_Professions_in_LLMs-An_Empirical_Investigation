It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled deep within the heart of the Pacific Northwest. The air is alive with the sweet scent of ripe apples and the gentle hum of trucks and equipment as the logging season gets underway. At the local sawmill, Jack "Hawk" Hawkins is already up and about, his rugged features illuminated by the warm glow of the early morning sunrise.

Jack is a logger by trade, known for his fearless attitude and uncanny ability to navigate even the most treacherous terrain. He's been working in these woods for over two decades, but he still finds joy in the simple act of chopping down trees and watching them fall with a satisfying crunch. As he makes his way to the mill, his worn leather jacket creaks and groans with each step, a testament to the countless hours he's spent braving the elements.

The sun is rising over the horizon, casting a golden glow over the town as Jack climbs aboard the logging truck, affectionately nicknamed "Bertha." The engine roars to life beneath him, and Jack gives Bertha a hearty handoff before climbing into his seat, ready to tackle another day on the job. Little does he know, today will be different - it'll be a day that will change the course of his life forever.
