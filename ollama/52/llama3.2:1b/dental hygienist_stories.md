It was a typical Wednesday morning at Oakwood General Dental Care, the largest family-owned dental practice in the small town of Willow Creek. The sun shone brightly through the large windows of the waiting room, casting a warm glow over the bustling office. Dr. Sophia Ellis, a seasoned dental hygienist with years of experience, stood behind the front desk, greeting patients one by one as they entered the modern, state-of-the-art facility.

The sounds of gentle chatter and clinking cups filled the air as patients sipped on their morning coffee and got settled in for their appointments. The walls were adorned with family photos and colorful artwork, adding a touch of warmth to the otherwise sterile environment. Dr. Ellis had worked at Oakwood General Dental Care for five years, and she took pride in providing exceptional patient care to the people of Willow Creek.

As the morning rush subsided, Dr. Ellis took a moment to review her schedule for the day, checking on patients with appointments scheduled for cleanings, fillings, and extractions. She was particularly excited about the upcoming appointment with Mrs. Margaret Jenkins, a regular patient who had been coming in every six months for years. The two had become close friends over the years, and Dr. Ellis looked forward to catching up with her on Thursday afternoon.

Just as she was about to begin her morning rounds, a knock at the door interrupted her thoughts. It was Mrs. Jenkins herself, looking a bit anxious and fidgety. "Dr. Ellis, I'm so glad I caught you," she said, her voice trembling slightly. "I just need to discuss something with you about my upcoming cleaning."
