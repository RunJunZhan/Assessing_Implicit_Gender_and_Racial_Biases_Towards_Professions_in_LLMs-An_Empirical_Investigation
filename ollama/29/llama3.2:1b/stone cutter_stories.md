It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Raven's Peak, nestled at the edge of the vast and ancient Redwood Forest. The air is alive with the sweet scent of damp earth and the distant thrum of hammers ringing out from the local quarry where Emrys "Em" Thompson works as a stone cutter.

Emrys, a ruggedly handsome man in his mid-30s with a strong jawline and piercing blue eyes, stands at the edge of the forest, gazing out at the trees that seem to stretch up to the sky like sentinels. He's been working for the local quarry for nigh on five years, cutting stone into beautiful works of art that adorn the buildings throughout Raven's Peak. Emrys takes pride in his work, carefully selecting and shaping each piece to fit the unique character of the structure it'll be placed in.

As he steps off the forest road, a burst of sunlight illuminates the dirt path leading up to the quarry, revealing a mess of discarded tools and half-finished projects. The sound of pickaxes striking stone echoes through the stillness, mingling with the distant rumble of the nearby river.

Emrys strides towards his small workshop, a weathered cabin nestled among the trees, its wooden slats worn smooth by years of use. Inside, the air is thick with the smell of sawdust and the faint tang of freshly sharpened steel. A half-finished statue of a mythical creature sits on the workbench, waiting for Emrys' attention to bring it to life. As he approaches, the statue's stone eyes seem to gleam with an otherworldly intelligence, as if watching him with a mixture of curiosity and expectation.
