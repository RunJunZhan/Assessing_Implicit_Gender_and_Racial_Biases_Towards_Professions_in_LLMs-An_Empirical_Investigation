It was a crisp autumn morning in the sleepy town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of the Pacific Northwest. The misty veil that shrouded the town's streets seemed to cling to the buildings like a damp, grey mist, as if trying to keep secrets from the curious eyes that peeked out from behind the windows.

Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stood at the edge of the town's central square, her feet planted firmly on the worn asphalt as she gazed out at the deserted streets. Her dark hair was tied back in a ponytail, revealing the sharp features of her face, which were illuminated by the warm glow of the setting sun. A pair of wire-rimmed glasses perched on the end of her nose, giving her an air of quiet intensity.

As a forensic psychologist at the local police department, Lexi had grown accustomed to the rhythms of small-town life. But today was different. Today, she had received a cryptic message from an anonymous source, hinting at a complex case that required her unique expertise.

The message read: "Meet me at the old oak tree in town square at midnight. Come alone."

Lexi's heart quickened as she tucked her phone into her pocket and began to make her way through the deserted streets. She had always been drawn to the darker corners of human nature, and this case seemed to be calling to her like a siren's song.

As she approached the old oak tree, a sense of unease settled in the pit of her stomach. What was Lexi supposed to find here? And who was behind these mysterious messages? She pushed open the creaky door and slipped inside, the shadows swallowing her whole.
