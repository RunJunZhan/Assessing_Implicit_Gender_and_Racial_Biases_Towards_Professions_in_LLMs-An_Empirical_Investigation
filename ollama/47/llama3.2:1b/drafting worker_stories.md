It was a typical Monday morning in the small town of Willow Creek, with the sun peeking through the windows of the local office building where 25-year-old drafting worker, Ethan Thompson, began his day. The smell of freshly brewed coffee wafted from the break room, mingling with the sound of chirping birds outside as he made his way to his desk.

Ethan lived in a cozy two-story house on Elm Street, just a short walk from the office building where he worked as a drafting artist for the local architecture firm. His neighbors were all friendly folks who knew each other's names and had become accustomed to Ethan's late-night work hours and early morning coffee runs.

As he settled into his chair, Ethan's mind began to wander back to the previous night's project – a new residential design that needed to be finalized before the city council meeting later that day. He rubbed the sleep from his eyes and got straight to work, his sharp mind already turning over ideas for the building plans. The hum of his computer and the soft glow of the screen were familiar companions in this quiet morning routine, as he set out to create something innovative and functional – a home that would make his clients proud.
