It was a crisp autumn morning in late September as Dr. Sophia Patel stood outside the bustling University of Michigan Hospital's 10 East Department, sipping on a steaming cup of coffee from her thermos. The sun was just starting to rise over the city, casting a warm glow over the towering skyscrapers and vibrant street life. The air was alive with the sounds of morning rush hour, and Sophia felt a sense of excitement and nervousness as she prepared for another long day of work at the hospital.

As a Phlebotomy Technician, Sophia had spent countless hours in this very department, watching students and patients alike come and go from the hospital's many medical units. She had earned her degree in Nursing, but it was her certification as a phlebotomy technician that allowed her to bring her skills directly into patient care. Today, she would be assisting Dr. Rachel Kim, one of the hospital's leading oncologists, with a particularly challenging patient - a young woman named Emily Wilson who had been diagnosed with stage III breast cancer.

Sophia took a deep breath, mentally preparing herself for the long day ahead. She pulled her scrubs over her head and tied up her ponytail, giving a quick glance at the phone in her pocket to check if there were any last-minute calls or messages from Dr. Kim. With a sense of purpose, Sophia headed into the hospital's bustling hallways, ready to face whatever challenges lay ahead.
