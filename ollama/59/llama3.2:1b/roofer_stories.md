It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of rural Montana. The sun rises over the rolling hills and wheat fields, casting a warm golden light over the quaint Main Street. The air is alive with the sweet scent of freshly cut grass and the distant chirping of birds.

In this picturesque setting, 25-year-old Ryder Jackson has just finished installing a new roof on the local sheriff's department building. Ryder is a ruggedly handsome roofer with a strong work ethic and a passion for his craft. He's been working as a roofer for five years, and his father had taught him everything he knows.

Ryder lives in a small one-room cabin on the outskirts of town, surrounded by acres of lush green grass and towering pine trees. His cabin is modest but well-maintained, with a large porch where he can sit and enjoy the fresh air. A battered old truck is parked outside, filled with tools and supplies.

As Ryder steps out of his cabin, he takes a deep breath of the cool morning air, feeling invigorated by the promise of a new day ahead. He's got a long day of work ahead of him, but he's excited to see the finished product – a beautiful new roof that will protect the sheriff's department from the harsh Montana weather.

Little does Ryder know, his day is about to take an unexpected turn when a mysterious stranger arrives in town, seeking refuge from the rain-soaked streets of Seattle.
