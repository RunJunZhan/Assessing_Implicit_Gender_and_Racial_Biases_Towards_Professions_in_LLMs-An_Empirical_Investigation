It's a crisp autumn evening in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of rural Oregon. The streets are lined with towering trees, their leaves rustling softly in the gentle breeze as it carries the sweet scent of ripe apples and damp earth. Against this picturesque backdrop, 22-year-old Lena Grant steps out of her family's old Victorian home on Main Street, a vision in silver sequins and lace that shimmers under the streetlights.

Lena has always felt a deep connection to her hometown, singing softly to herself as she walks down the sidewalk, her voice carrying on the wind like a whispered secret. She's been performing at local bars and clubs for years, but tonight feels different - maybe it's the full moon overhead, or perhaps it's the fact that she's finally saved up enough money to book a gig at the iconic Red Door nightclub in Portland. Whatever the reason, Lena can feel her nerves dissipating with each step, replaced by a sense of purpose and belonging.

As she walks towards the club, the sounds of indie rock and electronic music drift through the air, mingling with the distant hum of traffic and the chirping of crickets. The sun is setting over Willow Creek, casting a warm orange glow over the town square, where a group of locals are gathered around a bonfire, swapping stories and jokes in hushed tones. Lena's eyes scan the scene, her heart skipping a beat as she spots a familiar figure standing on the edge of the crowd - Jake Mitchell, the lead singer of her favorite band, The Raven's Wings.

Jake's got a look on his face that says he's got a secret, and Lena can't help but wonder what it might be. She quickens her pace, her boots clicking on the pavement as she approaches him, her voice barely above a whisper as she whispers, "Hey, Jake?"
