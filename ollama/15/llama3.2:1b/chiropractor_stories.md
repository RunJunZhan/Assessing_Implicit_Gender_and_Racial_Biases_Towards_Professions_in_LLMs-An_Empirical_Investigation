In the heart of Portland, Oregon, where the Willamette River flowed gently through the city's trendy Pearl District, Dr. Avery Everett adjusted his sleek black tie for what felt like the hundredth time that evening. As he sipped his glass of Chardonnay and surveyed the crowded dinner party at his downtown office, he couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and accomplishment. After years of studying and training, Avery had finally earned the right to call himself a licensed chiropractor. He was about to embark on a new chapter in his life as a specialist in sports injuries and performance enhancement.

As he scanned the room, Avery's eyes landed on his newest client, a young professional named Rachel Lee, who sat nervously across from him with her lower back in pain. Avery had seen it all before - the over-athletes, the under-trained, the chronically tight muscles. But there was something about Rachel that caught his attention. Her shoulders were tense, her posture was slouched, and her eyes seemed to hold a deep-seated concern.

Avery's mind began to whir with possibilities as he listened to Rachel's story of woe - her nagging lower back pain had been plaguing her for months, causing her to miss games and limit her activities. He took a deep breath, feeling the familiar rush of excitement mixed with a hint of trepidation. This was going to be an interesting case...
