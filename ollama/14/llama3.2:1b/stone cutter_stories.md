It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Raven's Peak, nestled in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains. The air is alive with the sweet scent of ripe apples and the distant tang of smoke from the local mine, where the town's sole source of income comes from extracting the region's rich stone deposits.

At the center of it all stands Oakley "Oak" Blackwood, a ruggedly handsome 35-year-old stone cutter with a reputation for being one of the best in the business. With calloused hands and a chiseled jawline, Oak has spent his entire life honing his craft, learning the intricacies of rock and stone from his father, who was also a master stone cutter.

Oak's small workshop is a cluttered but cozy haven on the outskirts of town, filled with the sound of hammers ringing against anvil, chisels biting into stone, and the occasional burst of laughter as Oak shares tales of his adventures in the mine. His worn denim overalls are stained with every color of the rainbow, and his faded black t-shirt bears the logo of his favorite local quarry: a stylized image of a granite block with the words "Blackwood's Boulders" etched beneath.

As the sun rises higher in the sky, casting a warm glow over the town, Oak is already hard at work, preparing for another day of extracting the next great stone. He takes pride in his work, knowing that every piece of stone he cuts will be used to create something beautiful – whether it's a sculpture, a building, or a simple ornament. Little does he know, today will bring more than just a typical day's labor; it will lead him on a journey that will take him deep into the heart of the Appalachian Mountains, and test his skills like never before.
