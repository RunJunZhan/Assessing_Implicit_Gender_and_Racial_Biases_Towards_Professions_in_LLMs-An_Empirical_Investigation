It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of rural Pennsylvania. The streets were lined with turn-of-the-century buildings, their wooden facades weathered to a soft gray that seemed to blend seamlessly into the misty dawn air. In a cozy neighborhood on Elm Street, number 214, lived Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson, a high school English teacher in her late twenties.

Lexi's classroom was a warm and welcoming space, with bookshelves overflowing with classic novels and poetry collections. Her desk was a shrine to literature, with bookmarks and post-it notes covering every inch of available surface. She spent most mornings before the students arrived, grading papers and preparing lesson plans for her AP English class.

As she sipped her coffee and listened to the distant rumble of the nearby railroad tracks, Lexi felt a sense of contentment wash over her. This was her happy place – surrounded by books and language, with the world outside receding into the background as she lost herself in the pages of the texts that mattered most to her. Little did she know, this ordinary-seeming life was about to take an unexpected turn, one that would change everything.
