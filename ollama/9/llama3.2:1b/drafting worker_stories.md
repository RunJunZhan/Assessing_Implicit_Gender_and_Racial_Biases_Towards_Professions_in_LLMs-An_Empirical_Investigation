It was a typical Wednesday morning in the small town of Oakdale, nestled in the heart of the Midwest. The sun was rising over the rolling hills and fields of wheat that stretched as far as the eye could see, casting a warm orange glow over the quiet streets. In the midst of this peaceful scene, 25-year-old drafting worker, Ethan Thompson, woke up in his small apartment above his family's old diner, the scent of freshly brewed coffee wafting through the air.

Ethan had grown up in Oakdale, learning the ins and outs of the local diner from a young age. After completing his degree in drafting at the University of Iowa, he had returned to his hometown to work alongside his father, Joe Thompson, who owned and operated the family diner. It was a dream job for Ethan, allowing him to combine his passion for design with his love for small-town America.

As Ethan got dressed in his worn jeans and faded T-shirt, he gazed out the window at the bustling streets below. The sounds of sizzling burgers and chirping children filled the air, a symphony that echoed the rhythm of small-town life. He took a deep breath, feeling the familiar sense of contentment wash over him, before heading downstairs to start his shift at the diner.

The diner was a hub of activity on a typical Wednesday morning, with locals sipping coffee and catching up on the news while enjoying the warm atmosphere. Ethan's father, Joe, was already expertly flipping pancakes in the kitchen, while the kitchen staff bustled about, preparing for the lunch rush. Ethan grinned as he clocked out his time sheet and headed out into the crisp morning air, ready to tackle another day of drafting and small-town charm.
