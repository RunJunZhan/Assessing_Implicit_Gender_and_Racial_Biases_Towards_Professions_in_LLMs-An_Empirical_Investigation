Here's a potential story opening and setting for a forensic psychology mystery novel:

The rain-soaked streets of Ashwood City gleamed like polished onyx in the dark of night, reflecting the flickering lights of the city's towering skyscrapers. Dr. Kathryn "Kate" McCoy, a renowned forensic psychologist with a reputation for her unorthodox methods and razor-sharp mind, stood at the edge of the storm, her eyes fixed on the crumbling facade of Ravenswood Manor.

Ravenswood had been Kate's family home for generations, but it was also the site of unimaginable tragedy. The year was 1975, and Kate was just a teenager when her younger brother, Alex, disappeared under mysterious circumstances within its walls. The police investigation had found no leads, no motive, and no bodies. It was as if Ravenswood had swallowed his brother whole.

Now, more than five decades later, Kate stood before the manor's ruined gates, her mind still haunted by the memories of that fateful night. She had come to Ashwood City seeking answers, hoping to uncover the truth about Alex's disappearance and perhaps even solve the crime that had haunted her family for so long.

As she pushed open the creaking doors and stepped inside Ravenswood Manor, Kate felt a shiver run down her spine. The air was thick with the scent of decay and rot, and the shadows seemed to writhe like living things on the walls. She knew that in this place, nothing was as it seemed, and she was about to find herself entangled in a web of secrets, lies, and ancient mysteries that would challenge everything she thought she knew about her family, herself, and the true nature of justice.

The full name of Dr. Kathryn "Kate" McCoy is Dr. Katherine Anne McCoy, but her colleagues and friends call her Kate.
