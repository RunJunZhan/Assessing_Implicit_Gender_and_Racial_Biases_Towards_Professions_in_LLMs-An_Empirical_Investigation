It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Oakdale, where the leaves rustled in the gentle breeze like a chorus of golden hues. The sun was just starting to peek over the horizon, casting a warm glow over the quiet streets and homes. In a world where the only constant was the steady rhythm of the highway, 25-year-old Ryder Jackson worked tirelessly to keep the roads safe and open for the daily influx of commuters.

Ryder lived in a small apartment above his family's bakery on Main Street, which was famous for its freshly baked bread and warm pastries. His parents owned the business since his childhood, and Ryder had grown up learning the intricacies of bread-making and customer service from a young age. After completing trade school, he'd worked as a mechanic before deciding to pursue a career in construction.

As Ryder stepped out onto the job site, he was greeted by the familiar sight of his crew's truck, adorned with the words "Jackson Crew" emblazoned on the side panel. The smell of freshly cut wood and diesel fuel filled his nostrils as he grabbed his tool belt and began to make his way down the highway. The asphalt stretched out before him like a golden snake, leading to the bustling town of Oakdale and the endless possibilities that lay beyond.

Ryder's thoughts were interrupted by the sound of his boss's voice over the radio, calling out the day's schedule and reminding everyone to take care on the roads. Ryder nodded in response, then turned back to scan the horizon for any signs of trouble. The morning was young, but he knew that with hard work and dedication, he'd be able to keep Oakdale running smoothly all day long.
