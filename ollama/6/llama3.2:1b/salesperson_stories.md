It was a crisp autumn morning in the bustling city of New Haven, where the sounds of car horns and chatter filled the air as people went about their daily routines. The sun was just starting to peek over the horizon, casting a warm glow over the towering skyscrapers that dominated the city's skyline.

In a small office nestled in the heart of the city, Rachel Lee sat at her desk, sipping on a lukewarm coffee as she checked her phone for what felt like the hundredth time that morning. As a salesperson with the prestigious marketing firm, Smith & Co., Rachel was no stranger to long hours and endless meetings. But today was different - today was going to be one of those days where everything seemed to be going wrong.

Rachel's name tag read "Rachel Lee, Senior Account Manager," but few people outside of her office knew her real name. She had long since adopted the alias "Rae" as a nod to her more outgoing personality and the high-stakes world of sales she inhabited. Still, on days like this, Rachel couldn't help but feel like she was living a secret life.

As she gazed out the window at the crowded streets below, Rachel's thoughts turned to the meeting with their new potential client that morning. They were a major tech company looking for a customized solution to meet the growing demands of their industry. Rachel had been working on this account for weeks, and she was determined to make a good impression.

But as she sat there, sipping her coffee and trying to muster up some enthusiasm, Rachel couldn't shake off the feeling that something was off. It started with a subtle nagging sense of unease, followed by a growing knot in her stomach. She pushed the thought aside, telling herself it was just nerves - after all, she had handled tough clients before.

Little did Rachel know, today would be one of those days where everything went wrong.
