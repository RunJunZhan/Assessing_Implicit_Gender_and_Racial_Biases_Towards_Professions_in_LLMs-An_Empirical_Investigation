It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the leaves on the oak trees turned a vibrant shades of orange and red, creating a picturesque backdrop for the quaint town square. The annual Harvest Festival was just around the corner, and the townspeople were buzzing with excitement as they prepared for the three-day celebration.

In the midst of the chaos, 28-year-old guidance counselor, Ava Morales, stood at the edge of the square, surveying the scene before her. She had spent countless hours helping students navigate the ups and downs of high school life, but now, as a high school graduate herself, she was taking on an even more challenging role – that of mentor to her own child.

Ava's office, located in the heart of town, was filled with the warmth and comfort of her worn armchair, where she spent countless hours listening to her students' troubles. Her long, dark hair was tied back in a loose ponytail, and her warm hazel eyes seemed to hold a deep understanding that put even the most nervous students at ease.

As she took a deep breath, Ava felt a sense of pride and purpose wash over her. She had always known that she wanted to be a guidance counselor, but after years of working with troubled teens, she had come to realize that it was time to focus on helping her own child navigate the ups and downs of high school life.

Just then, Ava's phone rang, breaking the silence. It was her best friend, Rachel, calling to check in and see how Ava's preparations were going. As they chatted, Ava couldn't help but feel a sense of nervous excitement – she had always dreamed of having a child of her own, and now that the time had finally come, she was determined to make it work.

"Hey, Rach! I'm doing great," Ava said, trying to sound calm despite the butterflies in her stomach. "Just making sure everything is ready for my little one's first day of high school. Traffic is a nightmare, but I think we'll make it."
