It was a crisp autumn morning on the sprawling ranch of Oakwood, nestled in the heart of rural Texas. The sun was just starting to rise over the rolling hills and vast pastures, casting a warm golden light over the landscape. Jack McCoy, a rugged and weathered rancher in his late 40s, woke up before dawn to begin his daily routine.

As he rubbed the sleep from his eyes, the silence of the small town of Willow Creek was suddenly pierced by the sound of horseshoes on gravel outside his bedroom window. He sat up in bed and swung his legs over the side, planting his feet firmly on the ground. The smell of fresh coffee wafted from downstairs, and Jack's stomach growled in anticipation.

He made his way to the kitchen, where his wife, Emily, had already brewed a pot of coffee and was busy preparing breakfast for their two children, Lily and Finn. The fire in the hearth crackled and popped as they chatted quietly about the day ahead. Jack poured himself a cup of black coffee and sat down at the table with them, taking a moment to appreciate the peacefulness of the morning.

"Good morning, sunshine," Emily said, smiling up at him from across the table.

"It's about time you woke up," Jack replied, grinning back at her. "I've got a long day ahead of me."
