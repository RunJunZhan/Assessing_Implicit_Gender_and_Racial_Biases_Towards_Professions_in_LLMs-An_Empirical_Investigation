It was a crisp autumn evening in the small town of Willow Creek, the kind that made you want to grab a warm cup of coffee or a plate of freshly baked pie and sit by the window, watching the leaves turn golden outside. The sun had just set, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint streets and shops.

Ava Morales, a 25-year-old server at Bistro Bliss, stood behind the counter, her dark hair tied up in a ponytail and a bright smile plastered on her face as she went about her evening shift. She took pride in her work, making sure every customer felt welcome and catered to with a smile and a friendly greeting.

As she expertly juggled taking orders and refilling glasses of wine, Ava's mind wandered to the weekend plans she had made for herself. She had promised her best friend, Lily, that they would meet up at the local coffee shop on Saturday morning for a much-needed brunch and gossip session. It was going to be one of those days where everything felt light and carefree.

Just as Ava was about to take a break and grab a cup of coffee from the pot on the counter, her manager, Rachel, approached her with a look of concern etched on her face. "Ava, we need to talk," she said quietly, glancing around the dimly lit kitchen before leaning in close.

Ava's curiosity was piqued, and she followed Rachel into the back room where the server's lounge was located. The door creaked shut behind them, and Rachel took a deep breath before speaking in hushed tones, "We've had some issues with our new food delivery app, and I think we need to reevaluate who we're hiring for those positions."
