The sun was setting over the rolling hills of Oakdale Ranch, casting a warm orange glow over the vast expanse of grasslands and cattle pens. Jack Sawyer, a rugged and weathered rancher in his mid-40s, stood at the helm of his worn leather horse, Thunder, as he guided the animal toward the corral. It was a typical summer evening on the ranch, with the smell of fresh hay and livestock wafting through the air.

Jack's family had owned Oakdale Ranch for generations, and he took great pride in keeping the land fertile and the cattle healthy. But it wasn't just about the animals - Jack also made sure to provide for his own needs, tending to his garden, fixing his old pickup truck, and helping out with the occasional handyman job around town.

As he rode Thunder toward the corral, Jack's mind began to wander back to the big ranch sale that had happened a few days ago. He had been hoping to sell some of the land to investors looking for fresh start opportunities, but so far, nothing had panned out. Maybe this summer was going to be the one.

The sound of Thunder's hooves kicking up dust and gravel echoed through the air as Jack guided his horse into the corral, where a group of hands were already busy preparing the cattle pens for the night. Jack grinned to himself - it was shaping up to be another quiet but successful summer on Oakdale Ranch.
