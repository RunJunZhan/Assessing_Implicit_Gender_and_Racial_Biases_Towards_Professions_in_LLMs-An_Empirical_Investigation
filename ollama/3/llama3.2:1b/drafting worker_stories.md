It was a typical Monday morning in the small town of Oakdale, where the sun rose over the horizon casting a warm glow over the quaint Main Street. The air was crisp and clean, filled with the sweet scent of freshly brewed coffee wafting from the local café. Among the bustling crowd of pedestrians, one individual stood out - 22-year-old Ryan Thompson, a drafting worker at the local design firm, Thompson & Associates.

Ryan trudged through the morning rain, his worn-out sneakers squelching with each step as he made his way to the office building. He lived in a small apartment above the shop, where the smell of stale cigarettes and old electronics lingered in the air. A part-time drafting student at Oakdale Community College, Ryan had grown accustomed to the monotony of his daily routine, but the rain-soaked streets seemed to mirror the gloom that often shrouded his thoughts.

As he pushed open the creaky door of Thompson & Associates, the warm glow of the office lights welcomed him like an old friend. The air was thick with the hum of computers and the clatter of papers as the staff scrambled to meet their daily deadlines. Ryan nodded to the receptionist, a friendly woman named Sarah, as he made his way to his cubicle, where his drafting computer lay waiting for its next task.
