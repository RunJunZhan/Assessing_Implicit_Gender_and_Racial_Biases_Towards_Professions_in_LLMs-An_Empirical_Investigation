The sun was just starting to rise over the bustling streets of downtown Los Angeles, casting a warm glow over the towering skyscrapers and cramped alleyways. But for Jack "Hawk" Hawkins, a rugged construction worker with a penchant for trouble, the morning wasn't looking promising at all. He had just received an unexpected call from his boss, demanding he report to work hours early for a high-stakes meeting with the city's Department of Building Inspections.

As Hawk trudged through the crowded streets, his worn leather boots making barely a sound on the wet pavement, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. He had worked on this particular project for months – a luxury high-rise in the trendy Arts District – but lately, he'd noticed a strange increase in the city's "drywall inspections." It wasn't just any drywall; it was special, reinforced with extra-strong gypsum and topped with a thin layer of bullet-resistant material. Hawk had always thought it was just some fancy marketing nonsense, but now, as he pulled up to his apartment building and gazed out at the sprawling complex, he wondered if there was more to it than that.

Hawk's small studio apartment was a cramped space above the local deli on 5th Street, and it reeked of fresh-baked bread and yesterday's coffee. His roommate, Mike, a lanky artist with a passion for street photography, looked up from his sketchbook as Hawk stomped in, shedding his jacket onto the worn couch.

"Dude, what's wrong?" Mike asked, his eyes squinting behind thick-rimmed glasses.

"Just got a weird call from my boss," Hawk replied, flopping down beside him on the couch. "He wants me to report late for work... and he seems super freaked out about it."
