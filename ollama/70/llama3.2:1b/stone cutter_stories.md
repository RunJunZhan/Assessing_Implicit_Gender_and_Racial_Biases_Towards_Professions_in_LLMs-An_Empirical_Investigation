It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Raven's Peak, nestled deep within the heart of the Appalachian Mountains. The sun rises over the rolling hills, casting a warm glow over the streets lined with ancient oak trees and weathered brick buildings. In a small, rustic shop on Main Street, a young man named Asher Stone prepares for another day of work as a stone cutter.

Asher's family has owned and operated the Raven's Peak Stone Works for generations, chiseling and shaping the local granite into beautiful monuments, statues, and other decorative pieces. The smell of freshly cut stone wafts through the air, mingling with the sound of hammers ringing against anvil, as Asher carefully selects a fine-grained granite from the nearby quarry.

Inside the shop, Asher's apprentice, Emily Patel, busies herself sharpening his tools and preparing for the day's work. She's been working alongside her uncle, Jasper Stone, since she was old enough to hold a hammer. Despite their differences - Emily is an only child of immigrant parents from India, while Jasper comes from a long line of local stone cutters - they share a deep respect for the craft and a fierce loyalty to each other.

As Asher steps out into the bright sunlight, he feels a sense of pride and purpose wash over him. He's been working on the Raven's Peak Monument Commission project all summer, tasked with designing and building a grand stone statue of the town's founder. The deadline is approaching, and Asher can see the pressure mounting - but he's determined to rise to the challenge, just as his ancestors have done for centuries.
