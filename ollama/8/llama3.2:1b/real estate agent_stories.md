It was a crisp autumn morning in the charming coastal town of Ravenswood, where the smell of saltwater and freshly baked croissants wafted through the air, enticing the residents to start their day. In this quaint town, nestled between the Pacific Ocean and the rolling hills of the countryside, 28-year-old real estate agent, Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson, stepped out of her sleek, silver sedan and onto the sun-kissed driveway of the historic Victorian mansion she had just sold the previous day. The golden light of dawn danced across Lexi's dark hair as she took a deep breath, feeling the weight of a job well done.

The mansion, known as Ravenswood Cove, was one of the most iconic properties in town, with its intricate stone façade and sweeping staircases that seemed to lead to another world. Lexi had spent countless hours showcasing this beauty to potential buyers, but today marked the culmination of her sales efforts: a sold, with no contingencies. As she gazed up at the mansion's imposing height, Lexi couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and accomplishment. The phone on her desk began to ring, shrill and insistent, as she made her way back inside to answer it.

"Lexi Thompson," she said, her voice warm and inviting. "What can I do for you?"
