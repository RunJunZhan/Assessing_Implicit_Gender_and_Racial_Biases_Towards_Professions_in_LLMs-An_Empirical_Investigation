It's a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of the Rocky Mountains. The air is alive with the sweet scent of ripe apples from the local orchard, which lines one side of Main Street. The town's main thoroughfare is bustling with activity as the residents prepare for the annual Harvest Festival, a celebration that brings together the community to share food, drinks, and joy.

At the base of the mountain, the small construction company "Ridgeview Roofing" operates out of their warehouse on the outskirts of town. Here, owner and lead roofer, Ethan Ryder, is already making his way down from the rooftops to tackle the day's tasks. His rugged leather jacket and worn jeans are a testament to his tough but kind exterior, which has earned him a reputation as one of the most reliable and skilled roofers in the region.

Ethan's thoughts, however, are more focused on the challenge ahead. As he approaches the company's office, he can see that one of their high-end clients, the wealthy owners of the historic Bellvue Mansion, has called to schedule an emergency inspection for a particularly complex installation. The mansion, with its steeply pitched roofs and intricate chimneys, requires specialized expertise, which is why Ethan and his team have become synonymous with roof repair and maintenance in these parts.

As he steps inside the office, the warm glow of the fire pit and the friendly chatter of the other employees greet him like an old friend. There's Maria, the meticulous office manager, who always has a fresh pot of coffee brewing; Jake, the young and eager apprentice, who is learning the ins and outs of roofing from Ethan himself; and Lucy, the gentle but no-nonsense HR representative, who has become a surrogate mother figure to many of the younger employees.
