It was a crisp autumn morning in the small coastal town of Ravenswood, where the misty fog rolled in off the Pacific Ocean like a damp blanket. The year was 1922, and the town was buzzing with excitement as the annual Harvest Festival was just around the corner. As the largest tour guide in Ravenswood, Emily Wilson had spent countless hours perfecting her knowledge of the town's history, landmarks, and hidden gems.

Emily stood at the helm of her vintage bus, a restored 1925 Chevrolet, adorned with colorful hand-painted murals depicting scenes from Ravenswood's past. The bus, affectionately known as "The Coastal Cruiser," chugged along the winding roads that led to the town's famous beachside resort, where the wealthy elite would come to indulge in sun-kissed fun and fine dining.

As Emily navigated the crowded streets, her bright red hair bobbed with each turn of the wheel, drawing admiring glances from the townspeople. She had lived in Ravenswood all her life, and her love for the town was only matched by her passion for sharing its secrets with visitors from far and wide. Today, she was preparing to embark on another thrilling tour, one that would take her to the old lighthouse on the coast, where a long-forgotten legend awaited...
