It was a drizzly autumn evening in the city of Ashwood, where the streets were lined with towering trees that seemed to stretch up to the sky like nature's own cathedral. The air was thick with the scent of damp earth and decaying leaves, and the sound of raindrops pattering against the pavement created a soothing melody that belied the turmoil that lay beneath.

In a small, unassuming office tucked away on a side street, a figure emerged from the shadows. This was Ethan Blackwood, a private detective with a reputation for being one of the city's most skilled and discreet investigators. His worn leather trenchcoat was emblazoned with the letter "B" in bold, silver letters, and his dark hair was slicked back, revealing a prominent brow line that seemed to hold a thousand midnights within.

Ethan stood at the entrance of his office, a cramped but efficient space filled with stacks of dusty files, worn leather armchairs, and a large, wooden desk that seemed to have been dragged from a century past. A faint hum of silence surrounded him, punctuated only by the occasional distant rumble of thunder or the soft crackle of a cigarette.

It was on this drizzly autumn evening that Ethan's phone rang, shrill in the stillness. He lifted the receiver, his eyes narrowing as he listened to the voice on the other end. The words were indistinguishable, but the tone was unmistakable – a mixture of urgency and desperation that seemed to vibrate through every cell in Ethan's body.

"Blackwood Investigations," he growled into the phone, his voice low and even. "What can I do for you?"
