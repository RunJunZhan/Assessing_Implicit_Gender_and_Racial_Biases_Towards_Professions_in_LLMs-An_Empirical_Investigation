It was a chilly autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the sun struggled to rise above the rolling hills and dense forests that surrounded it. The air was crisp and clean, filled with the sweet scent of freshly cut grass and the distant hint of wood smoke from the local bakery. This was the kind of place where everyone knew each other's names, and the local diner, "The Cozy Cup", was always bustling with activity.

At 22 years old, Ethan Wright had spent his entire life in Willow Creek. He grew up working at The Cozy Cup as a janitor, after saving up for several years to finally earn enough money to take a break from college. Now, on days like this, he loved nothing more than donning his worn-out scrubs and sweeping the floors, while listening to the familiar sounds of the diner coming to life.

Ethan's eyes sparkled with a hint of mischief as he made his way to the back room, where he stored his cleaning supplies. He had just finished loading up his cart with fresh trash bags and recycling bins when he noticed something odd - a small piece of paper on the counter, partially hidden behind the cash register. Ethan wrinkled his nose, not liking the smell of stale coffee that clung to it, but pulled it out, curious about what could have left it there.
