Here's a potential story opening and setting:

It was a crisp autumn morning in New York City when Julian Saint Clair first stepped onto the sleek, silver steps of his high-rise office building on Wall Street. The warm glow of sunrise streaming through the skyscraper's windows illuminated the polished marble floor beneath his feet as he made his way to the elevator. At 35, Julian was a master of his craft – a seasoned stock broker with over a decade of experience navigating the cutthroat world of finance.

As he rode the elevator up to the 23rd floor, Julian couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and accomplishment. His family's financial empire, Saint Clair Enterprises, had built its reputation on shrewd investments and astute risk-taking. Julian's own father, a former Wall Street legend, had taught him the ins and outs of the industry from a young age.

The doors slid open with a soft chime, and Julian stepped out into a bustling morning scene: traders huddled over laptops, phone operators fielding calls, and the sweet scent of freshly brewed coffee wafted through the air. The sounds of car horns and sirens blended together in a symphony of urban life as Julian made his way to his office – a spacious, glass-walled suite overlooking the Manhattan skyline.

As he pushed open the door, a warm smile spread across his face, and the soft hum of fluorescent lights greeted him like an old friend. This was his domain, where the fast-paced world of finance came alive, and Julian Saint Clair was its undisputed master.
