It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the scent of freshly baked pastries wafted from the local bakery on Main Street. The sun was just starting to rise over the rooftops, casting a warm glow over the quaint shops and homes. Among them was "Salvatore's Salads" - the cozy barber shop owned by Salvatore "Sal" Bianchi. A man in his late 50s with a wild shock of curly brown hair and a bushy mustache, Sal had spent years perfecting his craft as a barber to the residents of Willow Creek.

As he stood outside his shop, adjusting his apron for what felt like the hundredth time that morning, the sound of chatter and laughter drifted out into the street. The townsfolk were already starting their day, sipping coffee and chatting with friends in the local café down the block. Sal took a deep breath, feeling the familiar sense of contentment wash over him. This was his happy place - among the buzz of scissors, the clinking of cups, and the soft hum of conversation.

Just as Sal was about to open up the shop for business, a sleek black car pulled into the parking lot, sending a plume of dust flying behind it. The driver, a striking young woman with piercing green eyes and raven-black hair, stepped out onto the sidewalk, her gaze scanning the street until it landed on Sal's shop. For a moment, they just stared at each other - Sal's eyebrows arched in surprise, while she smiled coolly and nodded curtly before turning to get back into the car.
