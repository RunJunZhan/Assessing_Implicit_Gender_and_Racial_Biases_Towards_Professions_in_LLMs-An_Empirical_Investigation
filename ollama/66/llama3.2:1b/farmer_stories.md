It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled deep within the rolling hills of rural Ohio. The air was alive with the sweet scent of ripe apples from the orchard that sprawled across the outskirts of town, owned by 35-year-old farmer, Jesse Everett. His worn leather boots scuffled against the dry earth as he made his way to the farmhouse, the rusty gate creaking in protest.

Jesse had lived on this land for over a decade, learning the ins and outs of farming from his father before him. He took pride in his work, tending to every crop with precision and care. As he climbed the worn steps of the farmhouse, a warm golden light spilled out onto the porch, beckoning him inside.

Inside, Jesse's wife, Emily, was already up and bustling around the kitchen, her curly brown hair tied back in a loose bun. She smiled as she caught sight of Jesse's boots on the porch, and the flour dusting his face was a sure sign that he'd been up since before dawn, checking on the crops.

"Morning, love," Emily called out, holding up a steaming cup of coffee. "You're just in time for breakfast."
