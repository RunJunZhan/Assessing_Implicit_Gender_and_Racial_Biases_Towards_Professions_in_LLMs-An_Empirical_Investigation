The sun was just starting to peek over the horizon, casting a warm golden light over the rolling hills and lush green pastures of Oakwood Manor, the grand estate that had been in the possession of the wealthy and reclusive Mr. Edward Blackstone for generations. Groundskeeper James "Jim" Thompson, a ruggedly handsome man with a quick smile and a kind heart, was already hard at work, tending to the meticulously maintained lawns and gardens of the manor.

As he made his way through the dewy grass, Jim couldn't help but feel a sense of pride and purpose. He had been working as groundskeeper at Oakwood for five years now, and he loved every minute of it. The work was physically demanding, but it was also a privilege to be able to keep the manor and its surroundings looking their best.

As he approached the grand entrance, Jim noticed that something was off. The front door was slightly ajar, and the windows were all closed, as if no one had entered or left in a while. Jim's curiosity was piqued, and he pushed open the door to investigate further.

Inside, the manor was quiet and still, except for the distant sound of birds chirping and leaves rustling in the breeze. Jim fished out his key and turned it in the lock, revealing a dimly lit hallway that stretched out into the darkness. He called out to see if anyone answered, but there was only silence.

It wasn't until he stumbled upon a piece of paper on the floor, tucked away behind a potted plant, that Jim realized something was amiss. The paper bore a cryptic message: "They're coming for us."
