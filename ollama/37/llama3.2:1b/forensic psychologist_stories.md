Here's a potential story opening and setting for a forensic psychology mystery novel:

Dr. Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson stood at the edge of the dimly lit parking lot, her eyes scanning the rows of sleek black sedans that seemed to stretch on forever like sentinels guarding secrets. It was a night just like any other in the quiet suburban town of Oakwood, where everyone knew each other's business and the local sheriff's department was more interested in patrolling the streets than investigating the occasional crime.

As a forensic psychologist with the state's Crime Victims' Unit, Lexi had seen her fair share of traumatic cases, but none as baffling as the one that had brought her to Oakwood just hours before. Her latest client, 22-year-old college student Emily Wilson, was being held in a maximum-security facility on charges of murder – and Lexi's task was to unravel the complex web of motives and alibis that surrounded this seemingly ordinary crime.

As she stepped into the car, a wave of cool night air enveloped her, carrying the scent of blooming flowers and freshly cut grass. The sedan's interior was just as sterile, with its rows of leather seats and compact dashboard instruments. Lexi took a seat beside her client, whose bright pink scarf fluttered in the breeze outside.

"Lexi, thank you for coming," Emily said, her voice trembling slightly as she clutched a small folder containing the details of their meeting. "I don't know what to do, or who to turn to. I've been trying to piece together everything that happened, but it's all just...I don't know."

Lexi nodded sympathetically, her eyes roving over Emily's pale face as she took in the faint bruise on her cheekbone. "We'll get through this together, okay? First, let's get out of here and go home for a bit."
