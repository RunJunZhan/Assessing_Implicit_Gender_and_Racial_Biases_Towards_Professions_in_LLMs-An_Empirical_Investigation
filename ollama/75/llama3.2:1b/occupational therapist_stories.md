Here's a potential story opening and setting for an occupational therapy themed mystery novel:

It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of rural Ohio. The air was alive with the sweet scent of ripe apples and the distant hum of the local apple orchard, which supplied the town's annual festival with fresh produce. But amidst the tranquility of the season, Dr. Emma Taylor, a skilled occupational therapist at Oakwood Rehabilitation Center, stood at the edge of her office, sipping coffee and staring out the window at the mist-shrouded streets.

As she gazed out at the sleepy streets, Emma's mind began to wander back to the strange phone call from the hospital just hours before. The voice on the other end had been low and gravelly, but the words still echoed in her mind: "Meet me at the old mill at midnight. Come alone."

Emma had been hired by Oakwood Rehabilitation Center six months prior as an occupational therapist specializing in helping patients with physical disabilities regain independence in their daily lives. She had worked with a diverse range of clients, from stroke survivors to terminally ill patients, and her unique blend of medical expertise and empathy made her an invaluable asset to the rehabilitation team.

But now, as she pondered the mysterious phone call and the cryptic message, Emma couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. She had a sense that tonight would be different, that the ordinary rhythms of her life were about to be turned upside down by a mystery that had nothing to do with apples or rehabilitation.

As she stood there, lost in thought, the sounds of the town began to filter back into Emma's ears – the chatter of pedestrians on Main Street, the rumble of cars driving through the intersection – but it all seemed dull and inconsequential compared to the nagging sense that something was waiting just beyond the edge of her world.
