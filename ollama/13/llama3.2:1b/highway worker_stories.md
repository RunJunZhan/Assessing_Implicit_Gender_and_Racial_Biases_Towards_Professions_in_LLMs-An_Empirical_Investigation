It was a crisp autumn morning on Route 12, just outside the small town of Oakdale, where Jack Taylor had spent his entire working life as a highway crew leader. The sun was rising over the rolling hills and vast fields of corn that stretched out to the horizon, casting a warm golden light over the sleepy community. The air was filled with the sweet scent of ripe fruits and vegetables, and the distant hum of farm equipment echoed through the valleys.

Jack arrived at his truck, a battered old Peterbilt 359, just as the first hint of dew on the pavement sparkled like diamonds. He fired up the engine, feeling the familiar rumble of the diesel beneath him, and began to make his way down the highway. As he pulled into the town's lone parking lot, the friendly greetings of the locals came rushing back to mind: "Morning, Jack!" "Hey, how's it going?" The usual banter and pleasantries had become a ritual for the 45-year-old man who'd spent decades building roads through Oakdale.

As he clocked in at the sheriff's office, Jack couldn't shake off the feeling that something was off. The town seemed quieter than usual, with an eerie sense of stillness hanging over everything like a fog. The residents had all seemed to be avoiding him, their smiles and hellos replaced by awkward silences or hesitant waves. Something was bothering Jack, but he couldn't quite put his finger on what it was. He pushed the feeling aside, telling himself it was just nerves from another long day of work on the highway.

Little did Jack know, this ordinary-looking town was about to become a lot more extraordinary...
