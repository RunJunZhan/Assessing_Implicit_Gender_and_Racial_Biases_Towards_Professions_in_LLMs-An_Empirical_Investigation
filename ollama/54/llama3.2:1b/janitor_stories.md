It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the sun rose over the quaint Main Street, casting a warm glow over the cobblestone sidewalks. The air was alive with the sweet scent of freshly fallen leaves and the distant chirping of birds, signaling the start of another beautiful day.

Among the row of weathered Victorian houses, one structure stood out - the old Oakwood Manor, a grand estate that had been in the hands of the kindly Mr. Edward Blackstone for generations. The once-majestic mansion now wore a layer of peeling paint and faded ivy, its windows like empty eyes staring back at the passing world.

In a small janitor's closet tucked away on the second floor, Emrys "Em" Thompson stirred to life. A soft-spoken man in his late 40s with a wild shock of curly brown hair, Em had spent years cleaning up after Mr. Blackstone's grand parties and lavish meals. Despite his mundane job, Em took pride in keeping Oakwood Manor spotless, even as he dreamed of more than just dusting and sweeping.

As Em began his daily routine, the sounds of the town drifted through the open window - the chatter of pedestrians on Main Street, the clanging of pots from the nearby diner, and the occasional bark of a stray dog. The soft hum of the streetlamps cast an ethereal glow over the scene, illuminating the worn wooden floorboards beneath Em's feet.

With a quiet satisfaction, Em started his chores, preparing for another day of maintaining the grandeur of Oakwood Manor - and perhaps, just perhaps, uncovering secrets hidden within its ancient walls.
