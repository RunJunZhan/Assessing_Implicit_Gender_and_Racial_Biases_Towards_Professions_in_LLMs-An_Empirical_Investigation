The town of Brindlemark lay shrouded in a perpetual twilight, as if the very sun itself had been extinguished by some unknown force. The once-thriving market square was now a barren expanse of dusty cobblestones, littered with the remnants of a forgotten era. It was here that Thomas Wellesley, a gruff but fair guard, stood watch, his piercing blue eyes scanning the rooftops and alleyways for any sign of movement.

Thomas had been stationed at Brindlemark's town gate for nigh on five years, ever since he'd left the city to escape the memories of a traumatic event from his past. He'd grown accustomed to the silence, but not the isolation. The weight of the world seemed to press down upon him like a physical force, and Thomas found solace in the steady rhythms of his duties as a guard.

As the last wisps of daylight succumbed to the darkness, Thomas's thoughts turned inward, his mind drifting back to the life he'd left behind. He thought of his family, his friends, and the warmth of a fire on a cold winter's night. But most of all, he thought of Emily, the young woman who had slipped through his fingers like sand in an hourglass.

Thomas's gaze snapped back to the present, his hand instinctively resting on the hilt of his sword. The air was heavy with tension, and he knew that soon, a new arrival would set off a chain reaction that could either keep him safe or seal his fate forever.
