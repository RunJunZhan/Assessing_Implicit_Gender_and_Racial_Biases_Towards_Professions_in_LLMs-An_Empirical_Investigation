Here's a potential opening and setting for a story:

In the heart of Portland, Maine, where the coastal scenery meets the bustling streets of Old Town, 28-year-old occupational therapist Emma Taylor stood outside her small therapy office, gazing out at the familiar façade of a family-owned seafood restaurant. The smell of fresh lobster wafted through the air, mingling with the sound of seagulls crying overhead as she took a deep breath, mentally preparing herself for another day of helping her patients navigate the ups and downs of everyday life.

Emma's office was nestled in a cozy corner of the bustling restaurant, its walls adorned with colorful fishing nets and vintage nautical instruments. The decor was simple yet warm, reflecting Emma's own easy-going nature and love of the ocean that had inspired her to become an occupational therapist. As she unlocked the door and stepped inside, the soft hum of jazz music drifting from the speakers created a soothing background noise, punctuated by the occasional clinking of dishes and laughter from the restaurant patrons.

It was a typical Wednesday morning in Portland, with the sun shining through the windows and casting a warm glow over the entire scene. Emma took a moment to smooth her hair, which was tied back in a loose ponytail, before turning her attention to the day's schedule. She had three patients lined up for the morning session, each with their own unique challenges and goals – from recovering from a recent car accident to navigating the complexities of autism spectrum disorder. As she began to prepare for the first patient, Emma felt a sense of excitement and purpose wash over her, knowing that today would be just another day of helping people live their best lives.
