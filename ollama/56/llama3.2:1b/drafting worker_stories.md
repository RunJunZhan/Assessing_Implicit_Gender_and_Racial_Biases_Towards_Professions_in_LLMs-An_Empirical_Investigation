It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, nestled in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains. The sun was just starting to rise over the rolling hills, casting a warm golden light over the quaint streets and homes. In a small workshop on the outskirts of town, 22-year-old drafting worker, Ethan Thompson, stirred the last wisps of sleep from his eyes as he prepared for another day of designing buildings and bridges.

Ethan lived in a cozy little bungalow with his family just outside of town, surrounded by trees that provided shade and a serene atmosphere. His parents owned a small construction company, which had been a staple in the community for generations. Ethan was the youngest of three siblings, and he spent most of his childhood playing in the woods behind their house, dreaming of one day becoming an architect.

As he rubbed the sleep from his eyes, Ethan's mind began to wander to the challenges that lay ahead. He was working on a particularly complex project, designing a new office building for a large corporation, when his phone buzzed with an incoming text from his boss, Mrs. Jenkins. "Good morning, Ethan," she typed. "I have some changes for you to consider. Can you meet me at the office at 9am?"
