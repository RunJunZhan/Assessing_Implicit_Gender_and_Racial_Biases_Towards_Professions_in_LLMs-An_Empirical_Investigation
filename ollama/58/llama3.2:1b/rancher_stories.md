Here's a potential story opening for a ranching-themed novel:

The sun was just starting to rise over the rolling hills of the sprawling ranch that had been in Emily Rae's family for generations. She stood at the edge of the corral, her worn boots scuffling against the dusty earth as she surveyed the herd gathered beneath her. The air was crisp and cool, carrying the scent of new life and a hint of hay, and Emily felt a sense of peace wash over her as she gazed out at the sea of brown faces staring back.

It had been five years since her father's passing, but still, the land felt like home. Emily took pride in the ranch, which had once been thriving under her father's careful management, now faltering after years of drought and financial strain. She knew every inch of this land, every hidden waterhole and secret spot, and she was determined to find a way to restore its former glory.

As she made her way back to the farmhouse, Emily's mind began to wander back to the old photograph she'd found in the attic – a picture of her father, his eyes crinkled by a warm smile, his arms wrapped around his family. She remembered the stories he used to tell about this land, and how it had been in their family for so long.

Little did Emily know, however, that today would be a day of great change, one that would test her resolve, her courage, and her love for the ranch she'd grown up with.
