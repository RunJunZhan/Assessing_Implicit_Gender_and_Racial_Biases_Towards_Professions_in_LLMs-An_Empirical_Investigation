Here's a potential story opening and setting for an occupational therapy-themed novel:

---

The misty dawn crept over the sleepy town of Willow Creek, casting a mystical veil over its quaint streets and cozy homes. Dr. Emily Jourdan, a wise and compassionate occupational therapist in her late 30s, stood at the edge of the lake, gazing out at the water's surface. The soft lapping of the waves against the shore was the only sound breaking the silence as she took a deep breath, savoring the stillness of the morning.

Emily's small, wooden cottage on the outskirts of town served as her sanctuary – a place where she could retreat from the world and focus on helping others. As an occupational therapist, she had spent the better part of her career working with patients who struggled to find meaning and purpose in their daily lives, often due to injuries, illnesses, or neurological disorders.

As she stepped into her cottage, Emily's eyes adjusted to the dim light, and she made her way to the kitchen, where a steaming pot of tea waited on the stovetop. She poured herself a cup, savoring the comforting flavors as she mentally prepared for another day of assessing, counseling, and rehabilitation. The quiet morning routine was one Emily cherished – it gave her time to reflect on her own life, reconnect with loved ones, and recharge her energy for the challenges ahead.

Little did Emily know, today's patients would bring a fresh set of challenges, and her skills as an occupational therapist would be put to the test in ways she never could have imagined. The phone rang just then, shrill and insistent, breaking the peaceful morning atmosphere.
