Here's a potential opening for a story about an interior decorator:

---

The sun was setting over the bustling streets of New York City, casting a warm orange glow over the towering skyscrapers that seemed to stretch on forever. But for 25-year-old interior designer and decorator, Alexandra "Lexi" Thompson, it wasn't just any ordinary day in the city that got her excited. Lexi had just received an email from a prestigious interior design firm, offering her a job as the lead designer on their high-end residential division.

As she sat in her small apartment in Brooklyn, surrounded by paint swatches and design books, Lexi felt a thrill of anticipation. She had always dreamed of working with luxury clients and creating bespoke spaces that reflected their unique personalities. And now, it seemed like her dream was finally within reach.

The doorbell rang, breaking the silence. It was her best friend and confidant, Jamie, who stood in the doorway with a big smile on her face. "Lexi, I have some amazing news!" she exclaimed. "I just got a callback from the firm about that job you've been wanting for months!"

Lexi's heart skipped a beat as she took Jamie's hand in hers. She had given up hope of landing this role, but now it seemed like fate was finally smiling on her.

As they hugged, Lexi's phone buzzed with an incoming text from the firm. "Hey, Lexi, we'd like you to come in for an interview tomorrow at 10am sharp. They've got a special project lined up that requires your expertise."

Lexi's eyes widened as she read the message. A high-end residential project? This was exactly what she had been waiting for – and now it seemed like her big chance was finally within reach.

"Jamie, we have to go," Lexi said, tugging on her friend's arm. "I've got a meeting tomorrow with the firm."

As they made their way to Jamie's apartment, Lexi couldn't help but feel a sense of excitement and anticipation. She knew that this was going to be a big step for her career, and she was ready to take it.

But little did Lexi know, the project that had caught the firm's attention was more than just any ordinary residential property...
