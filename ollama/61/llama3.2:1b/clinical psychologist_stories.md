Here's a potential story opening and setting for a clinical psychology thriller:

The mist-shrouded streets of Ashwood, Massachusetts, were always at their most unforgiving in the late autumn, when the trees seemed to lean inward, as if trying to snare the last wisps of summer's warmth. Dr. Julia Moreno, a renowned clinical psychologist with a sharp mind and an even sharper instinct for human nature, stood at her office window, gazing out into the gloom.

It was a chilly November evening, and the kindling fires in the town's central square had long since burned down to embers. The only light came from the soft glow of streetlamps, casting a golden haze over the rows of neatly manicured lawns and the towering walls of Ashwood University, where Julia had spent the better part of her career.

As she stood there, lost in thought, a faint scratching noise caught her attention. It was a low, deliberate scratch, like fingernails on wood. Julia's trained eyes narrowed as she raised an eyebrow, her mind already racing with possibilities. Who could be out there, and what were they doing at this hour? She took a deep breath, smoothing her hair into place, and pushed open the creaking door of her office.

"Julia Moreno," a gravelly voice called out from the back of the room. "I need to see you."
