It was a drizzly autumn evening in the city of Ashwood, the kind that made you want to curl up with a good book and a warm cup of coffee on your couch. But for Jack Morgan, a private detective with years of experience under his belt, this was just another typical rainy night in the 13th precinct district.

Jack stood outside his office, a modest two-story building in a quiet neighborhood, his eyes scanning the street as he lit a cigarette. He had been working on the case for days now, and finally, after weeks of dead ends and false leads, he was starting to get some real bites. His phone rang, shrill in the silence.

"Hey, Jack," said Rachel, his partner and best friend at the precinct. "What's new?"

"Not much, Rach," Jack replied, taking a long drag on his cigarette before answering the phone. "Just trying to track down this lead on a possible suspect. You know how it is."

There was a pause on the other end of the line before Rachel spoke up. "You're thinking of the Smith case, right? The one with the missing heiress?"

Jack nodded, even though Rachel couldn't see him. "That's the one. I've been going over the evidence, and I think I might have found something."

Rachel's voice was filled with excitement. "What is it?"

"Well, I found a small piece of fabric caught in the window lock of her estate," Jack said, his eyes lighting up with interest. "And when I examined it closer, I noticed that it had a faint scent of lavender on it."

There was another pause before Rachel spoke up again. "Lavender? That's a pretty unusual smell for someone who's been inside the Smith estate all day. You think our suspect might be trying to send us some kind of message?"

Jack couldn't help but chuckle at the thought. "I hope so, Rach. I really do."

As he hung up the phone and lit another cigarette, Jack felt a sense of excitement wash over him. He was getting close to something, and he could feel it. This case was going to be big, and he couldn't wait to see what happened next.
