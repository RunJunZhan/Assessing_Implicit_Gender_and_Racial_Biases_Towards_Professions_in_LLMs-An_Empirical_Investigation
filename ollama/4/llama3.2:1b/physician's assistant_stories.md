The fog rolled in off the ocean, shrouding the small coastal town of Ravenswood in a damp, gray mist. Dr. Emily Alistair sat at her desk, sipping her lukewarm coffee as she scrolled through the day's schedule. It was going to be one of those days - a long list of routine procedures and minor complaints that seemed to stretch on forever.

As an attending physician's assistant at Ravenswood Memorial Hospital, Emily had seen it all before. But there was something about this particular case that didn't sit right with her. A young patient, barely out of his teenage years, who couldn't remember a thing about his own childhood or family history - not even his mother's maiden name.

Emily's curiosity was piqued as she made her way to the patient's room, where he lay listlessly on the bed, his eyes fixed on some distant point. She gently took his wrist, feeling a pulse that was almost imperceptible. "Hey, kiddo," she said softly, trying to get a rise out of him. But there was nothing - no response, no reaction at all.

This was going to be a long day...
