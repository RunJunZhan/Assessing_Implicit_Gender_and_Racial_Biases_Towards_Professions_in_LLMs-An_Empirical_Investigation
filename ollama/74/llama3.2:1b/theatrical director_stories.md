Aurora Wellesley, known to her fans as Rory, was known for her bold and unapologetic productions that explored themes of identity, class, and social justice. Her latest show, "The House on Orchard Street," was set to premiere at the iconic Helen Hayes Theatre, a venue that had hosted some of the most iconic plays in American history.

As Rory stood backstage, sipping champagne and surveying her crew, she couldn't help but feel a sense of excitement and nervousness. This was going to be her biggest show yet - one that would test her creativity, challenge her actors, and push her own boundaries as an artist. Little did she know, however, that this production would also put her at the center of a storm, forcing her to confront her own demons and question everything she thought she knew about herself and her craft.

The setting for Rory's production is the iconic Helen Hayes Theatre in New York City, which has hosted many legendary plays over the years.
