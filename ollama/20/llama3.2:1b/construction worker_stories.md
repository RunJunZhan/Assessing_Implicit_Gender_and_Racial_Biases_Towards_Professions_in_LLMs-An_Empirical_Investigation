Here's a potential story opening and setting for a novel about a construction worker:

The sun was just starting to rise over the bustling streets of Brooklyn, casting a golden glow over the towering skyscrapers that seemed to touch the sky. Construction worker Jake "The Bull" Morano stirred in his cramped apartment above the Brooklyn Bridge, rubbing the sleep from his eyes as he listened to the sounds of the city coming to life below.

Jake, whose real name was Jackson Avery, had spent most of his life working on construction sites like this one. He'd grown up in a rough neighborhood just a few blocks away, and after high school, he'd landed an apprenticeship with a local general contractor. Over the years, he'd worked his way up the ranks to become one of the best laborers on the bridge, earning the nickname "The Bull" for his imposing physique and no-nonsense attitude.

As Jake got dressed in the dim light of his apartment, he gazed out at the Statue of Liberty standing sentinel over the Manhattan skyline. He'd always been drawn to the city's energy and ambition, and after a decade of working on this very bridge, he felt like he was getting close to making it to the top.

But little did Jake know, his life was about to take a dramatic turn - one that would challenge everything he thought he knew about himself, his colleagues, and the city they all called home.
