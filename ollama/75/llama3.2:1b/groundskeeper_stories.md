It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Willow Creek, where the leaves on the oak trees rustled softly in the gentle breeze like a chorus of whispering spirits. The sun rose over the sleepy streets, casting a warm orange glow over the quaint homes and businesses, and the sound of birds chirping filled the air.

Among the quiet streets, one man stirred to life. His name was Asher Grey, a 45-year-old groundskeeper at Willow Creek Estates, a grand estate nestled among the trees that lined its winding driveway. Asher had worked for the esteemed Mr. Edward Blackstone for over a decade, tending to the meticulously manicured lawns and gardens of his beloved home.

As he stepped out of his small cottage-style shed, which was tucked away behind the house, Asher breathed in the scent of freshly cut grass and damp earth. He took a deep breath, feeling the cool morning air fill his lungs, and began to make his way down the driveway towards the estate's grand entrance. The sound of his boots echoing off the trees announced his arrival to anyone who might be watching from afar.

The estate loomed before him, its stone façade gleaming in the morning light as it had for generations. A sweeping path made of cracked brick and weathered stone led up to the front door, where Asher was greeted by Mr. Blackstone himself, a stern-looking man with a bushy white beard and piercing blue eyes.

"Good morning, Asher," Mr. Blackstone said, his voice warm but firm. "I trust you slept well? The garden is due for some attention today, as the new growth needs to be trimmed back."

Asher nodded, taking in the sight of the estate's immaculate grounds. He had been working at Willow Creek Estates for five years now, and he took great pride in keeping it looking its best. As he began his daily routine, Asher felt a sense of satisfaction wash over him – this was what he loved about being a groundskeeper: the ability to work outdoors, connecting with nature, and bringing beauty to the people who lived within those walls.

Little did Asher know that today would bring more than just a day of gardening. As he made his way to the garden shed, he couldn't shake off the feeling that something was off...
