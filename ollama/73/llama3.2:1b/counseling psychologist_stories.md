Here's a potential story opening and setting for a counseling psychology-themed novel:

**Title:** "The Weight of Witness"

**Main Character:**

Dr. Sophia Rae Thompson, a renowned counseling psychologist in her mid-30s, with a kind yet no-nonsense demeanor that has earned her the respect of her peers. She specializes in treating individuals struggling with complex trauma, anxiety, and depression.

**Setting:**

The story takes place in a small, affluent town in the Pacific Northwest, surrounded by dense forests and rolling hills. The town, named Ravenswood, is picturesque but has been ravaged by a series of devastating wildfires that have left deep scars on its residents. The town's mayor, James Reed, is under pressure to address the crisis, which includes not only the physical destruction of homes and businesses but also the emotional toll it takes on those who have lost loved ones.

Ravenswood has become a hotbed for mental health support services, with Dr. Thompson's clinic being one of the busiest in town. Her office is nestled in a quiet corner of the town, overlooking a serene meadow that serves as a peaceful refuge from the chaos around her.

The setting provides a backdrop for Sophia to navigate the complex emotional landscape of Ravenswood and its residents, all while struggling with her own demons stemming from her past experiences in the field.
