It was a crisp autumn morning in the small town of Raven's Peak, nestled deep within the mountains. The air was alive with the sweet scent of ripe apples and the distant clang of hammers echoing from the local quarry. In a small, weathered cottage on the outskirts of town, Emrys Blackwood stirred to life. He stretched his lean frame, yawned, and rubbed the sleep from his eyes, revealing a sharp jawline and piercing emerald green eyes that seemed to gleam with an inner light.

Emrys was a stone cutter by trade, known throughout Raven's Peak for his precision and skill in shaping the rough granite boulders that dotted the mountainside. His cottage, adorned with intricate carvings of mythical creatures and Celtic knots, was a testament to his dedication to his craft. A few days ago, Emrys had received an unusual order from the local lord: he would need to create a new gatehouse for the estate's fortified gardens. The project was complex, requiring intricate carvings and precision cuts that only Emrys could master.

As Emrys prepared to embark on the long journey to the quarry, his mind wandered to the tales of his ancestors, who had built this very town from scratch using their own hands. He wondered what stories they might have shared, and whether they too were stone cutters at heart. The old man's thoughts were interrupted by a knock at the door – it was his apprentice, Finnley – who looked as restless as Emrys himself, eager to begin the day's work.
