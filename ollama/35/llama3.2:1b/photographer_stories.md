It was a drizzly autumn evening in the small town of Ravenswood, nestled in the heart of the Appalachian Mountains. The streets were lined with old Victorian houses, their windows like empty eyes staring back at the passing raindrops. The air was crisp and cool, carrying the scent of ripe apples and damp earth.

Ava Moreno stood outside her family's antique shop, a faded sign creaking in the gentle breeze. "Maeve's Marvels" was emblazoned above the door in bold, curly letters. Ava's camera slung over her shoulder, a worn Leica lay nestled beside it. She adjusted the strap and slipped inside, where the soft glow of lamps and the murmur of hushed conversations enveloped her.

Inside, Ava's grandmother, Maeve, sat behind the counter, her silver hair styled in a loose bun, her eyes clouding with age. She smiled warmly as Ava approached, her voice like honey when she spoke about the past. "Ava, dear, what brings you to Ravenswood tonight?"

Ava hesitated for a moment before answering, "Just exploring the town, Grandma. I love finding new places to capture."
